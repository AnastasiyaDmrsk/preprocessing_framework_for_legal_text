The process contains the following actors: Member States, Applicants/Holders of driving licenses, Medical practitioners, Commission, Health sector.

The process starts with an application for a driving license or renewal.

The process for the Member States starts.
Member States shall ensure that applicants undergo a medical examination in which the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness covering all the medical conditions set out in Annex III are applied before a driving licence is issued for the first time.
That applies to the issuance of driving licences in category AM only if so required by the Member State.
A medical examination shall be required in respect of applications for driving licences of categories C, CE, C1, C1E, D, D1, DE or D1E, regardless of whether a medical examination was undertaken for another category.
Before a driving licence is renewed, applicants for the renewal shall undergo a medical examination covering the medical conditions set out in Annex III.
This shall apply to the renewal of driving licences in category AM only if so required by the Member State.
Member States may, for categories AM, A, A1, A2, B, B1 and BE, apply one or both of the following alternative measures instead of requiring a medical examination: require the applicant or holder of the driving licence to fill in a self-assessment form covering the medical conditions set out in Annex III when applying for the issuance or renewal of a driving licence; or establish a national system of assessment of fitness to drive to ensure that significant changes in physical or mental fitness are reacted to in order to comply with the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness set out in Annex III, after the driving licence has been issued to the applicant following a medical examination or self-assessment.
Member States may provide for appropriate measures to address non-compliance with the requirement to fill in a self-assessment form or for knowingly providing information in the self-assessment form that is incorrect or incomplete, or for failing to meet any requirement established in accordance with paragraph 3, point (b).
Member States may apply the alternative measure under paragraph 3, point (b), in such a way that it allows the monitoring of fitness to drive during periods of administrative validity.
If it becomes apparent that the applicant or holder of a driving licence is likely to have one or more of the medical conditions listed in Annex III, Member States shall ensure that the applicant or holder of the driving licence undergoes a medical examination prior to the Member States issuing or renewing the driving licence, on the basis of information acquired pursuant to the various alternative measures.
This Article shall not prevent Member States from taking measures to raise awareness and improve the knowledge of the health sector and of driving licence holders about the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness for driving set out in Annex III.
If Member States adopt guidelines for medical practitioners to help identify driving licence holders who no longer meet the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness to drive, they shall inform the Commission thereof.
If Member States develop public awareness campaigns to inform citizens about mental or physical health conditions that may impair fitness to drive, they shall inform the Commission thereof.
The standards set by Member States for the issuance or any subsequent renewal of driving licences may be stricter than those set out in Annex III.
The process for the Member States ends.

The process for the Applicants/Holders of driving licenses starts.
Applicants for driving licences shall undergo a medical examination in which the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness covering all the medical conditions set out in Annex III are applied before a driving licence is issued for the first time.
Applicants for the renewal shall undergo a medical examination covering the medical conditions set out in Annex III before a driving licence is renewed.
Applicants or holders of driving licenses may be required to fill in a self-assessment form covering the medical conditions set out in Annex III when applying for the issuance or renewal of a driving licence, or to be part of national system of assessment of fitness to drive.
Applicants or holders of driving licences may be subject to a medical examination prior to the Member States issuing or renewing the driving licence, if it becomes apparent that they are likely to have one or more of the medical conditions listed in Annex III.
The process for the Applicants/Holders of driving licenses ends.

The process for the Medical practitioners starts.
Medical practitioners may use guidelines to help identify driving licence holders who no longer meet the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness to drive.
The process for the Medical practitioners ends.

The process for the Commission starts.
The Commission shall make the guidelines available to the other Member States, if Member States adopt guidelines for medical practitioners to help identify driving licence holders who no longer meet the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness to drive and inform the Commission thereof.
The Commission shall make the information available to the other Member States, if Member States develop public awareness campaigns to inform citizens about mental or physical health conditions that may impair fitness to drive and inform the Commission thereof.
The process for the Commission ends.

The process for the Health sector starts.
Member States may take measures to raise awareness and improve the knowledge of the health sector about the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness for driving set out in Annex III.
The process for the Health sector ends.
