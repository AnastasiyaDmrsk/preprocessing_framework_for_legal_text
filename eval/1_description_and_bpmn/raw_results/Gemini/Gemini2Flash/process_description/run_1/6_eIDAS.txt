The process contains the following actors: Member States, Supervisory body, Commission, Qualified trust service providers, Non-qualified trust service providers, Other supervisory bodies, Conformity assessment body, Data protection authorities, Body responsible for the national trusted list, Trust service providers, Natural or legal person, ENISA.

The process starts with the need to supervise trust service providers and ensure the security of trust services.

The process for the Member States starts.
Member States shall designate a supervisory body established in their territory or, upon mutual agreement with another Member State, a supervisory body established in that other Member State.
Member States shall ensure that the designated body shall be responsible for supervisory tasks in the designating Member State.
Member States shall ensure that supervisory bodies are given the necessary powers and adequate resources for the exercise of their tasks.
Member States shall notify to the Commission the names and the addresses of their respective designated supervisory bodies.
Member States may authorise their respective supervisory bodies to carry out joint investigations in which staff from other Member States’ supervisory bodies is involved.
Member States shall agree upon and establish the arrangements and procedures for such joint actions in accordance with their national law.
The process for the Member States ends.

The process for the Supervisory body starts.
The supervisory body shall supervise qualified trust service providers established in the territory of the designating Member State to ensure, through ex ante and ex post supervisory activities, that those qualified trust service providers and the qualified trust services that they provide meet the requirements laid down in this Regulation.
The supervisory body shall take action if necessary, in relation to non-qualified trust service providers established in the territory of the designating Member State, through ex post supervisory activities, when informed that those non-qualified trust service providers or the trust services they provide allegedly do not meet the requirements laid down in this Regulation.
The tasks of the supervisory body shall include to cooperate with other supervisory bodies and provide them with assistance.
The tasks of the supervisory body shall include to analyse the conformity assessment reports referred to in Articles 20(1) and 21(1).
The supervisory body shall inform other supervisory bodies and the public about breaches of security or loss of integrity.
The supervisory body shall report to the Commission about its main activities.
The supervisory body may carry out audits or request a conformity assessment body to perform a conformity assessment of the qualified trust service providers.
The supervisory body shall cooperate with the data protection authorities, in particular, by informing them without undue delay, about the results of audits of qualified trust service providers, where personal data protection rules appear to have been breached.
The supervisory body shall grant qualified status to trust service providers and to the services they provide.
The supervisory body shall withdraw qualified status to trust service providers and to the services they provide in accordance with Articles 20 and 21.
The supervisory body shall inform the body responsible for the national trusted list referred to in Article 22(3) about its decisions to grant or to withdraw qualified status, unless that body is also the supervisory body.
The supervisory body shall verify the existence and correct application of provisions on termination plans in cases where the qualified trust service provider ceases its activities, including how information is kept accessible in accordance with point (h) of Article 24(2).
The supervisory body shall require that trust service providers remedy any failure to fulfil the requirements laid down in this Regulation.
Member States may require the supervisory body to establish, maintain and update a trust infrastructure in accordance with the conditions under national law.
By 31 March each year, each supervisory body shall submit to the Commission a report on its previous calendar year’s main activities together with a summary of breach notifications received from trust service providers.
A supervisory body shall, upon receipt of a justified request from another supervisory body, provide that body with assistance so that the activities of supervisory bodies can be carried out in a consistent manner.
A supervisory body to which a request for assistance is addressed may refuse that request on any of the following grounds: the supervisory body is not competent to provide the requested assistance; the requested assistance is not proportionate to supervisory activities of the supervisory body carried out in accordance with Article 17; providing the requested assistance would be incompatible with this Regulation.
The notified supervisory body shall inform the supervisory bodies in other Member States concerned and ENISA, where appropriate, in particular if a breach of security or loss of integrity concerns two or more Member States.
The notified supervisory body shall inform the public or require the trust service provider to do so, where it determines that disclosure of the breach of security or loss of integrity is in the public interest.
The supervisory body shall provide ENISA once a year with a summary of notifications of breach of security and loss of integrity received from trust service providers.
The process for the Supervisory body ends.

The process for the Commission starts.
Member States shall notify to the Commission the names and the addresses of their respective designated supervisory bodies.
The Commission shall make the annual report available to Member States.
The Commission may, by means of implementing acts, define the formats and procedures for the report.
The Commission may, by means of implementing acts, further specify the security measures referred to in paragraph 1 of Article 19.
The Commission may, by means of implementing acts, define the formats and procedures, including deadlines, applicable for the purpose of paragraph 2 of Article 19.
The process for the Commission ends.

The process for the Qualified trust service providers starts.
Qualified trust service providers shall take appropriate technical and organisational measures to manage the risks posed to the security of the trust services they provide.
Qualified trust service providers shall ensure that the level of security is commensurate to the degree of risk, having regard to the latest technological developments.
Qualified trust service providers shall take measures to prevent and minimise the impact of security incidents.
Qualified trust service providers shall inform stakeholders of the adverse effects of any such incidents.
Qualified trust service providers shall notify the supervisory body and, where applicable, other relevant bodies, of any breach of security or loss of integrity that has a significant impact on the trust service provided or on the personal data maintained therein, without undue delay but in any event within 24 hours after having become aware of it.
Where the breach of security or loss of integrity is likely to adversely affect a natural or legal person to whom the trusted service has been provided, the trust service provider shall also notify the natural or legal person of the breach of security or loss of integrity without undue delay.
The trust service provider shall inform the public or require to do so if the notified supervisory body determines that disclosure of the breach of security or loss of integrity is in the public interest.
The process for the Qualified trust service providers ends.

The process for the Non-qualified trust service providers starts.
Non-qualified trust service providers shall take appropriate technical and organisational measures to manage the risks posed to the security of the trust services they provide.
Non-qualified trust service providers shall ensure that the level of security is commensurate to the degree of risk, having regard to the latest technological developments.
Non-qualified trust service providers shall take measures to prevent and minimise the impact of security incidents.
Non-qualified trust service providers shall inform stakeholders of the adverse effects of any such incidents.
Non-qualified trust service providers shall notify the supervisory body and, where applicable, other relevant bodies, of any breach of security or loss of integrity that has a significant impact on the trust service provided or on the personal data maintained therein, without undue delay but in any event within 24 hours after having become aware of it.
Where the breach of security or loss of integrity is likely to adversely affect a natural or legal person to whom the trusted service has been provided, the trust service provider shall also notify the natural or legal person of the breach of security or loss of integrity without undue delay.
The trust service provider shall inform the public or require to do so if the notified supervisory body determines that disclosure of the breach of security or loss of integrity is in the public interest.
The process for the Non-qualified trust service providers ends.

The process for the Other supervisory bodies starts.
A supervisory body shall, upon receipt of a justified request from another supervisory body, provide that body with assistance so that the activities of supervisory bodies can be carried out in a consistent manner.
The process for the Other supervisory bodies ends.

The process for the Conformity assessment body starts.
The supervisory body may request a conformity assessment body to perform a conformity assessment of the qualified trust service providers.
The process for the Conformity assessment body ends.

The process for the Data protection authorities starts.
The supervisory body shall cooperate with the data protection authorities, in particular, by informing them without undue delay, about the results of audits of qualified trust service providers, where personal data protection rules appear to have been breached.
The process for the Data protection authorities ends.

The process for the Body responsible for the national trusted list starts.
The supervisory body shall inform the body responsible for the national trusted list about its decisions to grant or to withdraw qualified status, unless that body is also the supervisory body.
The process for the Body responsible for the national trusted list ends.

The process for the Natural or legal person starts.
Where the breach of security or loss of integrity is likely to adversely affect a natural or legal person to whom the trusted service has been provided, the trust service provider shall also notify the natural or legal person of the breach of security or loss of integrity without undue delay.
The process for the Natural or legal person ends.

The process for the ENISA starts.
Where appropriate, in particular if a breach of security or loss of integrity concerns two or more Member States, the notified supervisory body shall inform ENISA.
The supervisory body shall provide ENISA once a year with a summary of notifications of breach of security and loss of integrity received from trust service providers.
The process for the ENISA ends.
