The process contains the following actors: Transferring Member State, Commission, Member State responsible, Applicant, Representative, Health professional, Other person subject to an equivalent obligation of professional secrecy.

The process starts with the triggering event of the exchange of health data needed before a transfer is carried out.

The process for the Transferring Member State starts.
For the sole purpose of the provision of medical care or treatment, in particular concerning disabled persons, elderly people, pregnant women, minors and persons who have been subject to torture, rape or other serious forms of psychological, physical and sexual violence, the transferring Member State shall transmit information on any special needs of the person to be transferred to the Member State responsible, in so far as it is available to the competent authority in accordance with national law.
The Transferring Member State may transmit information on that person’s physical or mental health.
The Transferring Member State shall transfer that information in a common health certificate with the necessary documents attached.
The Transferring Member State shall transmit the information only after obtaining explicit consent from the applicant or their representative, except where transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests of the applicant or another person if the applicant is physically or legally incapable of consent.
The Transferring Member State shall exchange information only between health professionals or other persons subject to equivalent obligations of professional secrecy.
The Transferring Member State shall ensure that the information exchanged is only used for the purposes of providing medical care or treatment, and shall not be further processed.
The process for the Transferring Member State ends.

The process for the Commission starts.
The Commission shall draw up the common health certificate.
The Commission shall adopt uniform conditions and practical arrangements for exchanging the health information.
The process for the Commission ends.

The process for the Member State responsible starts.
The Member State responsible shall ensure that the special needs of the person to be transferred are adequately addressed, including any essential medical care that may be required.
The Member State responsible shall exchange information only between health professionals or other persons subject to equivalent obligations of professional secrecy.
The Member State responsible shall ensure that the information exchanged is only used for the purposes of providing medical care or treatment, and shall not be further processed.
The process for the Member State responsible ends.

The process for the Applicant starts.
The applicant shall provide explicit consent for the Transferring Member State to transmit their health information, except where transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests of the applicant or another person if the applicant is physically or legally incapable of consent.
The process for the Applicant ends.

The process for the Representative starts.
The representative shall provide explicit consent for the Transferring Member State to transmit their health information, except where transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests of the applicant or another person if the applicant is physically or legally incapable of consent.
The process for the Representative ends.

The process for the Health professional starts.
The health professional shall process personal health data and is subject, under national law or rules established by national competent bodies, to the obligation of professional secrecy.
The Health professional shall exchange information only between health professionals or other persons subject to equivalent obligations of professional secrecy.
The Health professional shall ensure that the information exchanged is only used for the purposes of providing medical care or treatment, and shall not be further processed.
The process for the Health professional ends.

The process for the Other person subject to an equivalent obligation of professional secrecy starts.
The Other person subject to an equivalent obligation of professional secrecy shall process personal health data.
The Other person subject to an equivalent obligation of professional secrecy shall exchange information only between health professionals or other persons subject to equivalent obligations of professional secrecy.
The Other person subject to an equivalent obligation of professional secrecy shall ensure that the information exchanged is only used for the purposes of providing medical care or treatment, and shall not be further processed.
The process for the Other person subject to an equivalent obligation of professional secrecy ends.
