The process contains the following actors: Member States, Applicants or holders, and the Commission.

The process starts with an application for the issuance or renewal of a driving licence, simultaneously triggering subprocesses for the Member States and the Applicants or holders.

The process for the Member States starts.
Member States shall assess whether a driving licence is issued for the first time.
If a driving licence is issued for the first time, Member States shall ensure that applicants undergo a medical examination in which the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness covering all the medical conditions set out in Annex III are applied.
Member States shall assess whether the driving licence is in category AM.
If the driving licence is in category AM, Member States shall require the medical examination for issuance only if so required by the Member State in question.
Member States shall assess whether the application is for driving licences of categories C, CE, C1, C1E, D, D1, DE or D1E.
If the application is for driving licences of categories C, CE, C1, C1E, D, D1, DE or D1E, Member States shall require a medical examination, regardless of whether a medical examination was undertaken for another category.
Member States shall assess whether the application is for categories AM, A, A1, A2, B, B1 and BE.
If the application is for categories AM, A, A1, A2, B, B1 and BE, Member States may apply alternative measures instead of requiring a medical examination.
If Member States apply alternative measures, Member States may send a requirement to fill in a self-assessment form covering the medical conditions set out in Annex III to the Applicants or holders.
If Member States apply alternative measures, Member States may establish a national system of assessment of fitness to drive to ensure that significant changes in physical or mental fitness are reacted to.
Member States may provide for appropriate measures to address non-compliance with the requirement to fill in a self-assessment form.
Member States may provide for appropriate measures to address knowingly providing information in the self-assessment form that is incorrect or incomplete.
Member States may provide for appropriate measures to address failing to meet any requirement established in the national system of assessment of fitness to drive.
Member States may apply the alternative measure of the national system of assessment in such a way that it allows the monitoring of fitness to drive during periods of administrative validity.
Member States shall assess whether it becomes apparent that the applicant or holder of a driving licence is likely to have one or more of the medical conditions listed in Annex III on the basis of information acquired pursuant to the alternative measures.
If it becomes apparent that the applicant or holder is likely to have a medical condition, Member States shall ensure that the applicant or holder of the driving licence undergoes a medical examination prior to issuing or renewing the driving licence.
Member States may take measures to raise awareness about the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness for driving set out in Annex III.
Member States may take measures to improve the knowledge of the health sector and of driving licence holders about the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness for driving set out in Annex III.
Member States shall assess whether they adopt guidelines for medical practitioners to help identify driving licence holders who no longer meet the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness to drive.
If Member States adopt guidelines for medical practitioners, Member States shall notify the adopted guidelines to the Commission.
Member States shall assess whether they develop public awareness campaigns to inform citizens about mental or physical health conditions that may impair fitness to drive.
If Member States develop public awareness campaigns, Member States shall notify the information regarding the campaigns to the Commission.
Member States may set stricter standards for the issuance or any subsequent renewal of driving licences than those set out in Annex III.
The process for the Member States ends with the application processed end event.

The process for the Applicants or holders starts.
Applicants or holders shall assess whether a driving licence is renewed.
If a driving licence is renewed, Applicants or holders shall undergo a medical examination covering the medical conditions set out in Annex III.
Applicants or holders shall assess whether the renewal is for driving licences in category AM.
If the renewal is for driving licences in category AM, Applicants or holders shall undergo the medical examination only if so required by the Member State in question.
Upon receiving a requirement to fill in a self-assessment form from the Member States, the Applicants or holders shall fill in a self-assessment form covering the medical conditions set out in Annex III.
The process for the Applicants or holders ends with the application requirements fulfilled end event.

The process for the Commission starts.
Upon receiving the adopted guidelines from the Member States, the Commission shall make the guidelines available to the other Member States.
Upon receiving the information regarding the campaigns from the Member States, the Commission shall make the information available to the other Member States.
The process for the Commission ends with the information distributed end event.
