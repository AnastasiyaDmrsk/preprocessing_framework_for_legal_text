The process contains the following actors: Member States, Obliged entities, and the FIU.

The process starts with the need to apply customer due diligence measures, simultaneously triggering subprocesses for the Member States and the Obliged entities.

The process for the Member States starts.
Member States shall require that verification of the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner take place before the establishment of a business relationship or the carrying out of the transaction.
Member States shall assess whether verification during the establishment of a business relationship is necessary so as not to interrupt the normal conduct of business and whether there is little risk of money laundering or terrorist financing.
If it is necessary so as not to interrupt the normal conduct of business and where there is little risk of money laundering or terrorist financing, Member States may allow verification of the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner to be completed during the establishment of a business relationship.
Member States shall assess whether there are adequate safeguards in place to ensure that transactions are not carried out by the customer or on its behalf until full compliance with the customer due diligence requirements laid down in points (a) and (b) of the first subparagraph of Article 13(1) is obtained.
If there are adequate safeguards in place, Member States may allow the opening of an account with a credit institution or financial institution, including accounts that permit transactions in transferable securities.
Member States shall carry out the following 5 activities in parallel:
- Member States shall require that an obliged entity does not carry out a transaction through a bank account where it is unable to comply with the customer due diligence requirements laid down in point (a), (b) or (c) of the first subparagraph of Article 13(1).
- Member States shall require that an obliged entity does not establish a business relationship where it is unable to comply with the customer due diligence requirements.
- Member States shall require that an obliged entity does not carry out the transaction where it is unable to comply with the customer due diligence requirements.
- Member States shall require that an obliged entity terminates the business relationship where it is unable to comply with the customer due diligence requirements.
- Member States shall require that an obliged entity considers sending a suspicious transaction report to the FIU in relation to the customer in accordance with Article 33 where it is unable to comply with the customer due diligence requirements.
All 5 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Member States shall assess whether notaries, other independent legal professionals, auditors, external accountants and tax advisors ascertain the legal position of their client, or perform the task of defending or representing that client in, or concerning, judicial proceedings, including providing advice on instituting or avoiding such proceedings.
If they ascertain the legal position or perform the task of defending or representing, Member States shall not apply the first subparagraph to notaries, other independent legal professionals, auditors, external accountants and tax advisors.
Member States shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
- Member States shall require that obliged entities apply the customer due diligence measures to all new customers.
- Member States shall require that obliged entities apply the customer due diligence measures at appropriate times to existing customers on a risk-sensitive basis, including at times when the relevant circumstances of a customer change.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
The process for the Member States ends with the requirements established end event.

The process for the Obliged entities starts.
Before the establishment of a business relationship or the carrying out of the transaction, Obliged entities shall verify the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner.
Obliged entities shall assess whether Member States allow verification of the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner to be completed during the establishment of a business relationship.
If Member States allow verification to be completed during the establishment of a business relationship, Obliged entities shall complete those procedures as soon as practicable after initial contact.
Obliged entities shall assess whether they are unable to comply with the customer due diligence requirements laid down in point (a), (b) or (c) of the first subparagraph of Article 13(1).
If Obliged entities are unable to comply with the customer due diligence requirements, Obliged entities shall carry out the following 5 activities in parallel:
- Obliged entities shall not carry out a transaction through a bank account, except where they are notaries, other independent legal professionals, auditors, external accountants and tax advisors ascertaining the legal position of their client, or performing the task of defending or representing that client in, or concerning, judicial proceedings, including providing advice on instituting or avoiding such proceedings.
- Obliged entities shall not establish a business relationship, except where they are notaries, other independent legal professionals, auditors, external accountants and tax advisors ascertaining the legal position of their client, or performing the task of defending or representing that client in, or concerning, judicial proceedings.
- Obliged entities shall not carry out the transaction, except where they are notaries, other independent legal professionals, auditors, external accountants and tax advisors ascertaining the legal position of their client, or performing the task of defending or representing that client in, or concerning, judicial proceedings.
- Obliged entities shall terminate the business relationship, except where they are notaries, other independent legal professionals, auditors, external accountants and tax advisors ascertaining the legal position of their client, or performing the task of defending or representing that client in, or concerning, judicial proceedings.
- Obliged entities shall consider sending a suspicious transaction report to the FIU in relation to the customer in accordance with Article 33, except where they are notaries, other independent legal professionals, auditors, external accountants and tax advisors ascertaining the legal position of their client, or performing the task of defending or representing that client in, or concerning, judicial proceedings.
All 5 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Obliged entities shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
- Obliged entities shall apply the customer due diligence measures to all new customers.
- At appropriate times, Obliged entities shall apply the customer due diligence measures to existing customers on a risk-sensitive basis, including at times when the relevant circumstances of a customer change.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
The process for the Obliged entities ends with the customer due diligence applied end event.

The process for the FIU starts.
Upon receiving a suspicious transaction report in relation to the customer in accordance with Article 33 from the Obliged entities, the process for the FIU ends with the report received end event.
