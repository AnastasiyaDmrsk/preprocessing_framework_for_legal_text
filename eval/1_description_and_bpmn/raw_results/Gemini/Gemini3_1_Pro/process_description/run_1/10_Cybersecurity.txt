The process contains the following actors: the Commission, the ECCG, and ENISA (lane: Management Board).

The process starts with the need for a European cybersecurity certification scheme, simultaneously triggering subprocesses for the Commission and the ECCG.

The process for the Commission starts.
The Commission shall assess whether a candidate scheme is based on the Union rolling work programme or is a duly justified case not included in the Union rolling work programme.
If the scheme is based on the Union rolling work programme, the Commission may send a request to prepare a candidate scheme or to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme to ENISA.
If the scheme is a duly justified case not included in the Union rolling work programme, the Commission may send a request to prepare a candidate scheme or to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme to ENISA.
The Commission shall assess whether a scheme requested by the Commission or the ECCG is not included in the Union rolling work programme.
If the scheme requested is not included in the Union rolling work programme, the Commission shall update the Union rolling work programme accordingly.
Upon receiving the candidate scheme from ENISA, the Commission may adopt implementing acts providing for a European cybersecurity certification scheme for ICT products, ICT services and ICT processes which meets the requirements set out in Articles 51, 52 and 54.
The Commission shall adopt those implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure referred to in Article 66(2).
At least every five years, the Commission shall assess whether it is necessary to revise the adopted European cybersecurity certification scheme.
If it is necessary, the Commission may send a request to start the process of developing a revised candidate scheme to ENISA.
The process for the Commission ends with the scheme adopted end event.

The process for the ECCG starts.
The ECCG shall assess whether a candidate scheme is a duly justified case not included in the Union rolling work programme.
If it is a duly justified case not included in the Union rolling work programme, the ECCG may send a request to prepare a candidate scheme or to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme to ENISA.
Upon receiving a request for assistance and expert advice from ENISA, the ECCG shall provide ENISA with assistance and expert advice in relation to the preparation of the candidate scheme.
The ECCG shall adopt an opinion on the candidate scheme.
The ECCG shall send the opinion on the candidate scheme to ENISA.
At least every five years, the ECCG shall assess whether it is necessary to revise the adopted European cybersecurity certification scheme.
If it is necessary, the ECCG may send a request to start the process of developing a revised candidate scheme to ENISA.
The process for the ECCG ends with the scheme reviewed end event.

The process for ENISA starts.
Upon receiving a request to prepare a candidate scheme or to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme from the Commission or the ECCG, ENISA shall assess whether the request is from the Commission or from the ECCG.
If the request is from the Commission, ENISA shall prepare a candidate scheme which meets the requirements set out in Articles 51, 52 and 54.
If the request is from the ECCG, ENISA may prepare a candidate scheme which meets the requirements set out in Articles 51, 52 and 54.
If ENISA refuses the request from the ECCG, the Management Board shall take any decision to refuse such a request.
If ENISA refuses the request from the ECCG, ENISA shall give reasons for its refusal.
If ENISA prepares a candidate scheme, ENISA shall carry out the following 3 activities in parallel:
- ENISA shall consult all relevant stakeholders by means of a formal, open, transparent and inclusive consultation process.
- ENISA shall establish an ad hoc working group in accordance with Article 20(4) for the purpose of providing ENISA with specific advice and expertise.
- ENISA shall closely cooperate with the ECCG.
All 3 activities shall be completed before the next step.
If ENISA prepares a candidate scheme, ENISA shall send a request for assistance and expert advice to the ECCG.
ENISA shall assess whether the ECCG sends an opinion on the candidate scheme.
If the ECCG sends an opinion, ENISA shall take utmost account of the opinion of the ECCG.
If ENISA prepares a candidate scheme, ENISA shall send the candidate scheme prepared in accordance with paragraphs 3, 4 and 5 to the Commission.
At least every five years, ENISA shall evaluate each adopted European cybersecurity certification scheme.
ENISA shall take into account the feedback received from interested parties during the evaluation.
Upon receiving a request to start the process of developing a revised candidate scheme from the Commission or the ECCG, ENISA shall start the process of developing a revised candidate scheme in accordance with Article 48 and this Article.
The process for ENISA ends with the scheme evaluated end event.
