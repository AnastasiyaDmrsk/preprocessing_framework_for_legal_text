The process contains the following actors: Manufacturers, the Commission, and the Competent authority.

The process starts with the placement of devices on the market or putting them into service, simultaneously triggering subprocesses for the Manufacturers, the Commission, and the Competent authority.

The process for the Manufacturers starts.
When placing their devices on the market or putting them into service, Manufacturers shall ensure that they have been designed and manufactured in accordance with the requirements of this Regulation.
Manufacturers shall carry out the following 3 activities in parallel:
- Manufacturers shall establish a system for risk management.
- Manufacturers shall document a system for risk management.
- Manufacturers shall implement and maintain a system for risk management.
All 3 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Manufacturers shall conduct a clinical evaluation in accordance with the requirements set out in Article 61 and Annex XIV, including a PMCF.
Manufacturers shall assess whether the devices are custom-made devices.
If the devices are not custom-made devices, Manufacturers shall draw up technical documentation for those devices.
If the devices are not custom-made devices, Manufacturers shall keep up to date technical documentation for those devices.
Manufacturers shall include the elements set out in Annexes II and III in the technical documentation.
If the devices are custom-made devices, Manufacturers shall carry out the following 3 activities in parallel:
- Manufacturers shall draw up documentation in accordance with Section 2 of Annex XIII.
- Manufacturers shall keep up to date documentation in accordance with Section 2 of Annex XIII.
- Manufacturers shall keep available for competent authorities documentation in accordance with Section 2 of Annex XIII.
All 3 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Manufacturers shall assess whether compliance with the applicable requirements has been demonstrated following the applicable conformity assessment procedure and whether the devices are other than custom-made or investigational devices.
If compliance is demonstrated and the devices are other than custom-made or investigational devices, Manufacturers shall draw up an EU declaration of conformity in accordance with Article 19.
If compliance is demonstrated and the devices are other than custom-made or investigational devices, Manufacturers shall affix the CE marking of conformity in accordance with Article 20.
Manufacturers shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
- Manufacturers shall comply with the obligations relating to the UDI system referred to in Article 27.
- Manufacturers shall comply with the registration obligations referred to in Articles 29 and 31.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Manufacturers shall keep the technical documentation, the EU declaration of conformity and, if applicable, a copy of any relevant certificate available for the competent authorities for a period of at least 10 years after the last device covered by the EU declaration of conformity has been placed on the market.
Manufacturers shall assess whether the device is an implantable device.
If the device is an implantable device, Manufacturers shall keep the technical documentation available for at least 15 years after the last device has been placed on the market.
Upon request by a competent authority, Manufacturers shall provide that technical documentation in its entirety or a summary thereof to the competent authority.
Manufacturers shall assess whether they have a registered place of business outside the Union.
If Manufacturers have a registered place of business outside the Union, Manufacturers shall ensure that the authorised representative has the necessary documentation permanently available.
Manufacturers shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
- Manufacturers shall ensure that procedures are in place to keep series production in conformity with the requirements of this Regulation.
- Manufacturers shall adequately take into account changes in device design or characteristics and changes in the harmonised standards or CS in a timely manner.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Manufacturers shall assess whether the devices are other than investigational devices.
If the devices are other than investigational devices, Manufacturers shall establish, document, implement, maintain, keep up to date and continually improve a quality management system.
Manufacturers shall cover all parts and elements of a manufacturer's organisation dealing with the quality of processes, procedures and devices in the quality management system.
Manufacturers shall govern the structure, responsibilities, procedures, processes and management resources required to implement the principles and actions necessary to achieve compliance in the quality management system.
Manufacturers shall address the aspects listed in points (a) to (m) of paragraph 9 in the quality management system.
Manufacturers shall implement and keep up to date the post-market surveillance system in accordance with Article 83.
Manufacturers shall ensure that the device is accompanied by the information set out in Section 23 of Annex I in an official Union language(s) determined by the Member State.
Manufacturers shall assess whether they consider or have reason to believe that a device which they have placed on the market or put into service is not in conformity with this Regulation.
If Manufacturers consider or have reason to believe the device is not in conformity, Manufacturers shall immediately take the necessary corrective action to bring that device into conformity, to withdraw it or to recall it.
If Manufacturers consider or have reason to believe the device is not in conformity, Manufacturers shall inform the distributors of the device in question and, where applicable, the authorised representative and importers accordingly.
Manufacturers shall assess whether the device presents a serious risk.
If the device presents a serious risk, Manufacturers shall immediately inform the competent authorities of the Member States in which they made the device available and, where applicable, the notified body that issued a certificate for the device in particular of the non-compliance and of any corrective action taken.
Manufacturers shall have a system for recording and reporting of incidents and field safety corrective actions as described in Articles 87 and 88.
Upon request by a competent authority, Manufacturers shall provide it with all the information and documentation necessary to demonstrate the conformity of the device.
Upon request by a competent authority, Manufacturers shall provide samples of the device free of charge or grant access to the device.
Upon request by a competent authority, Manufacturers shall cooperate with a competent authority on any corrective action taken.
Manufacturers shall assess whether they have their devices designed or manufactured by another legal or natural person.
If Manufacturers have their devices designed or manufactured by another legal or natural person, Manufacturers shall include the information on the identity of that person in the information to be submitted in accordance with Article 30(1).
Manufacturers shall have measures in place to provide sufficient financial coverage in respect of their potential liability under Directive 85/374/EEC.
The process for the Manufacturers ends with the obligations fulfilled end event.

The process for the Commission starts.
The Commission may adopt delegated acts in accordance with Article 115 amending the Annexes II and III.
The process for the Commission ends with the delegated acts adopted end event.

The process for the Competent authority starts.
The Competent authority may send a request for the technical documentation to the Manufacturers.
The Competent authority may send a request for all the information and documentation necessary to demonstrate the conformity of the device to the Manufacturers.
The Competent authority may require that the manufacturer provide samples of the device free of charge or grant access to the device.
The Competent authority may send a request to cooperate on any corrective action taken to the Manufacturers.
The Competent authority shall assess whether the manufacturer fails to cooperate or the information and documentation provided is incomplete or incorrect.
If the manufacturer fails to cooperate or the information provided is incomplete, the Competent authority may take all appropriate measures to prohibit or restrict the device's being made available on its national market, to withdraw the device from that market or to recall it.
The Competent authority shall assess whether it considers or has reason to believe that a device has caused damage.
If the Competent authority considers or has reason to believe that a device has caused damage, the Competent authority shall facilitate the provision of the information and documentation to the potentially injured patient or user and other third parties affected by the damage, except where disclosure of the information and documentation is ordinarily dealt with in the context of legal proceedings.
The process for the Competent authority ends with the supervision completed end event.
