The process contains the following actors: Member State, AI Office, SME, and Board.

The process starts with three parallel subprocesses: one for Member State, one for AI Office, and one for the Board.

The process for the Board starts with the Template Needed start event. The Board may request standardised templates from the AI Office if required. After the Board receives the standardised templates from the AI Office, the process for the Board ends with the Templates Received end event.

The SME may share its interests and needs. The shared interests and needs are transmitted to the Member State, to be taken into account when setting the fees for conformity assessment. The process for the SME ends.

The process for the Member State starts. The Member State shall carry out the following five activities in parallel:
- Member State shall facilitate the participation of SMEs in the development process.
- Member State shall organise specific awareness raising and training activities.
- The Member State shall assess whether to utilise existing dedicated channels or establish new ones. If existing dedicated channels are available, the Member State shall utilise existing dedicated channels. If no channels exist, the Member State shall, where appropriate, establish new channels for communication with SMEs, deployers, and other innovators. Following either path, the Member State shall provide advice and the Member State shall respond to queries about the implementation.
- Upon receiving the interests and needs from SME, Member State shall take into account the specific interests and needs when setting the fees for conformity assessment, reducing those fees proportionately. The Member State shall set the fees for conformity assessment.
- Member State shall provide SMEs with priority access to the AI regulatory sandboxes if SMEs fulfil the eligibility conditions and selection criteria.
After all these parallel activities are merged together, the process ends for Member State with end event.

The process for the AI Office starts. The AI Office shall carry out the following four activities in parallel:
-	Upon receiving the request from the Board, the AI Office shall provide standardised templates as specified by the Board in its request.
-	AI Office shall develop a single information platform. Then, AI Office shall maintain a single information platform.
-	AI Office shall organise appropriate communication campaigns.
-	AI Office shall evaluate the convergence of best practices. Then, AI Office shall promote the convergence of best practices.
All four parallel activities shall be completed before the process for the AI Office ends with the AI Office Activities Completed end event.
