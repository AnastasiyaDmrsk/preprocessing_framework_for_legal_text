The process contains the following actors: Commission, Transferring Member State (lane: Health Professional), Applicant / Representative, and Member State Responsible (with two lanes: Health Professional and Competent Authority).

The process starts with two parallel subprocesses: the Commission identifies the need for a standardized health data exchange framework, and the Transferring Member State determines that a health data exchange is required for a transfer.

The Commission shall carry out the following two activities in parallel: draw up the common health certificate and adopt implementing acts for the common health certificate in accordance with the examination procedure. All activities shall be completed before the framework is considered established.

The Transferring Member State's Health Professional shall identify special needs information prior to initiating any data transmission. The Health Professional shall assess whether information on the person's physical or mental health shall be included. If yes, the Health Professional shall include information on physical or mental health in the transmission package.
Following identification, the Transferring Member State shall request explicit consent from the Applicant / Representative. The Applicant / Representative shall provide a consent decision or incapacity information upon receiving the consent request, except where no decision is required. The consent decision is transmitted back to the Transferring Member State via a message flow.

Upon receiving the consent decision, the Transferring Member State shall proceed based on the consent outcome:
- If consent is explicitly given, the Health Professional shall transmit to the Member State Responsible the information on special needs, including the common health certificate with necessary documents attached, for the sole purpose of the provision of medical care or treatment, in so far as the information is available to the competent authority.
- If the applicant is physically or legally incapable of giving consent, the Health Professional shall assess whether the transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests of the applicant or of another person. If transmission is necessary for vital interests, the Health Professional shall transmit the health information to the Member State Responsible. If transmission is not necessary for vital interests, the Health Professional shall not transmit the data, and the process ends with the Health Data Not Transmitted end event.
- If consent is refused, the Health Professional shall not transmit the health data. The process ends with the Health Data Not Transmitted end event.
The Health Professional shall exchange information only with the Health Professional or equivalent person in the Member State Responsible. The Health Professional shall use the exchanged information only for the purposes of medical care or treatment and shall not further process the information.
The process for the Transferring Member State ends.

Upon receiving the transmitted health information, the Member State Responsible's Competent Authority shall ensure that the special needs are adequately addressed. The process for the Member State Responsible ends with Special Needs Addressed end event.
