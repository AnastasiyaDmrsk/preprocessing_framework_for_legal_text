The process contains the following actors: the Commission, ENISA (with two lanes: ENISA and Management Board), the ECCG, and the Interested Party.

The process starts with a new or updated cybersecurity certification scheme being required, triggering parallel workflows for both the Commission and the ECCG. The Interested Party provides feedback independently at any point in the scheme development.

The Commission shall assess whether it wants to update an existing scheme. If an update is required, the Commission shall request a new or revised candidate scheme from ENISA. The Commission shall receive the resulting candidate scheme and shall assess whether it is already included in the Union rolling work programme. If the scheme is not yet included, the Commission shall update the Union rolling work programme, except where the scheme is already listed. The Commission shall then assess whether to adopt implementing acts. If implementing acts are to be adopted, the Commission shall adopt implementing acts. Following adoption, the Commission shall assess whether to start the process of developing a revised scheme. If a revised scheme is needed, the Commission shall request a revised candidate scheme from ENISA and the cycle recommences, except where no revision is required. The process for the Commission ends with Process Completed end event.

ENISA starts the process by receiving request from either the Commission or the ECCG. If a request is received from the ECCG, ENISA shall assess whether to refuse it. If the request is refused, the Management Board shall take the decision to refuse and ENISA then shall provide reasons for the refusal, except where the request is accepted. If the request is accepted, or upon receiving a request from the Commission, ENISA shall prepare a candidate scheme. ENISA shall consult relevant stakeholders. Then, ENISA shall establish ad hoc working groups. Then, ENISA shall cooperate with the ECCG and receive guidance from it. After receiving the opinion of the ECCG on the candidate scheme, ENISA shall take utmost account of that opinion when finalising the scheme. After receiving the feedback from Interested Party on the finalised scheme, at least every five years, ENISA shall evaluate each adopted scheme and, upon receiving a request from Commission OR ECCG, ENISA shall start the process of developing a revised candidate scheme. The process for ENISA ends with Process Completed end event.

The ECCG shall assess whether it wants to request an update to an existing scheme. If an update is required, the ECCG shall request a new or revised candidate scheme. If the Commission has already required a new or revised candidate scheme, the ECCG shall cooperate with ENISA and shall provide assistance and expert advice during scheme preparation, except where the Commission has not issued such a request. The ECCG shall adopt an opinion on the candidate scheme and shall transmit it to ENISA. Upon receiving the finalised scheme, the ECCG shall assess whether to start the process of developing a revised scheme. If a revision is needed, the ECCG shall request a revised candidate scheme, except where no revision is required. The process for the ECCG ends.

The Interested Party shall provide feedback on the candidate scheme to ENISA. The process for the Interested Party ends with Feedback Provided end event.
