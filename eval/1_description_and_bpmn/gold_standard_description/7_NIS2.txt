The process contains the following actors: Member State (with two lanes: CSIRT / Competent Authority and Single Point of Contact), Commission, Cooperative Group, ENISA, Other Member State Concerned (lane: CSIRT / Competent Authority), and Essential and Important Entity.

The process starts with the Essential and Important Entity detecting a significant incident, and independently, the Commission adopting acts.

Upon detecting a significant incident, the Essential and Important Entity shall notify the CSIRT / Competent Authority without undue delay. If it is appropriate to notify service recipients, the Essential and Important Entity shall notify service recipients (and cyber threat recipients) and shall communicate any measures or remedies to take. Within 24 hours of detection, the Essential and Important Entity shall submit an early warning to CSIRT / Competent Authority and shall then report any information necessary to determine the cross-border impact of the incident.

Following the early warning, the Essential and Important Entity shall receive feedback from CSIRT / Competent Authority. If guidance is required, the Essential and Important Entity shall request guidance or operational advice from the CSIRT / Competent Authority and shall await the guidance received, except where no guidance is needed. If technical support is required, the Essential and Important Entity shall request additional technical support, except where no technical support is needed.

Then,  within 72 hours of detection, the Essential and Important Entity shall submit an incident notification. If public awareness is necessary and a request is received from the Member State, the Essential and Important Entity shall inform the public about the incident, except where no public awareness is required. Within one month after handling, the Essential and Important Entity shall assess whether the incident is ongoing. If the incident is ongoing, the Essential and Important Entity shall provide a progress report. The Essential and Important Entity shall, upon request by CSIRT/Competent Authority, submit an intermediate report on relevant status updates and shall submit a final report. The process for the Essential and Important Entity ends with Incident Reporting Closed end event.

The CSIRT / Competent Authority shall receive the early warning from the Essential and Important Entity. If the Competent Authority has been notified directly, Competent Authority shall forward the notification to the CSIRT, except where the CSIRT was notified directly. Upon receiving the information, the CSIRT / Competent Authority shall determine any cross-border impact of the incident. If the incident is cross-border or cross-sectoral, the CSIRT / Competent Authority shall provide relevant information to the Single Point of Contact, except where the incident has no cross-border dimension. The Member State shall provide a response including initial feedback to the Essential and Important Entity.

If the Competent Authority has been notified and guidance is requested, the Competent Authority shall cooperate with the CSIRT on guidance and shall provide guidance or operational advice to the Essential and Important Entity. Upon request, the CSIRT shall provide additional technical support. If the incident is suspected to be of criminal nature, the CSIRT / Competent Authority shall provide guidance on reporting to law enforcement authorities, except where no criminal nature is suspected.

If the incident concerns two or more Member States, the CSIRT / Competent Authority OR Single Point of Contact shall inform the Other Member State Concerned and ENISA and shall preserve the security, commercial interests, and confidentiality of the information shared, except where the incident is confined to a single Member State. If needed, the CSIRT / Competent Authority may consult the Other Member State Concerned. If public awareness is necessary following consultation, the Member State shall assess whether to inform the public directly OR to require the Essential and Important Entity to inform the public, except where no public awareness is necessary.

If forward notifications to the SPOC are required, the CSIRT / Competent Authority shall request the SPOC to forward notifications to the Other Member State Concerned. The Member State shall receive the intermediate report and the final report from the Essential and Important Entity and shall provide relevant information about the incidents to the competent authorities. Every three months, the Single Point of Contact shall submit a summary report. The process for the CSIRT / Competent Authority ends with Handling Complete end event and for the Single Point of Contact with Report Submitted end event.

The Other Member State Concerned may consult the entity upon receiving a consultation request from the CSIRT / Competent Authority. If public awareness is necessary, the Other Member State Concerned shall assess whether to inform the public directly OR to require the Essential and Important Entity to inform the public, except where no public awareness is required. The process for the Other Member State Concerned ends.

Upon receiving the summary report from the Single Point of Contact, ENISA shall adopt technical guidance for the summary report and shall inform the Cooperation Group and the CSIRTs network about its findings on notifications received every six months. The ENISA process ends.

The Cooperative Group shall exchange advice and cooperate upon receiving a cooperation request from the Commission.

For Commission, if adoption is needed, the Commission shall specify the type, format, and procedure for incident notifications and shall exchange advice and cooperate with the Cooperative Group. By 17 October 2024, the Commission shall assess whether adoption of implementing acts is needed. The Commission shall assess whether the specification concerns essential and important entities. If so, the Commission shall further specify the cases in which an incident is considered significant, except where no such specification is required. The Commission shall adopt implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure. The process for the Commission ends upon adoption of the implementing acts.

Finally, the process ends with the Essential and Important Entity closing the incident reporting cycle, the Member State completing all notification and coordination obligations, and the Commission adopting the necessary implementing acts for incident notification procedures.
