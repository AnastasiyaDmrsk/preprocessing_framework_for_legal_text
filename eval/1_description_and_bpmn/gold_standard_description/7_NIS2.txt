The process contains the following actors: Member State (with two lanes: CSIRT / Competent Authority and Single Point of Contact), Commission, Cooperative Group, ENISA, Other Member State Concerned (lane: CSIRT / Competent Authority), and Essential and Important Entity (with two lanes: Trust Service Provider and Essential and Important Entity).

The process starts with the Essential and Important Entity detecting a significant incident, and independently, the Commission adopting acts.

Upon detecting a significant incident, the Essential and Important Entity shall notify the CSIRT / Competent Authority without undue delay. The Essential and Important Entity shall report any information enabling the CSIRT or Competent Authority to determine any cross-border impact of the incident.
If it is appropriate to notify service recipients, the Essential and Important Entity shall notify service recipients of the significant incident and shall communicate any measures or remedies to take in response to a significant cyber threat; where appropriate, the Essential and Important Entity shall also inform those recipients of the significant cyber threat itself.
Within 24 hours of becoming aware of the significant incident, the Essential and Important Entity shall submit an early warning to the CSIRT / Competent Authority. The early warning shall, where applicable, indicate whether the incident is suspected of being caused by unlawful or malicious acts and whether it could have a cross-border impact.
The Essential and Important Entity shall receive a response including initial feedback from the CSIRT / Competent Authority. If guidance is required, the Essential and Important Entity shall request guidance or operational advice from the CSIRT / Competent Authority and shall await the guidance received. If technical support is required, the Essential and Important Entity shall request additional technical support from the CSIRT.
Within 72 hours of becoming aware of the significant incident, the Essential and Important Entity shall submit an incident notification to the CSIRT / Competent Authority.
Upon request by the CSIRT or Competent Authority, the Essential and Important Entity shall submit an intermediate report on relevant status updates. Not later than one month after the submission of the incident notification, the Essential and Important Entity shall submit a final report.
The Essential and Important Entity shall assess whether the incident is ongoing at the time of the final report submission. If the incident is ongoing, the Essential and Important Entity shall provide a progress report at that time and shall submit a new final report within one month of handling the incident, except where the incident has been resolved.
If public awareness is necessary and the Member State has requested it, the Essential and Important Entity shall inform the public about the incident.
The process for the Essential and Important Entity ends with the Incident Reporting Closed end event.

If significant incidents have an impact on the provision of trust services, the Trust Service Provider shall notify the CSIRT / Competent Authority without undue delay and in any event within 24 hours of becoming aware.
The Trust Service Provider shall submit an intermediate report on relevant status updates upon request by the CSIRT / Competent Authority. The Trust Service Provider shall submit a final report not later than one month after the submission of the incident notification. The Trust Service Provider shall provide a progress report if the incident is ongoing at the time of the final report submission.
The process for the Trust Service Provider lane ends with the Incident Reporting Closed end event.

The Member State's Competent Authority shall ensure that essential and important entities notify the CSIRT / Competent Authority of significant incidents without undue delay.
The Competent Authority shall ensure that entities report any information enabling determination of cross-border impact. The Competent Authority shall ensure that entities submit early warnings within 24 hours. The Competent Authority shall ensure that entities submit incident notifications within 72 hours. The Competent Authority shall ensure that entities submit intermediate reports upon request. The Competent Authority shall ensure that entities submit final reports within one month, and provide progress reports where the incident is ongoing.
The Competent Authority shall ensure that entities communicate measures or remedies to affected service recipients where applicable.
The CSIRT / Competent Authority shall receive the early warning from the Essential and Important Entity.
If the Competent Authority has been notified directly, Competent Authority shall forward the notification to the CSIRT, except where the CSIRT was notified directly.
Upon receiving the information, the CSIRT / Competent Authority shall determine any cross-border impact of the incident. If the incident is cross-border or cross-sectoral, the CSIRT / Competent Authority shall provide relevant information to the Single Point of Contact, except where the incident has no cross-border dimension.
CSIRT / Competent Authority shall provide a response including initial feedback to the notifying Essential and Important Entity.
If the Essential and Important Entity has notified Competent Authority first and has requested guidance, the Competent Authority shall cooperate with the CSIRT on guidance and the Competent Authority shall provide guidance or operational advice to the Essential and Important Entity.
Upon request, the CSIRT shall provide additional technical support. If the incident is suspected to be of criminal nature, the CSIRT / Competent Authority shall provide guidance on reporting to law enforcement authorities, except where no criminal nature is suspected.
If the incident concerns two or more Member States, the CSIRT / Competent Authority OR Single Point of Contact shall inform the Other Member State Concerned and ENISA and CSIRT / Competent Authority OR Single Point of Contact shall preserve the security, commercial interests, and confidentiality of the information shared, except where the incident is confined to a single Member State.
If needed, the CSIRT / Competent Authority may consult the Other Member State Concerned.
Where public awareness is necessary, the CSIRT / Competent Authority shall consult the entity concerned. After consulting the entity, the Member State shall assess whether to inform the public directly, or to require the Essential and Important Entity to inform the public.
If forward notifications to the Single Point of Contact are required, the CSIRT / Competent Authority shall request the Single Point of Contact to forward notifications to the Other Member State Concerned. The Member State shall receive the intermediate report and the final report from the Essential and Important Entity and shall provide relevant information about the incidents to the competent authorities. Every three months, the Single Point of Contact shall submit a summary report. The process for the CSIRT / Competent Authority ends with Handling Complete end event and for the Single Point of Contact with Report Submitted end event.
The Other Member State Concerned may consult the entity upon receiving a consultation request from the CSIRT / Competent Authority. If public awareness is necessary, the Other Member State Concerned shall assess whether to inform the public directly OR to require the Essential and Important Entity to inform the public, except where no public awareness is required. The process for the Other Member State Concerned ends.
Upon receiving the summary report from the Single Point of Contact, ENISA shall adopt technical guidance for the summary report and shall inform the Cooperation Group and the CSIRTs network about its findings on notifications received every six months. The ENISA process ends.

The Cooperative Group shall exchange advice and cooperate upon receiving a cooperation request from the Commission.

For Commission, if adoption is needed, the Commission shall specify the type, format, and procedure for incident notifications and shall exchange advice and cooperate with the Cooperative Group.
By 17 October 2024, the Commission shall assess whether adoption of implementing acts is needed.
The Commission shall assess whether the specification concerns essential and important entities.
If so, the Commission shall further specify the cases in which an incident is considered significant, except where no such specification is required. The Commission shall adopt implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure.
The process for the Commission ends upon adoption of the implementing acts.

Finally, the process ends with the Essential and Important Entity closing the incident reporting cycle, the Member State completing all notification and coordination obligations, and the Commission adopting the necessary implementing acts for incident notification procedures.
