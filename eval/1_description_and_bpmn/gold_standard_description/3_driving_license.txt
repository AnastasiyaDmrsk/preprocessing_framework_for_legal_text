The process contains the following actors: Applicant, Member State, and Commission.

The process starts with the Applicant initiating a request for a new or renewed driving license, which triggers the Member State's compliance check workflow.

The Member State shall initiate a driving license compliance check upon receiving the application. If the license category is C, CE, C1, C1E, D, D1, DE, or D1E, the Member State shall require a medical examination, except where the category does not fall under these classifications. If the license category is AM and an examination is deemed necessary, the Member State shall require a medical examination, except when no examination is warranted. For all other license categories, the Member State shall determine whether a national system of assessment of fitness to drive is required.

If a national system is required, the Member State may establish a national system of assessment of fitness to drive, and then the Member State may apply that system to monitor compliance, except where such a system already exists. If the applicant is found to be non-compliant, the Member State may provide for appropriate measures to address non-compliance, except where compliance has been confirmed. If no national system is required, the Member State may require the applicant to fill in a self-assessment form covering the relevant medical conditions and may await the completed assessment. If the self-assessment reveals non-compliance, the Member State may provide for appropriate measures to address non-compliance, except where the assessment confirms fitness to drive.

The Applicant shall undergo a medical examination upon receiving the request from the Member State if the license category is C, CE, C1, C1E, D, D1, DE, or D1E. The Applicant shall fill in a self-assessment form covering the relevant medical conditions if so required by the Member State, except when no such form is requested. If a medical examination is still required after the self-assessment, the Applicant shall undergo a medical examination upon receiving the corresponding request.

Following the compliance determination, if the applicant is likely to have one or more of the medical conditions referred to in the relevant annexes, the Member State shall ensure that the applicant undergoes a medical examination, except where no such conditions are identified.

If the Member State decides to take measures to improve driver fitness, the following activities shall occur in parallel:
-	The Member State may take measures to raise awareness if awareness is required.
-	The Member State may improve the knowledge of the minimum standards of fitness to drive if improvement is required.
Both parallel activities shall be completed before the process continues to the guidelines phase.

The Member State may adopt guidelines for medical practitioners if required and shall inform the Commission of those adopted guidelines, except when no guidelines have been adopted. Upon receiving the notification, the Commission shall make the adopted guidelines available to all other Member States.

The Member State may develop public awareness campaigns if required and shall inform the Commission of such campaigns, except when no campaigns have been developed. Upon receiving the campaigns notification from Member State, the Commission shall make the relevant information available to all other Member States.

The Member State may set standards stricter than those set out in Annex III if it determines that higher requirements are warranted, except where the Annex III standards are deemed sufficient.

Finally, the process ends with the Member State completing all compliance obligations, the Applicant completing all required examinations (Examination over), and the Commission making the relevant guidelines and campaign information available to other Member States (Information available).
