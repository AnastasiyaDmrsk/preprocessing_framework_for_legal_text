The process begins with a start event.
First, a Supervisory Body is designated.
Following this, the Commission is notified of the designated Supervisory Body.
The process then involves supervising Qualified Trust Service Providers.
After supervision, there is a decision point.
If a Trust Service Provider is not qualified, action is taken on them.
Following this action, there is another decision point.
From this decision point, cooperation with other Supervisory Bodies takes place.
Next, conformity assessment reports are analyzed.
After analysis, other bodies are informed of security breaches.
Subsequently, a report is made to the Commission.
Following the report, audits are carried out.
After audits, cooperation with Data Protection Authorities occurs.
Then, the qualified status of providers is granted or withdrawn.
Following this, the National Trusted List Body is informed.
Next, termination plans are verified.
After verification, remediation of failures is required.
At this point, there is a decision point.
This decision point leads to another decision point.
This decision point then leads to another decision point.
Following these decision points, a request for assistance is received.
Upon receiving the request, there is a decision point regarding competence.
If the body is competent, assistance is provided.
After providing assistance, there is a decision point.
If the body is not competent, assistance is refused due to competence.
After refusing assistance, there is a decision point.
If the assistance is proportionate, assistance is provided.
After providing assistance, there is a decision point.
If the assistance is not proportionate, assistance is refused due to proportionality.
After refusing assistance, there is a decision point.
If the assistance is compatible, assistance is provided.
After providing assistance, there is a decision point.
If the assistance is incompatible, assistance is refused due to compatibility.
After refusing assistance, joint investigations are conducted.
Following joint investigations, a Supervisory Body is notified of a security breach.
At this point, there is a decision point regarding the impact of the breach.
If the breach has a significant impact, stakeholders are notified of the breach.
After notifying stakeholders, there is a decision point.
If the breach has no significant impact, no further action is taken on the breach.
After taking no further action, there is a decision point.
If the breach concerns multiple Member States, other Member States and ENISA are informed.
After informing other Member States and ENISA, there is a decision point.
If the breach concerns a single Member State, the public is informed or the provider is required to inform them.
After informing the public or the provider, there is a decision point.
Finally, ENISA is provided with a breach summary.
The process concludes with an end event.
