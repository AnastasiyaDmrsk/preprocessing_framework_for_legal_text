The process begins with a starting event.

Following the start, the first step is to "Verify Customer and Beneficial Owner Identity".

After verifying the identity, the process reaches a decision point. There are two possible paths:

1. If the verification is for "Before Business Relationship/Transaction", the process proceeds to "Establish Business Relationship/Carry out Transaction".
2. If the verification is for "During Business Relationship (Low Risk)", the process moves to "Complete Verification as soon as Practicable". From here, it then proceeds to "Establish Business Relationship/Carry out Transaction".

Regardless of which path was taken to reach "Establish Business Relationship/Carry out Transaction", the process then moves to another decision point.

From this second decision point, the process continues with "Apply Customer Due Diligence Measures to New and Existing Customers".

After applying the due diligence measures, the process encounters a third decision point. Here, there are two possible outcomes:

1. If "Customer Due Diligence Met", the process moves forward.
2. If "Customer Due Diligence Not Met", the process proceeds to "Do Not Carry Out Transaction/Terminate Relationship/Consider Suspicious Transaction Report".

Both paths from this third decision point converge at an end event.

The process concludes with an end event.
