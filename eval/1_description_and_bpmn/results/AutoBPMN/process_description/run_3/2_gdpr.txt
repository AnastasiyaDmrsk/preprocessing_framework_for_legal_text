The process begins when a processor becomes aware of a personal data breach. Following this, the processor notifies the controller about the breach.

Upon being notified, the controller also becomes aware of the personal data breach. At this point, a decision is made based on the risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals.

If the risk to rights and freedoms is unlikely, the process proceeds directly to documenting the breach.

However, if there is a potential risk, the controller assesses the risk to rights and freedoms. After the risk assessment, a decision is made regarding notification of the supervisory authority.

If the risk is deemed "not high risk" to data subjects, the process moves to documenting the breach.

If the risk assessment indicates a high risk, the controller proceeds to notify the supervisory authority within 72 hours. If the notification is made within the 72-hour timeframe, the process continues to provide breach details to the authority. If there is a delay in notification, the controller must provide reasons for the delay before proceeding to provide breach details to the authority.

After providing the initial breach details to the authority, a further decision is made. If information is provided in phases, the controller provides further information. If all information is provided at once, this step is skipped.

Following the communication with the supervisory authority, the process moves to documenting the breach.

Once the breach is documented, a decision is made on whether to communicate the breach to the data subject.

If communication to the data subject is not required, the process ends.

If communication is required, the controller assesses the method of communication. If appropriate technical or organizational measures are deemed suitable, the controller communicates the breach to the data subject using these measures. If it is deemed to involve disproportionate effort to communicate directly, a public communication or similar measure is taken.

After the communication to the data subject (or the decision not to communicate), the process concludes.
