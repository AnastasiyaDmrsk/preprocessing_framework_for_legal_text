The process begins with a start event. From there, it proceeds to a decision point. If a certain condition is met (indicated by "true"), the process moves to a task where the Commission requests ENISA to prepare or review a scheme based on the Union rolling work program.

Following this request, the process reaches another decision point. From this point, it can either proceed to a task where ENISA prepares a candidate scheme if it's included in the work program, or it can go to a different decision point.

This second decision point leads to two potential paths. One path involves the Commission requesting ENISA to prepare or review a scheme. The other path involves the ECCG requesting ENISA to prepare or review a scheme. Both of these paths then lead to a task where the Union rolling work program is updated, after which ENISA prepares a candidate scheme.

Regardless of whether the candidate scheme was prepared directly or after updating the work program, the process then moves to another decision point. From here, it can either go back to the initial decision point after the Commission's request, or it can proceed to a task where ENISA consults stakeholders.

If ENISA consults stakeholders, the process continues with ENISA establishing an ad hoc working group. Subsequently, ENISA cooperates with the ECCG, and the ECCG provides assistance and adopts an opinion. ENISA then considers the ECCG's opinion.

After considering the ECCG's opinion, ENISA transmits the candidate scheme to the Commission. The Commission then adopts implementing acts for the scheme. Finally, ENISA evaluates the adopted scheme every 5 years.

Following the evaluation, there is another decision point. If a certain condition is met (indicated by "true"), the Commission or ECCG requests ENISA to develop a revised scheme, which leads back to the task where the Commission requests ENISA to prepare or review a scheme based on the Union rolling work program. If this condition is not met, the process escalates.

The process concludes with an end event. There are also several loops and decision points that allow for the process to branch and potentially return to earlier stages, indicating a cyclical or iterative nature for certain activities.
