The process begins with a starting event. The first step is to determine if health data transfer is needed for the transfer.

Following this determination, there is a decision point. If health data transfer is needed, the process proceeds to obtain explicit consent or justify the necessity. After obtaining consent or justification, a common health certificate and relevant documents are prepared. These health information and documents are then transmitted. Subsequently, it is ensured that any special needs are addressed by the responsible Member State. The data is then processed exclusively by health professionals who are bound by a secrecy obligation. The information is used only for the specified purposes.

If, at the initial decision point, it is determined that health data transfer is not needed, the process bypasses the steps of obtaining consent, preparing documents, transmitting information, and ensuring special needs are addressed. Instead, it directly proceeds to the final decision point.

Similarly, if consent or justification is not obtained after the initial determination that health data transfer is needed, the process also bypasses the subsequent steps and proceeds to the final decision point.

From the final decision point, the process concludes with an end event.
