The process begins with a starting event.

The first step is to **Ensure design and manufacturing compliance**.
Following this, the process moves to **Establish and maintain risk management system**.
Next, the process involves **Conducting clinical evaluation including PMCF (Post-Market Clinical Follow-up)**.

After the clinical evaluation, there is a decision point.
If the device is a **"Custom-made device"**, the process proceeds to **Prepare and maintain documentation for custom-made devices**.
Then, the process continues with **Complying with UDI (Unique Device Identification) and registration obligations**.
This is followed by **Keeping technical documentation, EU declaration of conformity, and certificates available**.
The next step is to **Ensure series production conformity and update QMS (Quality Management System)**.
Subsequently, the process requires to **Implement and update post-market surveillance system**.
This is followed by **Ensuring the device is accompanied by required information**.

At this point, there is a series of checks and potential actions related to device conformity.
A decision is made regarding the device's conformity.
If the **"Device not in conformity"**, the process requires to **Take corrective action, inform distributors, and authorities if serious risk**. After this, there are further decision points that lead back to a common point.
If the **"Device in conformity"**, the process bypasses the corrective actions and moves forward.

Regardless of the conformity path taken, the process eventually leads to **Establishing a system for recording and reporting incidents and corrective actions**.
Following this, the process involves **Providing information and documentation to competent authorities upon request**.

Here, the process branches into interactions with competent authorities.
A decision is made based on the manufacturer's cooperation and the completeness of information provided.
If the **"Manufacturer fails to cooperate or provides incomplete/incorrect info"**, the **Competent authority takes measures to prohibit or restrict device availability**. This leads to further decision points that eventually merge.
If the **"Manufacturer cooperates or provides complete and correct info"**, the process moves to **Provide samples or access to device if requested**.
This is followed by **Cooperating with competent authority on corrective actions**.

From this point, there are further decision branches related to whether the competent authority considers the device caused damage.
If the **"Competent authority considers device caused damage"**, the process involves **Facilitating provision of information to injured party**. This leads to further decision points that eventually merge.
If **"No damage or legal proceedings handle disclosure"**, the process bypasses the facilitation of information to the injured party and moves forward.

The process then continues with identifying the **legal or natural person designing/manufacturing the device if applicable**.
This is followed by ensuring **measures for sufficient financial coverage for potential liability**.
After this, there are more decision points that lead back to the interaction with competent authorities.

There are parallel paths that also involve interactions with competent authorities, mirroring the steps described above, including scenarios where the manufacturer fails to cooperate or cooperates, and decisions about whether the device caused damage. These paths also lead to identifying the responsible person and ensuring financial coverage.

Returning to the earlier decision point after clinical evaluation:
If the device is **"Other devices"** (meaning not a custom-made device), the process proceeds to **Draw up and keep up-to-date technical documentation**.
Then, a decision is made regarding conformity demonstration.
If **"Conformity demonstrated"**, the process moves to **Draw up EU declaration of conformity and affix CE marking**.
This is followed by **Complying with UDI and registration obligations**.
Then, **Keeping technical documentation, EU declaration of conformity, and certificates available**.
Next, **Ensuring series production conformity and update QMS**.
Subsequently, **Implementing and update post-market surveillance system**.
This is followed by **Ensuring the device is accompanied by required information**.

Similar to the custom-made device path, there are checks for device conformity.
If the **"Device not in conformity"**, the process requires to **Take corrective action, inform distributors, and authorities if serious risk**. This leads to further decision points that eventually merge.
If the **"Device in conformity"**, the process bypasses the corrective actions and moves forward.

Regardless of the conformity path, the process then leads to **Establishing a system for recording and reporting incidents and corrective actions**.
Following this, the process involves **Providing information and documentation to competent authorities upon request**.

Again, the process branches into interactions with competent authorities, mirroring the steps described earlier for custom-made devices. This includes scenarios of manufacturer cooperation, device damage considerations, identifying the responsible person, and ensuring financial coverage.

Finally, if **"Conformity not demonstrated"** for "Other devices", the process moves to **Address non-conformity**.
After addressing non-conformity, or after the conformity is demonstrated and all subsequent steps are completed, the process converges.

The process concludes with an end event.
