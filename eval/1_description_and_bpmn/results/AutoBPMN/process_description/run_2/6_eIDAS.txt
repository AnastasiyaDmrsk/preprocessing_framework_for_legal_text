The process begins with a start event.
First, a Supervisory Body is designated.
Following this, the Commission is notified of the designated Supervisory Body.
The Supervisory Body then supervises Qualified Trust Service Providers.
After supervising, there is a decision point.
If a Trust Service Provider is not qualified, action is taken on them.
Following this action, there is another decision point.
From this decision point, the process moves to cooperating with other Supervisory Bodies.
After cooperating, conformity assessment reports are analyzed.
Next, other bodies are informed of security breaches.
Then, a report is made to the Commission.
Following the report, audits are carried out.
After conducting audits, cooperation with Data Protection Authorities takes place.
Subsequently, qualified status is granted or withdrawn.
The National Trusted List Body is then informed.
After informing the National Trusted List Body, termination plans are verified.
Following the verification of termination plans, remediation of failures is required.
After requiring remediation, there is a decision point.
This decision point leads to another decision point.
This decision point then leads to another decision point.
From this decision point, a request for assistance is received.
Upon receiving a request for assistance, there is a decision point regarding competence.
If the body is competent, assistance is provided.
After providing assistance, there is a decision point.
If the body is not competent, assistance is refused due to competence.
After refusing assistance due to competence, there is a decision point.
From this decision point, there is another decision point regarding proportionality.
If the assistance is proportionate, assistance is provided.
After providing assistance, there is a decision point.
If the assistance is not proportionate, assistance is refused due to proportionality.
After refusing assistance due to proportionality, there is a decision point.
From this decision point, there is another decision point regarding compatibility.
If the assistance is compatible, assistance is provided.
After providing assistance, there is a decision point.
If the assistance is incompatible, assistance is refused due to compatibility.
After refusing assistance due to compatibility, joint investigations are conducted.
Following joint investigations, a Supervisory Body is notified of a security breach.
After notifying the Supervisory Body of a security breach, there is a decision point regarding the impact of the breach.
If the breach has a significant impact, stakeholders are notified of the breach.
After notifying stakeholders, there is a decision point.
If the breach has no significant impact, no further action is taken on the breach.
After taking no further action, there is a decision point.
From this decision point, there is another decision point regarding the scope of the breach.
If the breach concerns multiple Member States, other Member States and ENISA are informed.
After informing other Member States and ENISA, there is a decision point.
If the breach concerns a single Member State, the public is informed or the provider is required to inform them.
After informing the public or the provider, ENISA is provided with a breach summary.
Finally, Commission Implementing Acts are defined, and the process ends with an end event.
