The process begins with a starting event. The first step is to determine if health data transfer is needed for the transfer.

Following this determination, there is a decision point. If health data transfer is needed, the process proceeds to obtain explicit consent or justify the necessity. After obtaining consent or justification, another decision point is reached. If consent or justification is obtained, the process moves to prepare a common health certificate and other necessary documents. Once these are prepared, the health information and documents are transmitted. Subsequently, it is ensured that any special needs are addressed by the responsible Member State. The health data is then processed by a health professional who is bound by a secrecy obligation. The exchanged information is used only for the specified purposes.

If, at the initial decision point, it is determined that health data transfer is not needed, the process bypasses the consent and justification steps and directly proceeds to a later stage. Similarly, if consent or justification is not obtained at the second decision point, the process also bypasses the subsequent steps related to data preparation and transmission.

Regardless of whether the health data transfer was initially deemed necessary and consent was obtained, or if it was deemed unnecessary, the process converges at a subsequent decision point. From this point, the process leads to an end event.
