The process begins with a starting event. Following this, the Commission requests ENISA to prepare or review a scheme based on the Union rolling work program.

At this point, a decision is made. If the scheme is included in the work program, ENISA prepares a candidate scheme. If not, another decision point is reached. This second decision point branches based on whether the request comes from the Commission or the ECCG. If it's a Commission request, the Commission asks ENISA to prepare or review the scheme. If it's an ECCG request, the ECCG asks ENISA to prepare or review the scheme.

Regardless of whether the scheme was initially included in the work program or requested later, the process moves to updating the Union rolling work program. After this update, ENISA prepares a candidate scheme.

Next, there's a decision point that leads to a loop. If a certain condition is met (indicated by "true"), ENISA consults stakeholders. Following stakeholder consultation, ENISA establishes an ad hoc working group. Then, ENISA cooperates with the ECCG. The ECCG provides assistance and adopts an opinion. ENISA then considers the ECCG's opinion. Finally, ENISA transmits the candidate scheme to the Commission.

The Commission adopts implementing acts for the scheme. After this, ENISA evaluates the adopted scheme every 5 years. This evaluation leads to another decision point. If a revision is needed, either the Commission or the ECCG requests ENISA to develop a revised scheme. ENISA then prepares a candidate scheme, which triggers stakeholder consultation again. There is also a path that leads to an escalation event, which then feeds back into the revision decision point.

If no revision is needed, the process continues from the evaluation step. The process concludes with an end event.
