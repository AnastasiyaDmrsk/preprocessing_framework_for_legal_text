The process begins when a processor becomes aware of a personal data breach. Following this, the processor notifies the controller. Upon being notified, the controller becomes aware of the personal data breach and then proceeds to assess the risk to data subjects.

After the risk assessment, a decision point is reached. If the risk to rights and freedoms is unlikely, the process moves to documenting the breach. If the risk assessment indicates a potential risk, the controller proceeds to notify the supervisory authority within 72 hours. Subsequently, the controller provides the breach details to the authority.

At this stage, there's a possibility for information to be provided in phases. If so, further information is provided. If all information is provided at once, or after further information is supplied, the process continues.

Following the notification to the supervisory authority, and if the risk to data subjects is deemed low, the process moves to documenting the breach.

After documenting the breach, another decision point is encountered based on the risk to data subjects. If there is a high risk to data subjects, the controller communicates the breach to the data subject. In parallel, or if the risk is not high, the process considers public communication or similar measures if the supervisory authority requires it.

Following the communication to the data subject, or if public communication is deemed necessary by the supervisory authority, a further decision is made. If the supervisory authority deems the conditions for communication met, the process continues.

If there is a high risk to data subjects, the controller communicates the breach to the data subject. This communication might also be triggered if the supervisory authority requires it, even if the initial risk was not high.

The process then considers whether public communication or a similar measure is necessary, potentially if the supervisory authority requires it.

Finally, after various communication steps and decisions, the process concludes with documenting the breach, which leads to the end of the process. If there is a high risk to data subjects, the breach is communicated to the data subject. If the supervisory authority requires communication, this step is also undertaken. The process then moves towards documenting the breach, which ultimately leads to the end of the process.
