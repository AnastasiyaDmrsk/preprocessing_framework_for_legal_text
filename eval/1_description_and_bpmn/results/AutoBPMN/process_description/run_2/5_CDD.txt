The process begins with a start event. Following this, the first step is to verify the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner. After this verification, the process reaches a decision point.

If the verification is deemed "Necessary & Low Risk," the process proceeds to complete the verification during the establishment of the relationship. Subsequently, the verification is completed as soon as practicable. This leads to applying customer due diligence measures to existing customers, then to new customers, and finally to existing customers on a risk-sensitive basis. After this, the process reaches another decision point.

If the initial verification is a "Standard Case," the process moves to establish the business relationship or carry out the transaction. At this point, there is another decision to be made. If it is "Unable to Comply," the process dictates that the transaction should not be carried out, the relationship should be terminated, and reporting should be considered. If it is possible to "Comply," the process then proceeds to apply customer due diligence measures to existing customers, followed by applying them to new customers, and then applying them to existing customers on a risk-sensitive basis.

Both paths, after applying customer due diligence measures to existing customers on a risk-sensitive basis, converge at a decision point. From this decision point, the process then proceeds to another decision point.

Finally, after these decision points, the process concludes with an end event.
