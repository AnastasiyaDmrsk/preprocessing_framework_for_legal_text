The process begins with a start event. The first step is to "Designate Supervisory Body". Following this, the "Commission of Supervisory Body" is notified.

Next, the process involves "Supervise Qualified Trust Service Providers". After this supervision, there is a decision point. If the trust service providers are not qualified, the process moves to "Take Action on Non-Qualified Trust Service Providers".

From this action, the process continues with a series of tasks: "Cooperate with Other Supervisory Bodies", "Analyze Conformity Assessment Reports", "Inform Other Bodies of Security Breaches", "Report to Commission", "Carry Out Audits", "Cooperate with Data Protection Authorities", "Grant/Withdraw Qualified Status", "Inform National Trusted List Body", "Verify Termination Plans", and "Require Remediation of Failures".

After requiring remediation, there is another decision point. Following this, the process proceeds to "Receive Request for Assistance".

Upon receiving a request for assistance, there is a decision based on competence. If the request is deemed "Competent", the process moves to "Provide Assistance". If the request is "Not Competent", the process moves to "Refuse Assistance". Both providing and refusing assistance lead to the next step, which is "Conduct Joint Investigations".

Following joint investigations, the process involves "Implement Security Measures". After implementing security measures, there is a notification step: "Notify Supervisory Body of Security Breach".

This notification leads to a decision point regarding the impact of the breach. If the impact is "Significant", the process continues with "Notify Stakeholders of Breach", followed by "Inform Other Member States and ENISA", then "Inform Public or Require Provider to Inform", and subsequently "Provide Summary of Notifications to ENISA". This is followed by "Commission Defines Formats and Procedures" and "Commission Makes Report Available".

If the impact is "No Significant Impact", the process moves to "Continue Operations".

Both the path for significant impact and the path for no significant impact converge before reaching the end event. The process concludes with an end event.
