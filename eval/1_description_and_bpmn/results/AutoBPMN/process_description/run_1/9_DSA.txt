The process begins with an individual or entity applying for Trusted Flagger status. Following the application, the Digital Services Coordinator (DSC) reviews the application.

At this point, a decision is made. If the application meets the required conditions, the Trusted Flagger status is awarded. Once awarded, the Trusted Flagger is responsible for publishing an annual report and then sending this report to the DSC. Subsequently, the Trusted Flagger makes this report publicly available. The DSC then communicates the Trusted Flagger's information to the Commission and the Board.

Following this communication, there's a check. If the condition is true, the Commission publishes a Trusted Flagger Database.

However, there's an alternative path if issues are reported by a provider. If a provider reports that notices are insufficiently precise, the DSC suspends the Trusted Flagger's status for an investigation. The DSC then investigates these notices. During this investigation, a decision is made: if the conditions for Trusted Flagger status are no longer met, the DSC revokes the status. The DSC then communicates this revocation to the Commission and the Board. After the revocation communication, the Commission publishes the Trusted Flagger Database.

If, after the investigation, the conditions are still met, the DSC reinstates the Trusted Flagger's status. The Trusted Flagger then continues their operations.

Returning to the point where the DSC communicates Trusted Flagger information to the Commission and the Board, if the initial check after this communication does not lead to the Commission publishing the database, the process continues through a series of checks.

Similarly, if a provider does not report any issues, the Trusted Flagger continues their operations. This path also leads to a series of checks.

There's also a path where if the application does not meet the conditions, the application is rejected. This rejection leads to the end of the process.

The process concludes with the Commission publishing the Trusted Flagger Database, or by the rejection of an application. There is also an escalation point indicated, which leads back to a decision point.
