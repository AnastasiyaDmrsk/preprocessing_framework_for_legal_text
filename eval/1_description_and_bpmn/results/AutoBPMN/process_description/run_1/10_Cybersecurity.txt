The process begins with a starting event. From there, it proceeds to a decision point. If a certain condition is met, the process continues to another decision point.

From this second decision point, there are two possible paths.

Path 1: The Commission may request ENISA to prepare or review a scheme based on the Union rolling work program. Following this, the process reaches another decision point. This decision point leads to a further decision point. From this third decision point, there's a path labeled "Based on Union rolling work programme" which leads to ENISA preparing a candidate scheme. After ENISA prepares the candidate scheme, the process moves to another decision point. This decision point then leads to another decision point.

Path 2: From the second decision point, there's another path that leads to a decision point. From this decision point, there's a path labeled "Duly justified cases" which leads to a task where the Commission or ECCG may request ENISA to prepare or review a scheme not included in the Union rolling work program. Following this, the Union rolling work program is updated. Then, ENISA prepares a candidate scheme. After ENISA prepares the candidate scheme, the process moves to a decision point. This decision point then leads to another decision point.

Both Path 1 and Path 2 eventually converge at a decision point. From this decision point, there's a path labeled "Based on Union rolling work programme" which leads to ENISA preparing a candidate scheme. After ENISA prepares the candidate scheme, the process moves to a decision point. This decision point then leads to another decision point.

From this decision point, there's a path labeled "Duly justified cases" which leads to a task where the Commission or ECCG may request ENISA to prepare or review a scheme not included in the Union rolling work program. Following this, the Union rolling work program is updated. Then, ENISA prepares a candidate scheme. After ENISA prepares the candidate scheme, the process moves to a decision point. This decision point then leads to another decision point.

Both of these paths then converge at a decision point. From this decision point, the process continues to ENISA consulting stakeholders. After ENISA consults stakeholders, ENISA establishes an ad hoc working group. Subsequently, ENISA cooperates with the ECCG. Following this, the ECCG provides assistance and adopts an opinion. Then, ENISA takes the utmost account of the ECCG's opinion. After that, ENISA transmits the candidate scheme to the Commission. The Commission then adopts implementing acts for the European cybersecurity certification scheme.

Finally, ENISA evaluates the adopted scheme every five years. This evaluation leads to a decision point. If a certain condition is met, there's a path labeled "true" which leads to a task where the Commission or ECCG requests ENISA to develop a revised scheme. This then loops back to the task where the Commission may request ENISA to prepare or review a scheme based on the Union rolling work program. This loop also leads to an escalation event which then proceeds to the decision point mentioned earlier.

Regardless of the path taken after the five-year evaluation, the process eventually reaches a decision point, then another decision point, and finally concludes with an end event.
