The process begins with a start event. The first activity is to "Ensure design and manufacturing compliance." Following this, the process involves establishing and maintaining a risk management system. Next, a clinical evaluation, including Post-Market Clinical Follow-up (PMCF), is conducted.

At this point, there is a decision point. If the device is custom-made, the process proceeds to "Prepare and maintain documentation for custom-made devices." Regardless of whether it was custom-made or not, the next step is to comply with Unique Device Identification (UDI) and registration obligations. This is followed by keeping technical documentation, the EU declaration of conformity, and certificates available.

The process then moves to ensuring series production conformity and updating the Quality Management System (QMS). Subsequently, an implementation and update of the post-market surveillance system takes place. The device must also be ensured to be accompanied by the required information.

Another decision point is reached. If the device is not in conformity, corrective action is taken, and distributors and authorities are informed if there is a serious risk. If the device is in conformity, the process continues.

Following the conformity checks, a system is established for recording and reporting incidents and corrective actions. This is followed by providing information and documentation to competent authorities upon request.

Here, the process branches into several potential paths involving interactions with competent authorities.

One path involves the competent authority requesting information. If the manufacturer fails to cooperate or provides incomplete/incorrect information, the competent authority takes measures to prohibit or restrict the device's availability. If the manufacturer cooperates or provides complete and correct information, they are asked to provide samples or access to the device if requested. They must also cooperate with the competent authority on corrective actions.

Another path, potentially branching from the cooperation path, considers if the competent authority believes the device caused damage. If so, the manufacturer facilitates the provision of information to injured parties. If no damage or legal proceedings are involved, this step is bypassed.

In parallel or subsequent to these interactions, the process may involve identifying the legal or natural person designing or manufacturing the device, if applicable. This is followed by ensuring measures for sufficient financial coverage for potential liability.

The process also includes a path for "Other devices" which involves drawing up and keeping up-to-date technical documentation. If conformity is demonstrated, an EU declaration of conformity is drawn up, and the CE marking is affixed. If conformity is not demonstrated, the non-conformity is addressed.

The process then converges and continues with steps that are also seen in other branches: complying with UDI and registration obligations, keeping technical documentation, EU declaration of conformity, and certificates available, ensuring series production conformity and updating the QMS, implementing and updating the post-market surveillance system, and ensuring the device is accompanied by required information.

Similar to earlier decision points, there are checks for device conformity. If the device is not in conformity, corrective actions are taken, and distributors and authorities are informed if there is a serious risk. If the device is in conformity, the process continues.

The process then re-enters the phase of establishing a system for recording and reporting incidents and corrective actions, followed by providing information and documentation to competent authorities upon request.

Again, there are complex interactions with competent authorities, mirroring the earlier described paths:
* If the manufacturer fails to cooperate or provides incomplete/incorrect information, the competent authority takes measures to prohibit or restrict the device's availability.
* If the manufacturer cooperates or provides complete and correct information, they provide samples or access to the device if requested and cooperate with the competent authority on corrective actions.
* In scenarios where the competent authority considers the device caused damage, the manufacturer facilitates the provision of information to injured parties. If no damage or legal proceedings are involved, this step is bypassed.

These interactions with competent authorities also lead to identifying the legal or natural person designing/manufacturing the device, if applicable, and ensuring measures for sufficient financial coverage for potential liability.

The process concludes with an end event.
