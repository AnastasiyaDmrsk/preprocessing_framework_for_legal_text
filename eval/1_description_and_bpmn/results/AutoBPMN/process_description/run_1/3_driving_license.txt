The process begins with a start event. The first step is to determine the license category.

Following this, there's a decision point.

If the license category is "AM Category", another decision is made.
If it's "AM Category - Required", a medical examination is conducted, followed by checking fitness standards. After checking fitness standards, there's a complex branching point with multiple paths that all seem to lead to a common end point.
If it's "AM Category - Not Required", the process moves to a different branching point.

From the "AM Category - Not Required" path, there's another decision based on whether it's a "Renewal" or "First Issuance".
If it's a "Renewal" and "AM Category - Required", a medical examination is conducted, followed by checking fitness standards. This then leads to another complex branching point with multiple paths that all seem to lead to a common end point.
If it's a "Renewal" and "AM Category - Not Required", the process moves to a different branching point.
If it's a "First Issuance" and "AM Category - Required", a medical examination is conducted, followed by checking fitness standards. This then leads to another complex branching point with multiple paths that all seem to lead to a common end point.
If it's a "First Issuance" and "AM Category - Not Required", the process moves to a different branching point.

The initial decision point also has another path for categories "C, CE, C1, C1E, D, D1, DE, D1E". For these categories, a medical examination is conducted, followed by checking fitness standards. This then leads to another complex branching point with multiple paths that all seem to lead to a common end point.

There's also a path for "Other Categories".
From "Other Categories", there's a decision based on whether it's a "Renewal" or "First Issuance".
If it's a "Renewal" and "AM Category - Required", a medical examination is conducted, followed by checking fitness standards. This then leads to another complex branching point with multiple paths that all seem to lead to a common end point.
If it's a "Renewal" and "AM Category - Not Required", the process moves to a different branching point.
If it's a "First Issuance" and "AM Category - Required", a medical examination is conducted, followed by checking fitness standards. This then leads to another complex branching point with multiple paths that all seem to lead to a common end point.
If it's a "First Issuance" and "AM Category - Not Required", the process moves to a different branching point.

After these various paths, there's a point where if the "Fitness Met", the license is issued or renewed.

Following the issuance or renewal, there's a decision based on "Alternative Measures Apply (AM, A, A1, A2, B, B1, BE)".
If "Alternative Measures Apply", there's a further decision for "Self-Assessment Form".
If "Self-Assessment Form" is to be filled, the "Fill Self-Assessment Form" task is performed.
After filling the form, there's a decision based on "Self-Assessment OK".
If "Self-Assessment OK" and "No Significant Change", the license is issued or renewed. This is followed by a series of decision points and escalations.
If "Self-Assessment OK" but not "No Significant Change", the process leads to a "Conduct Medical Examination" task, followed by checking fitness standards. This then leads to a complex branching point with multiple paths, some of which involve escalations.
If "Self-Assessment Incorrect/Incomplete", "Apply Non-Compliance Measures" is performed. This is followed by a series of decision points and escalations.

If "Alternative Measures Apply" is not the case, meaning "No Alternative Measures", the license is issued or renewed. This is followed by a series of decision points and escalations.

There's also a path for "National Assessment System".
If "National Assessment System" is established, there's a decision for "System No Issue".
If "System No Issue" and "No Significant Change", the license is issued or renewed. This is followed by a series of decision points and escalations.
If "System No Issue" but not "No Significant Change", the process leads to a "Conduct Medical Examination" task, followed by checking fitness standards. This then leads to a complex branching point with multiple paths, some of which involve escalations.

The process also includes multiple instances of conducting a medical examination and checking fitness standards, followed by complex branching points with escalations, across various license categories and renewal/issuance scenarios.

The process concludes with an end event.
