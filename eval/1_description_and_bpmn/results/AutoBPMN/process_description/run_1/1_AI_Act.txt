The process begins with a starting event. Following this, Member States undertake actions. After these actions, a decision point is reached. If SMEs are eligible for priority access, they are provided with priority access to AI Regulatory Sandboxes. If other SMEs are eligible, they are provided with access to the AI Regulatory Sandbox. Both of these paths then converge at another decision point.

From this point, awareness raising and training are organized. Subsequently, communication channels are utilized or established. This is followed by facilitating SME participation in standardization. Then, SME interests are considered in conformity assessment fees. After this, the AI Office takes actions.

At this stage, another decision point is reached. If the Board requests templates, standardized templates are provided. If general information is needed, a single information platform is developed and maintained. If obligations awareness is needed, communication campaigns are organized. If public procurement best practices are needed, the convergence of best practices is evaluated and promoted. All these paths then converge at an end event, concluding the process.
