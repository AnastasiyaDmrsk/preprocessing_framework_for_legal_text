The process contains the following actors: Member State, Supervisory Body, Commission, Another Supervisory Body, Conformity Assessment Body, Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider.

The process starts with the need to establish supervision for trust services.

The process for the Member State starts.
Member State shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:

* Member State shall designate a supervisory body.
* Member State shall give the necessary powers and adequate resources to a supervisory body.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Member State shall notify to the Commission the names and the addresses of the supervisory bodies.
Member State may require the supervisory body to establish, maintain and update a trust infrastructure if trust infrastructure is required.
Member State may authorise their supervisory bodies to carry out joint investigations if needed.
Member State shall assess whether Member States authorise their supervisory bodies to carry out joint investigations. If Member States authorise their supervisory bodies to carry out joint investigations, Member State shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
* Member State shall agree upon the arrangements and procedures for such joint actions.
* Member State shall establish the arrangements and procedures for such joint actions.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
The process for the Member State ends with the Member State supervision established end event.

The process for the Supervisory Body starts.
Supervisory Body shall supervise qualified trust service providers.
Supervisory Body shall assess whether when informed that those do not meet the requirements. If when informed that those do not meet the requirements, Supervisory Body shall take action in relation to non-qualified trust service providers.
Supervisory Body shall carry out the following 11 activities in parallel:

* Supervisory Body shall cooperate with other supervisory bodies.
* Supervisory Body shall provide other supervisory bodies with assistance.
* Supervisory Body shall analyse the conformity assessment reports.
* Supervisory Body shall inform other supervisory bodies and the public about breaches of security or loss of integrity.
* Supervisory Body shall carry out audits.
* Supervisory Body shall request a conformity assessment body to perform a conformity assessment.
* Supervisory Body shall cooperate with the data protection authorities.
* Supervisory Body shall grant qualified status to trust service providers.
* Supervisory Body shall withdraw qualified status from trust service providers.
* Supervisory Body shall inform the body responsible for the national trusted list about the status change, except where that body is also the supervisory body.
* Supervisory Body shall require that trust service providers remedy any failure to fulfil the requirements.
All 11 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Supervisory Body shall assess whether where personal data protection rules have been breached. If where personal data protection rules have been breached, Supervisory Body shall inform the data protection authorities about the results.
Supervisory Body shall assess whether in cases where the qualified trust service provider ceases its activities. If in cases where the qualified trust service provider ceases its activities, Supervisory Body shall verify the existence and correct application of provisions on termination plans.
Upon receiving a requirement from the Member State, Supervisory Body shall establish a trust infrastructure if required by Member State.
Supervisory Body shall maintain a trust infrastructure if required by Member State.
Supervisory Body shall update a trust infrastructure if required by Member State.
By 31 March each year, Supervisory Body shall submit to the Commission a report on its previous year's main activities and a summary of breach notifications.
Upon receipt of a justified request from another supervisory body, Supervisory Body shall assess whether the supervisory body is not competent to provide the assistance, the requested assistance is not proportionate to supervisory activities, or providing the requested assistance would be incompatible with this Regulation. If the supervisory body is not competent to provide the assistance, the requested assistance is not proportionate to supervisory activities, or providing the requested assistance would be incompatible with this Regulation, Supervisory Body shall refuse assistance. If the supervisory body is competent to provide the assistance, the requested assistance is proportionate to supervisory activities, and providing the requested assistance would be compatible with this Regulation, Supervisory Body shall provide assistance.
Supervisory Body shall carry out joint investigations if authorised.
Upon receiving a notification of a breach of security or loss of integrity, Supervisory Body shall assess whether if a breach of security or loss of integrity concerns two or more Member States. If if a breach of security or loss of integrity concerns two or more Member States, Supervisory Body shall inform the supervisory bodies in other Member States concerned and ENISA of the significant incident.
Supervisory Body shall assess whether if in the public interest. If if in the public interest, Supervisory Body shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
* Supervisory Body shall inform the public about the breach of security or loss of integrity.
* Supervisory Body shall require the trust service provider to inform the public about the breach.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Once a year, Supervisory body shall provide ENISA with a summary of notifications of breach.
The process for the Supervisory Body ends with the supervision completed end event.

The process for the Commission starts.
Upon receiving the annual report, Commission shall make the annual report available to Member States.
Commission may define the formats and procedures for the report if adjustments needed.
Commission shall adopt those implementing acts.
Commission shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:

* Commission may further specify the measures if specification is required.
* Commission may define the formats and procedures if specification is required.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
The process for the Commission ends with the Commission oversight completed end event.

The process for the Another Supervisory Body starts.
Another Supervisory Body shall request assistance.
Another Supervisory Body shall carry out joint investigations if authorised.
The process for the Another Supervisory Body ends with the assistance requested end event.

The process for the Conformity Assessment Body starts.
Upon receiving a request from the Supervisory Body, Conformity Assessment Body shall perform a conformity assessment of the qualified trust service providers upon request.
The process for the Conformity Assessment Body ends with the conformity assessment completed end event.

The process for the Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider starts.
Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall carry out the following 3 activities in parallel:

* Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall take appropriate technical and organisational measures to manage the risks.
* Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall take measures to prevent and minimise the impact of security incidents.
* Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall inform stakeholders of the adverse effects of any security incidents.
All 3 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Upon receiving a requirement from the supervisory body, Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall remedy failures to fulfil requirements if required by supervisory body.
Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall assess whether if the breach of security or loss of integrity has a significant impact on the trust service provided or on the personal data. If if the breach of security or loss of integrity has a significant impact on the trust service provided or on the personal data, Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
* Without undue delay but in any event within 24 hours after having become aware of it, Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall notify the supervisory body and other relevant bodies.
* Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall notify the natural or legal person.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall inform the public about the breach of security or loss of integrity if in the public interest, except where trust service provider should inform public only if required by supervisory body.
The process for the Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider ends with the trust service provision secured end event.
