The process contains the following actors: Manufacturer (with lanes: Manufacturer of custom-made devices, Manufacturer of implantable devices, Quality Management System), Competent Authority, and Patient / User or Successor or Health Insurance Company or Third Party.

The process starts with placing devices on the market or putting them into service.

The process for the Manufacturer starts.
When placing their devices on the market or putting them into service, Manufacturer shall ensure that devices have been designed and manufactured IAW the requirements.
Manufacturer shall carry out the following 4 activities in parallel:

* establish a system for risk management
* document a system for risk management
* implement a system for risk management
* maintain a system for risk management
All 4 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Manufacturer shall conduct a clinical evaluation.
Manufacturer shall assess whether the device is a custom-made device. If the device is not a custom-made device, Manufacturer shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
* draw up technical documentation, except where Manufacturer of custom-made devices
* keep up to date technical documentation, except where Manufacturer of custom-made devices
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
If the device is a custom-made device, Manufacturer of custom-made devices shall carry out the following 3 activities in parallel:
* draw up documentation
* keep up to date documentation
* keep available for competent authorities documentation
All 3 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Manufacturer shall assess whether compliance with the applicable requirements has been demonstrated. If compliance with the applicable requirements has been demonstrated, Manufacturer shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
* draw up an EU declaration of conformity, except where Manufacturer of custom-made devices or Manufacturer of investigational devices
* affix the CE marking of conformity, except where Manufacturer of custom-made devices or Manufacturer of investigational devices
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Manufacturer shall comply with the obligations.
Manufacturer shall assess whether the device is an implantable device. If the device is not an implantable device, Manufacturer shall keep the technical documentation, the EU declaration of conformity and a copy of any relevant certificate for a period of at least 10 years after the last device has been placed on the market, except where Manufacturer of implantable devices. If the device is an implantable device, Manufacturer of implantable devices shall keep the technical documentation, the EU declaration of conformity and a copy of any relevant certificate for a period of at least 15 years after the last device has been placed on the market.
Upon receiving a request for technical documentation from the Competent Authority, Manufacturer shall provide technical documentation in its entirety or a summary to the Competent Authority.
Manufacturer shall assess whether manufacturer has a registered place of business outside the Union and allow the authorised representative to fulfil the tasks mentioned in Article 11(3). If manufacturer has a registered place of business outside the Union and allow the authorised representative to fulfil the tasks mentioned in Article 11(3), Manufacturer shall ensure that the authorised representative has the necessary documentation available.
Manufacturer shall ensure that procedures are in place to keep series production in conformity with the requirements.
Manufacturer shall take into account changes in device design or characteristics and changes in the harmonised standards.
Manufacturer shall carry out the following 6 activities in parallel:
* establish a quality management system, except where Manufacturer of investigational devices
* document a quality management system, except where Manufacturer of investigational devices
* implement a quality management system, except where Manufacturer of investigational devices
* maintain a quality management system, except where Manufacturer of investigational devices
* keep up to date a quality management system, except where Manufacturer of investigational devices
* improve a quality management system, except where Manufacturer of investigational devices
All 6 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Quality Management System shall carry out the following 3 activities in parallel:
* cover all parts and elements of a manufacturer's organisation, except where Manufacturer of investigational devices
* govern the structure, responsibilities, procedures, processes and management resources, except where Manufacturer of investigational devices
* address at least the aspects set out in paragraph 9, except where Manufacturer of investigational devices
All 3 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Manufacturer shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
* implement the post-market surveillance system
* keep up to date the post-market surveillance system
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Manufacturer shall ensure that the device is accompanied by the information.
Manufacturer shall assess whether the device is not in conformity with this Regulation. If the device is not in conformity with this Regulation, Manufacturer shall carry out the following 4 activities in parallel:
* bring the device into conformity
* withdraw the device
* recall the device
* transmit information of the non-compliance to the distributors of the device, the authorised representative and importers
All 4 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Manufacturer shall assess whether the device is not in conformity with this Regulation and the device presents a serious risk. If the device is not in conformity with this Regulation and the device presents a serious risk, Manufacturer shall transmit information of the non-compliance to the competent authorities and the notified body.
Manufacturer shall have a system for recording and reporting of incidents and corrective actions.
Upon receiving a request for documentation necessary to demonstrate the conformity of the device from the Competent Authority, Manufacturer shall provide the competent authority with all the information and documentation.
Upon receiving a request to provide samples of the device free of charge from the Competent Authority, Manufacturer shall provide samples of the device to the Competent Authority.
Upon receiving a request to grant access to the device from the Competent Authority, Manufacturer shall grant access to the device to the Competent Authority.
Upon receiving a request for collaboration from the Competent Authority, Manufacturer shall cooperate with a competent authority.
Manufacturer shall assess whether manufacturers have their devices designed or manufactured by another legal or natural person. If manufacturers have their devices designed or manufactured by another legal or natural person, Manufacturer shall ensure that the information on the person identity is part of the submitted information.
Manufacturer shall have measures to provide sufficient financial coverage.
The process for the Manufacturer ends with compliance and post-market maintenance end event.

The process for the Competent Authority starts.
Competent Authority may send a request for technical documentation to the Manufacturer if required.
Competent Authority may send a request for documentation necessary to demonstrate the conformity of the device to the Manufacturer if required.
Competent Authority may send a request to provide samples of the device free of charge to the Manufacturer if required.
Competent Authority may send a request to grant access to the device to the Manufacturer if required.
Competent Authority may send a request for collaboration to the Manufacturer if required.
Competent Authority shall assess whether the manufacturer fails to cooperate or the information and documentation provided is incomplete or incorrect. If the manufacturer fails to cooperate or the information and documentation provided is incomplete or incorrect, Competent Authority may carry out the following 3 activities in parallel:

* take all appropriate measures to prohibit or restrict the device
* withdraw the device from that market
* recall the device until the manufacturer cooperates or provides information
All 3 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Upon receiving a request for provision of information related to device damage from the Patient / User or Successor or Health Insurance Company or Third Party, Competent Authority shall assess whether a competent authority considers or has reason to believe that a device has caused damage. If a competent authority considers or has reason to believe that a device has caused damage, Competent Authority shall facilitate the provision of the information and documentation to the potentially injured patient or user, except where there is an overriding public interest in disclosure or the competent authority need not comply with this obligation where disclosure of the information and documentation referred to in the first subparagraph is ordinarily dealt with in the context of legal proceedings.
The process for the Competent Authority ends with regulatory oversight completion end event.

The process for the Patient / User or Successor or Health Insurance Company or Third Party starts.
Patient / User or Successor or Health Insurance Company or Third Party may send a request for provision of information related to device damage to the Competent Authority if a competent authority considers or has reason to believe that a device has caused damage.
Patient / User or Successor or Health Insurance Company or Third Party may claim compensation for damage caused by a defective device if required.
The process for the Patient / User or Successor or Health Insurance Company or Third Party ends with compensation or information retrieval end event.
