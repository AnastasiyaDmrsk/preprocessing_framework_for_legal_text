The process contains the following actors: Processor, Controller, Supervisory Authority.

The process starts with a personal data breach.

The process for the Processor starts.
After becoming aware of a personal data breach, Processor shall notify the controller of a personal data breach.
The process for the Processor ends.

The process for the Controller starts.
Controller shall assess whether a personal data breach has occurred. If a personal data breach has occurred, not later than 72 hours after having become aware of it, Controller shall notify the personal data breach to the Supervisory Authority, except where the personal data breach is unlikely to result in a risk.
Controller shall assess whether the notification is not made within 72 hours after having become aware. If the notification is not made within 72 hours after having become aware, Controller shall accompany the notification with reasons for the delay.
Upon receiving a personal data breach from the Processor, Controller may provide the information if it is not possible to provide all the information at the same time.
Controller shall assess whether a personal data breach has occurred. If a personal data breach has occurred, Controller shall document any personal data breaches.
Controller shall assess whether the data breach is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons. If the data breach is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons, Controller shall communicate the personal data breach to the data subject, except where the controller has implemented appropriate technical and organisational protection measures that were applied to the personal data affected and render it unintelligible to unauthorised persons, such as encryption, or the controller has taken subsequent measures which ensure that the high risk to the rights and freedoms of data subjects is no longer likely to materialise, or the communication would involve disproportionate effort.
Controller shall assess whether communication to the data subject would involve disproportionate effort. If communication to the data subject would involve disproportionate effort, Controller shall carry out a public communication or similar measure, except where instead of t7.
The process for the Controller ends.

The process for the Supervisory Authority starts.
Upon receiving the personal data breach, Supervisory Authority shall verify compliance.
Supervisory Authority shall consider the likelihood of the data breach resulting in a high risk.
Supervisory Authority may require the controller to communicate the data breach to the data subject if the controller has not already communicated and if the personal data breach is likely a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons, except where conditions referred to in paragraph 3 are met.
Supervisory Authority may decide that any of the conditions are met if the controller has not already communicated and if the personal data breach is likely a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons.
The process for the Supervisory Authority ends.
