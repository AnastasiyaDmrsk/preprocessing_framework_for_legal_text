The process contains the following actors: Member States, the Board, and the AI Office.

The process starts with the need to implement measures for providers and deployers, simultaneously triggering subprocesses for the Member States, the Board, and the AI Office.

The process for the Member States starts.
Member States shall carry out the following 5 activities in parallel:
- Member States shall assess whether SMEs having a registered office or a branch in the Union fulfil the eligibility conditions and selection criteria. If SMEs fulfil the eligibility conditions and selection criteria, Member States shall provide SMEs with priority access to the AI regulatory sandboxes. Member States shall assess whether other SMEs fulfil the eligibility conditions and selection criteria. If other SMEs fulfil the eligibility conditions and selection criteria, Member States shall provide other SMEs with access to the AI regulatory sandbox.
- Member States shall organise specific awareness raising activities on the application of this Regulation tailored to the needs of SMEs, deployers and local public authorities. Member States shall organise training activities on the application of this Regulation tailored to the needs of SMEs, deployers and local public authorities.
- Member States shall utilise existing dedicated channels for communication with SMEs, deployers, other innovators and local public authorities. Member States shall assess whether it is appropriate to establish new channels for communication. If it is appropriate, Member States shall establish new channels for communication. Member States shall provide advice about the implementation of this Regulation. Member States shall respond to queries about the implementation of this Regulation.
- Member States shall facilitate the participation of SMEs in the standardisation development process. Member States shall facilitate the participation of other relevant stakeholders in the standardisation development process.
- Member States shall take into account the specific interests and needs of the SME providers when setting the fees for conformity assessment. Member States shall reduce those fees proportionately to their size, market size and other relevant indicators.
All 5 activities shall be completed before the next step.
The process for the Member States ends with the measures implemented end event.

The process for the Board starts.
The Board shall send a request specifying standardised templates for areas covered by this Regulation to the AI Office.
The process for the Board ends with the request sent end event.

The process for the AI Office starts.
The AI Office shall carry out the following 4 activities in parallel:
- Upon receiving a request specifying standardised templates for areas covered by this Regulation, the AI Office shall provide standardised templates.
- The AI Office shall develop a single information platform providing easy to use information in relation to this Regulation for all operators across the Union. The AI Office shall maintain a single information platform providing easy to use information in relation to this Regulation for all operators across the Union.
- The AI Office shall organise appropriate communication campaigns to raise awareness about the obligations arising from this Regulation.
- The AI Office shall evaluate the convergence of best practices in public procurement procedures in relation to AI systems. The AI Office shall promote the convergence of best practices in public procurement procedures in relation to AI systems.
All 4 activities shall be completed before the next step.
The process for the AI Office ends with the measures implemented end event.
