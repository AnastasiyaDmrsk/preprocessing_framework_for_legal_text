The process contains the following actors: Member State, Customer or Beneficial Owner, Obliged Entity.

The process starts with the need to establish a business relationship or carry out a transaction.

The process for the Member State starts. The Member State shall require verification of the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner before the establishment of a business relationship or the carrying out of the transaction. The Member State shall assess whether verification may be completed during the establishment of the business relationship rather than before it. If necessary so as not to interrupt the normal conduct of business and where there is little risk of money laundering or terrorist financing, the Member State may allow verification of the identity to be completed during the relationship establishment. If neither condition is met, verification shall be completed before the establishment of a business relationship or the carrying out of the transaction. The Member State shall assess whether to allow the opening of an account with a credit institution or financial institution before full verification has been completed. If adequate safeguards are in place to ensure that transactions are not carried out by the customer or on its behalf until full compliance with the customer due diligence requirements is obtained, the Member State may allow the opening of an account. If adequate safeguards are not in place, the Member State shall not allow the opening of an account before full verification. The Member State shall require that obliged entities apply the customer due diligence measures not only to all new customers but also at appropriate times to existing customers on a risk-sensitive basis, including at times when the relevant circumstances of a customer change. The process for the Member State ends.

The process for the Customer or Beneficial Owner starts. The Customer or Beneficial Owner shall verify the identity before the establishment of a business relationship or the carrying out of the transaction. The Customer or Beneficial Owner shall assess whether verification of the identity is allowed to be completed during the establishment of a business relationship. If verification during the establishment of the business relationship is allowed, the Customer or Beneficial Owner shall complete identity verification procedures as soon as practicable after initial contact. If verification during the establishment is not allowed, the Customer or Beneficial Owner shall complete verification before the establishment of a business relationship or the carrying out of the transaction. The process for the Customer or Beneficial Owner ends.

The process for the Obliged Entity starts. The Obliged Entity shall apply the customer due diligence measures not only to all new customers but also at appropriate times to existing customers on a risk-sensitive basis. The Obliged Entity shall assess whether it is able to comply with the customer due diligence requirements. If the Obliged Entity is able to comply with the customer due diligence requirements, the process for the Obliged Entity ends. If the Obliged Entity is unable to comply with the customer due diligence requirements, the Obliged Entity shall carry out the following 5 activities in parallel: the Obliged Entity shall not carry out a transaction through a bank account, except where the Obliged Entity is a notary, an independent legal professional, an auditor, an external accountant, or a tax advisor only to the strict extent that those persons ascertain the legal position of their client or perform the task of defending or representing that client in or concerning judicial proceedings including providing advice on instituting or avoiding such proceedings; the Obliged Entity shall not establish a business relationship, except where the Obliged Entity is a notary, an independent legal professional, an auditor, an external accountant, or a tax advisor only to the strict extent that those persons ascertain the legal position of their client or perform the task of defending or representing that client in or concerning judicial proceedings including providing advice on instituting or avoiding such proceedings; the Obliged Entity shall not carry out the transaction, except where the Obliged Entity is a notary, an independent legal professional, an auditor, an external accountant, or a tax advisor only to the strict extent that those persons ascertain the legal position of their client or perform the task of defending or representing that client in or concerning judicial proceedings including providing advice on instituting or avoiding such proceedings; the Obliged Entity shall terminate the business relationship, except where the Obliged Entity is a notary, an independent legal professional, an auditor, an external accountant, or a tax advisor only to the strict extent that those persons ascertain the legal position of their client or perform the task of defending or representing that client in or concerning judicial proceedings including providing advice on instituting or avoiding such proceedings; the Obliged Entity shall consider making a suspicious transaction report to the FIU, except where the Obliged Entity is a notary, an independent legal professional, an auditor, an external accountant, or a tax advisor only to the strict extent that those persons ascertain the legal position of their client or perform the task of defending or representing that client in or concerning judicial proceedings including providing advice on instituting or avoiding such proceedings. All 5 activities shall be completed before the process for the Obliged Entity ends. The process for the Obliged Entity ends.
