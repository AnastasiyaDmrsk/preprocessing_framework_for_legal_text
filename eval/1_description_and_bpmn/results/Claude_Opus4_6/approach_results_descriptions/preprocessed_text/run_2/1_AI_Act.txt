The process contains the following actors: Member State, SME, Board, AI Office.

The process starts with the obligation to implement measures for providers and deployers, in particular SMEs, including start-ups, under Article 62.

The process for the Member State starts. The Member State shall carry out the following 4 activities in parallel:

Activity 1: The Member State shall provide SMEs with priority access to the AI regulatory sandbox, if the SMEs have a registered office or a branch in the Union and if the SMEs fulfil the eligibility conditions and selection criteria. The Member State shall not preclude other SMEs from access to the AI regulatory sandbox, except where those other SMEs do not fulfil the eligibility conditions and selection criteria.

Activity 2: The Member State shall organise specific awareness raising and training activities on the application of this Regulation tailored to the needs of SMEs including start-ups, deployers and local public authorities.

Activity 3: The Member State shall utilise existing dedicated channels for communication with SMEs including start-ups, deployers, other innovators and local public authorities. The Member State shall assess whether new channels are appropriate. If new channels are appropriate, the Member State shall establish new channels. The Member State shall provide advice about the implementation of this Regulation. The Member State shall respond to queries about the implementation of this Regulation, including as regards participation in AI regulatory sandboxes.

Activity 4: The Member State shall facilitate the participation of SMEs and other relevant stakeholders in the standardisation development process.

All 4 activities shall be completed before the next step. Upon receiving the specific interests and needs from the SME, the Member State shall take into account the specific interests and needs of the SME providers, including start-ups. The Member State shall set the fees for conformity assessment, reducing those fees proportionately to the size, market size and other relevant indicators of the SMEs. The process for the Member State ends.

The process for the SME starts. The SME may share its specific interests and needs with the Member State if required. The process for the SME ends.

The process for the Board starts. The Board may request standardised templates from the AI Office if required. The process for the Board ends.

The process for the AI Office starts. The AI Office shall carry out the following 4 activities in parallel:

Activity 1: Upon receiving a request from the Board, the AI Office shall provide standardised templates for areas covered by this Regulation, as specified by the Board in its request.

Activity 2: The AI Office shall develop a single information platform providing easy information in relation to this Regulation for all operators across the Union. The AI Office shall maintain the single information platform.

Activity 3: The AI Office shall organise appropriate communication campaigns to raise awareness about the obligations arising from this Regulation.

Activity 4: The AI Office shall evaluate the convergence of best practices in public procurement procedures in relation to AI systems. The AI Office shall promote the convergence of best practices in public procurement procedures in relation to AI systems.

All 4 activities shall be completed before the process ends. The process for the AI Office ends.
