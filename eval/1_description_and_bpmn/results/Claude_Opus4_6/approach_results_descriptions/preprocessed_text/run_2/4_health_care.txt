The process contains the following actors: Applicant / Representative, Health Professional, Competent Authority, Commission.

The process starts with the need to exchange health data before a transfer is carried out for the sole purpose of the provision of medical care or treatment.

The process for the Health Professional starts. The Health Professional shall obtain explicit consent from the Applicant / Representative prior to transmitting the health information, except where the applicant is physically or legally incapable of giving his or her consent and such transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests of the applicant or of another person. The Health Professional shall transmit the consent request to the Applicant / Representative.

The process for the Applicant / Representative starts. Upon receiving the consent request from the Health Professional, the Applicant / Representative shall assess whether to grant consent. If the Applicant / Representative agrees, the Applicant / Representative may provide consent. If the Applicant / Representative disagrees, the Applicant / Representative may refuse consent. The Applicant / Representative shall transmit the consent decision to the Health Professional. The process for the Applicant / Representative ends.

The process for the Health Professional continues. Upon receiving the consent decision from the Applicant / Representative, the Health Professional shall assess whether explicit consent has been obtained. If explicit consent has been provided, the Health Professional shall transmit to the Member State responsible information on any special needs of the person to be transferred, for the sole purpose of the provision of medical care or treatment, in so far as the information is available to the competent authority. If explicit consent has not been provided, the Health Professional shall assess whether the applicant is physically or legally incapable of giving his or her consent and such transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests of the applicant or of another person. If the applicant is incapable and transmission is necessary to protect vital interests, the Health Professional shall transmit to the Member State responsible information on any special needs of the person to be transferred, in so far as the information is available to the competent authority. If the applicant is capable or transmission is not necessary to protect vital interests, the Health Professional shall not transmit the health information, except that the lack of consent shall not constitute an obstacle to the transfer itself. The Health Professional may include information on physical or mental health in specific cases. The Health Professional shall carry out the processing of personal health data. The Health Professional shall exchange information only between health professionals or other persons referred. The Health Professional shall use exchanged information only for the purposes set out in paragraph 1. The Health Professional shall not further process the information. The process for the Health Professional ends.

The process for the Competent Authority starts. Upon receiving the transmitted information on special needs from the Health Professional, the Competent Authority shall ensure that those special needs are adequately addressed, including any essential medical care that may be required. The process for the Competent Authority ends.

The process for the Commission starts. The Commission shall draw up the common health certificate. The Commission shall adopt implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure laid down. The Commission shall adopt uniform conditions and practical arrangements for exchanging the information referred to in paragraph 1. The Commission shall adopt those implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure laid down. The process for the Commission ends.
