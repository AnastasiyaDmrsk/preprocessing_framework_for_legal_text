The process contains the following actors: Online Platform Provider, Digital Services Coordinator, Trusted Flagger, Commission, Board.

The process starts with an entity submitting an application for trusted flagger status to the Digital Services Coordinator.

The process for the Online Platform Provider starts. The Online Platform Provider shall take the necessary technical and organisational measures to ensure that notices submitted by trusted flaggers are handled. The Online Platform Provider shall give priority to notices, where such notices are submitted within the trusted flagger's designated area of expertise and through the mechanisms referred to in Article 16. The Online Platform Provider shall process notices, where such notices are submitted within the trusted flagger's designated area of expertise and through the mechanisms referred to in Article 16. The Online Platform Provider shall decide upon notices, where such notices are submitted within the trusted flagger's designated area of expertise and through the mechanisms referred to in Article 16. The Online Platform Provider shall assess whether a trusted flagger has submitted a significant number of insufficiently precise, inaccurate or inadequately substantiated notices. If the trusted flagger has submitted such notices, the Online Platform Provider shall communicate that information to the Digital Services Coordinator, providing the necessary explanations and supporting documents. If the trusted flagger has not submitted such notices, no further action is required. The process for the Online Platform Provider ends.

The process for the Digital Services Coordinator starts. The Digital Services Coordinator shall verify the following 3 conditions in parallel: (a) the applicant has particular expertise and competence for the purposes of detecting, identifying and notifying illegal content, (b) the applicant is independent from any provider of online platforms, and (c) the applicant carries out its activities for the purposes of submitting notices diligently, accurately and objectively. All 3 conditions shall be verified before the Digital Services Coordinator proceeds. The Digital Services Coordinator shall award the status of trusted flagger to the applicant, where the applicant is established in the Member State of that Digital Services Coordinator and where the applicant meets all of the conditions. The Digital Services Coordinator shall communicate to the Commission and the Board the names, addresses and email addresses of entities to which the Digital Services Coordinator has awarded the status of trusted flagger, or whose trusted flagger status the Digital Services Coordinator has suspended or revoked. Upon receiving information from the Online Platform Provider indicating that a trusted flagger has submitted insufficiently precise, inaccurate or inadequately substantiated notices, the Digital Services Coordinator shall assess whether there are legitimate reasons to open an investigation. If the Digital Services Coordinator considers that there are legitimate reasons, the Digital Services Coordinator shall suspend the status of trusted flagger during the period of the investigation. If the Digital Services Coordinator considers that there are no legitimate reasons, no suspension is required. The Digital Services Coordinator shall carry out the investigation without undue delay following the suspension of the trusted flagger status. The Digital Services Coordinator shall assess whether the entity still meets the conditions set out in paragraph 2. If the entity no longer meets the conditions, the Digital Services Coordinator shall afford the entity an opportunity to react to the findings of the investigation and the intention to revoke the status, and shall transmit those findings to the Trusted Flagger. Upon receiving the reaction from the Trusted Flagger, the Digital Services Coordinator shall revoke the trusted flagger status. If the entity still meets the conditions, no revocation is required. The process for the Digital Services Coordinator ends.

The process for the Trusted Flagger starts. The Trusted Flagger shall, at least once a year, publish easily comprehensible and detailed reports on notices submitted during the relevant period, including an explanation of the procedures in place to ensure that the trusted flagger retains its independence. The Trusted Flagger shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel: the Trusted Flagger shall send those reports to the awarding Digital Services Coordinator, and the Trusted Flagger shall make those reports publicly available. All 2 activities shall be completed before the Trusted Flagger proceeds. Upon receiving the findings of the investigation and the intention to revoke from the Digital Services Coordinator, the Trusted Flagger shall react to the investigation findings. The process for the Trusted Flagger ends.

The process for the Commission starts. Upon receiving the names, addresses and email addresses from the Digital Services Coordinator, the Commission shall publish the information in a publicly available database in an easily accessible and machine-readable format. The Commission shall keep the database up to date. The Commission shall assess whether guidelines to assist providers of online platforms and Digital Services Coordinators in the application of paragraphs 2, 6 and 7 are necessary. If guidelines are necessary, the Commission shall transmit a consultation request to the Board. The Commission or the Board shall consult before issuing the guidelines. Upon receiving the consultation input from the Board, the Commission shall issue guidelines. If guidelines are not necessary, no further action is required. The process for the Commission ends.

The process for the Board starts. Upon receiving a consultation request from the Commission, the Board shall provide consultation input to the Commission. The process for the Board ends.
