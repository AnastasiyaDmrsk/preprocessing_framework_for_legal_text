The process contains the following actors: Member State, Applicant, Commission.

The process starts with an application for the issuance or renewal of a driving licence.

The process for the Member State starts. The Member State shall ensure that applicants undergo a medical examination before a driving licence is issued for the first time, except where the application concerns the issuance of driving licences in category AM only if so required by the Member State in accordance with Article 10(1), point (b). The Member State shall assess whether the application concerns categories C, CE, C1, C1E, D, D1, DE or D1E. If the application concerns categories C, CE, C1, C1E, D, D1, DE or D1E, the Member State shall require a medical examination, regardless of whether a medical examination was undertaken for another category, except where for category AM the medical examination may be required. If the application concerns categories AM, A, A1, A2, B, B1 and BE, the Member State shall assess whether to apply alternative measures instead of a medical examination. If the Member State does not apply alternative measures, the Member State shall ensure that the applicant undergoes a medical examination. If the Member State applies alternative measures, the Member State shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel: (1) The Member State may require the applicant to fill in a self-assessment form covering the medical conditions for driving licence categories AM, A, A1, A2, B, B1 and BE, notwithstanding paragraphs 1 and 2, to the extent not otherwise provided for by Annex III. (2) The Member State may establish a national system of assessment of fitness to drive for categories AM, A, A1, A2, B, B1 and BE, applicable after the licence has been issued following a prior medical examination or self-assessment. Both activities shall be completed before the Member State proceeds.

The Member State may provide for appropriate measures to address non-compliance if the applicant fails to comply with the requirement to fill in a self-assessment form, or if the applicant knowingly provides information in the self-assessment form that is incorrect or incomplete, or if the applicant fails to meet any requirement established in accordance with paragraph 3, point (b). The Member State may apply the national system to monitor fitness to drive during periods of administrative validity if the Member State has established a national system of assessment of fitness to drive.

The Member State shall assess whether the applicant or holder of a driving licence is likely to have one or more of the medical conditions listed in Annex III. If the applicant is likely to have the medical conditions, the Member State shall ensure that the applicant or holder of the driving licence undergoes a medical examination prior to issuing or renewing the driving licence. If the applicant is not likely to have the medical conditions, the Member State shall proceed with the issuance or renewal of the driving licence.

The Member State may take measures to raise awareness about the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness for driving if awareness is required. The Member State may take measures to improve the knowledge of the health sector if knowledge improvement is required. The Member State may adopt guidelines for medical practitioners if adopting of guidelines is required. If the Member State adopts guidelines for medical practitioners, the Member State shall inform the Commission of the guidelines adopted. The Member State may develop public awareness campaigns if awareness campaigns are required. If the Member State develops public awareness campaigns, the Member State shall inform the Commission of any public awareness campaigns developed. The Member State may set standards stricter than those set out in Annex III if stricter standards are required. The process for the Member State ends.

The process for the Applicant starts. The Applicant shall assess which type of fitness assessment is required. If a medical examination is required, the Applicant shall undergo a medical examination before a driving licence is issued for the first time or renewed, except where alternative measures are used instead. The Applicant shall undergo a medical examination for driving licences of categories C, CE, C1, C1E, D, D1, DE or D1E. The Applicant shall undergo a medical examination for category AM only if so required by the Member State. If the Member State requires a self-assessment form instead of a medical examination, the Applicant shall fill in a self-assessment form for driving licence categories AM, A, A1, A2, B, B1 and BE, to the extent not otherwise provided for by Annex III. The process for the Applicant ends.

The process for the Commission starts. Upon receiving information from a Member State that guidelines for medical practitioners have been adopted, the Commission shall make available to the other Member States the guidelines adopted. Upon receiving information from a Member State that public awareness campaigns have been developed, the Commission shall make available to the other Member States the information. The process for the Commission ends.
