The process contains the following actors: Member State, Supervisory Body, Another Supervisory Body, Conformity Assessment Body, Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider, Commission.

The process starts with the obligation for Member States to designate a supervisory body.

The process for the Member State starts. Member State shall designate a supervisory body. Member State shall give the necessary powers and adequate resources to a supervisory body. Member State shall notify to the Commission the names and the addresses of the supervisory bodies. Member State shall assess whether a trust infrastructure is required. If a trust infrastructure is required, the Member State may require the supervisory body to establish, maintain and update a trust infrastructure. If a trust infrastructure is not required, the Member State proceeds without further action on this matter. Member State shall assess whether their supervisory bodies should carry out joint investigations. If needed, Member State may authorise their supervisory bodies to carry out joint investigations. If Member States authorise their supervisory bodies to carry out joint investigations, Member State shall agree upon the arrangements and procedures for such joint actions. Member State shall establish the arrangements and procedures for such joint actions. If joint investigations are not needed, the Member State proceeds without further action on this matter. The process for the Member State ends.

The process for the Supervisory Body starts. Upon receiving the notification that the Member State has completed the designation and resource allocation, the Supervisory Body shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel: the Supervisory Body shall supervise qualified trust service providers; the Supervisory Body shall take action in relation to non-qualified trust service providers, when informed that those do not meet the requirements. Both activities shall be completed before the Supervisory Body proceeds to its ongoing tasks. The Supervisory Body shall carry out the following 13 activities in parallel: the Supervisory Body shall cooperate with other supervisory bodies; the Supervisory Body shall provide other supervisory bodies with assistance; the Supervisory Body shall analyse the conformity assessment reports; the Supervisory Body shall inform other supervisory bodies and the public about breaches of security or loss of integrity; the Supervisory Body shall carry out audits; the Supervisory Body shall request a conformity assessment body to perform a conformity assessment; the Supervisory Body shall cooperate with the data protection authorities; the Supervisory Body shall inform the data protection authorities about the results, where personal data protection rules have been breached; the Supervisory Body shall grant qualified status to trust service providers; the Supervisory Body shall withdraw qualified status from trust service providers; the Supervisory Body shall inform the body responsible for the national trusted list about the status change, except where that body is also the supervisory body; the Supervisory Body shall verify the existence and correct application of provisions on termination plans, in cases where the qualified trust service provider ceases its activities; the Supervisory Body shall require that trust service providers remedy any failure to fulfil the requirements. All 13 activities shall be completed as part of the ongoing supervisory mandate. Upon receiving the requirement from the Supervisory Body, the Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall remedy failures to fulfil requirements, if required by the supervisory body. If required by the Member State, the Supervisory Body shall establish a trust infrastructure. If required by the Member State, the Supervisory Body shall maintain a trust infrastructure. If required by the Member State, the Supervisory Body shall update a trust infrastructure. By 31 March each year, the Supervisory Body shall submit to the Commission a report on its previous year's main activities and a summary of breach notifications. The Supervisory Body shall assess whether to provide or refuse assistance upon receipt of a justified request from another supervisory body. If the request is justified and within competence, the Supervisory Body shall provide assistance. If the supervisory body is not competent to provide the assistance, or the requested assistance is not proportionate to supervisory activities, or providing the requested assistance would be incompatible with this Regulation, the Supervisory Body shall refuse assistance. If authorised by Member States, the Supervisory Body shall carry out joint investigations. Upon receiving a notification of a breach of security or loss of integrity from a trust service provider, the Supervisory Body shall assess whether the breach concerns two or more Member States. If a breach of security or loss of integrity concerns two or more Member States, the Supervisory Body shall inform the supervisory bodies in other Member States concerned and ENISA of the significant incident. If the breach does not concern two or more Member States, the Supervisory Body proceeds without cross-border notification. The Supervisory Body shall assess whether disclosure of the breach of security or loss of integrity is in the public interest. If in the public interest, the Supervisory Body shall inform the public about the breach of security or loss of integrity. Alternatively, if in the public interest, the Supervisory Body shall require the trust service provider to inform the public about the breach. If not in the public interest, no public disclosure is required. Once a year, the Supervisory Body shall provide ENISA with a summary of notifications of breach. The process for the Supervisory Body ends.

The process for Another Supervisory Body starts. Another Supervisory Body shall request assistance from the Supervisory Body. Upon receiving a response, Another Supervisory Body shall receive assistance or a refusal from the Supervisory Body. If authorised by Member States, Another Supervisory Body shall carry out joint investigations. The process for Another Supervisory Body ends.

The process for the Conformity Assessment Body starts. Upon request from the Supervisory Body, the Conformity Assessment Body shall perform a conformity assessment of the qualified trust service providers. The process for the Conformity Assessment Body ends.

The process for the Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider starts. The Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall take appropriate technical and organisational measures to manage the risks. The Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall take measures to prevent and minimise the impact of security incidents. The Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall inform stakeholders of the adverse effects of any security incidents. The Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall assess whether a breach of security or loss of integrity has a significant impact on the trust service provided or on the personal data. If the breach of security or loss of integrity has a significant impact, the Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall notify the supervisory body and other relevant bodies, without undue delay but in any event within 24 hours after having become aware of it. If the breach of security or loss of integrity has a significant impact, the Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall notify the natural or legal person. If in the public interest and if required by the supervisory body, the Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall inform the public about the breach of security or loss of integrity. If required by the Supervisory Body, the Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall remedy failures to fulfil requirements. The process for the Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider ends.

The process for the Commission starts. Upon receiving the notification of names and addresses from the Member State, the Commission acknowledges the designated supervisory bodies. Upon receiving the annual report from the Supervisory Body, the Commission shall make the annual report available to Member States. The Commission shall assess whether adjustments to the formats and procedures for the report are needed. If adjustments are needed, the Commission may define the formats and procedures for the report. The Commission shall adopt those implementing acts. If no adjustments are needed, the Commission proceeds without further action on this matter. The Commission shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel: the Commission may further specify the measures, if specification is required; the Commission may define the formats and procedures applicable for breach notifications, if specification is required. Both activities shall be completed before the Commission adopts the corresponding implementing acts. The process for the Commission ends.
