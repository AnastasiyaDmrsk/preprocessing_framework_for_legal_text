The process contains the following actors: Commission, ECCG, ENISA (with 2 lanes: ENISA, Management Board), Interested Party.

The process starts with a need to prepare or review a European cybersecurity certification scheme.

The process for the Commission starts. The Commission shall assess whether the request is based on the Union rolling work programme. If the request is based on the Union rolling work programme, the Commission shall assess whether to prepare a new scheme or review an existing scheme. If the Commission decides to prepare a new scheme, the Commission may request ENISA to prepare a candidate cybersecurity certification scheme on the basis of the Union rolling work programme [t1]. If the Commission decides to review an existing scheme, the Commission may request ENISA to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme on the basis of the Union rolling work programme [t2]. If the request is not based on the Union rolling work programme, the Commission shall assess whether to prepare a new scheme or review an existing scheme. If the Commission decides to prepare a new scheme, the Commission may, in duly justified cases, request ENISA to prepare a candidate cybersecurity certification scheme where the candidate scheme is not included in the Union rolling work programme [t3]. If the Commission decides to review an existing scheme, the Commission may, in duly justified cases, request ENISA to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme where the existing scheme is not included in the Union rolling work programme [t4]. The Commission shall update the Union rolling work programme following a request under paragraph 2 [t5]. Upon receiving the candidate scheme from ENISA, the Commission may adopt implementing acts providing for a European cybersecurity certification scheme, based on the candidate scheme prepared by ENISA, in accordance with the examination procedure [t17]. Upon receiving the evaluation results from ENISA, the Commission may request ENISA to start the process of developing a revised candidate scheme in accordance with Article 48 and Article 49 if necessary [t20]. The process for the Commission ends.

The process for the ECCG starts. The ECCG shall assess whether to prepare a new scheme or review an existing scheme. If the ECCG decides to prepare a new scheme, the ECCG may, in duly justified cases, request ENISA to prepare a candidate cybersecurity certification scheme where the candidate scheme is not included in the Union rolling work programme [t3]. If the ECCG decides to review an existing scheme, the ECCG may, in duly justified cases, request ENISA to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme where the existing scheme is not included in the Union rolling work programme [t4]. During the preparation of the candidate scheme, the ECCG shall provide ENISA with assistance and expert advice in relation to the preparation of the candidate scheme [t13]. The ECCG shall adopt an opinion on the candidate scheme [t14]. Upon receiving the evaluation results from ENISA, the ECCG may request ENISA to start the process of developing a revised candidate scheme in accordance with Article 48 and Article 49 if necessary [t20]. The process for the ECCG ends.

The process for ENISA starts. ENISA shall assess whether the request originates from the Commission or from the ECCG. If the request originates from the Commission, ENISA shall prepare a candidate cybersecurity certification scheme following the request from the Commission pursuant to Article 48 [t6]. If the request originates from the ECCG, ENISA shall assess whether to accept or refuse the request. If ENISA accepts the request, ENISA may prepare a candidate cybersecurity certification scheme following the request from the ECCG [t7]. If ENISA refuses the request from the ECCG pursuant to Article 48(2), ENISA shall give reasons for its refusal [t8]. The Management Board shall take the decision to refuse the request from the ECCG [t9]. The process for the Management Board lane ends with the refusal decision end event. ENISA shall carry out the following 3 activities in parallel when preparing a candidate scheme: ENISA shall consult all relevant stakeholders by means of a formal, open, transparent and inclusive consultation process [t10]; ENISA shall establish an ad hoc working group for the purpose of providing ENISA with specific advice and expertise for each candidate scheme being prepared [t11]; ENISA shall cooperate closely with the ECCG [t12]. All 3 activities shall be completed before ENISA proceeds to consider the ECCG opinion. ENISA shall take utmost account of the opinion of the ECCG before transmitting the candidate scheme to the Commission, except where the opinion of the ECCG shall not bind ENISA, and except where the absence of an ECCG opinion shall not prevent ENISA from transmitting the candidate scheme to the Commission [t15]. ENISA shall transmit the candidate scheme to the Commission [t16]. At least every five years, ENISA shall evaluate each adopted European cybersecurity certification scheme, taking into account the feedback received from interested parties [t18]. Upon receiving a request from the Commission or the ECCG, ENISA shall start developing a revised candidate scheme in accordance with Article 48 and Article 49 [t21]. The process for ENISA ends.

The process for the Interested Party starts. The Interested Party may provide feedback on adopted European cybersecurity certification schemes if required [t19]. The process for the Interested Party ends.
