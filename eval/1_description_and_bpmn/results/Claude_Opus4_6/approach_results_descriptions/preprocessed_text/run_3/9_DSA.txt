The process contains the following actors: Online Platform Provider, Digital Services Coordinator, Trusted Flagger, Commission, Board.

The process starts with an entity submitting an application for trusted flagger status to the Digital Services Coordinator of the Member State in which the applicant is established.

The process for the Digital Services Coordinator starts. The Digital Services Coordinator shall verify whether the applicant meets all of the following 3 conditions in parallel: (1) the applicant meets condition set out in paragraph 2(a), (2) the applicant meets condition set out in paragraph 2(b), (3) the applicant meets condition set out in paragraph 2(c). All 3 conditions shall be verified before the next step. The Digital Services Coordinator shall award the status of trusted flagger, upon application by any entity, provided the applicant is established and meets all of the conditions. The Digital Services Coordinator shall communicate to the Commission and the Board the names, addresses and email addresses of entities awarded trusted flagger status, following award of trusted flagger status in accordance with paragraph 2, or following suspension of trusted flagger status in accordance with paragraph 6, or following revocation of trusted flagger status in accordance with paragraph 7. Upon receiving information from a provider of online platforms indicating that a trusted flagger has submitted insufficiently precise, inaccurate or inadequately substantiated notices, the Digital Services Coordinator shall assess whether there are legitimate reasons to open an investigation. If the Digital Services Coordinator considers that there are legitimate reasons to open an investigation, the Digital Services Coordinator shall suspend the status of trusted flagger during the period of the investigation. If the Digital Services Coordinator considers that there are no legitimate reasons, the Digital Services Coordinator shall not suspend the status. The Digital Services Coordinator shall carry out the investigation without undue delay following suspension of trusted flagger status under paragraph 6. Before revoking the trusted flagger status of an entity, the Digital Services Coordinator shall afford the entity an opportunity to react to the investigation findings and the intention to revoke the entity's status as trusted flagger. Upon receiving the entity's reaction, the Digital Services Coordinator shall assess whether the entity still meets the conditions set out in paragraph 2. If the entity no longer meets the conditions, the Digital Services Coordinator shall revoke the trusted flagger status. If the entity still meets the conditions, the Digital Services Coordinator shall not revoke the status. The process for the Digital Services Coordinator ends.

The process for the Online Platform Provider starts. The Online Platform Provider shall take the necessary technical and organisational measures to ensure that notices submitted by trusted flaggers are handled appropriately. The Online Platform Provider shall give priority to notices, if the notices are submitted within the trusted flagger's designated area of expertise and through the mechanisms referred to in Article 16. The Online Platform Provider shall process notices, if the notices are submitted within the trusted flagger's designated area of expertise and through the mechanisms referred to in Article 16. The Online Platform Provider shall decide upon notices, if the notices are submitted within the trusted flagger's designated area of expertise and through the mechanisms referred to in Article 16. The Online Platform Provider shall assess whether a trusted flagger has submitted a significant number of insufficiently precise, inaccurate or inadequately substantiated notices. If the Online Platform Provider has such information, the Online Platform Provider shall communicate that information to the Digital Services Coordinator, providing the necessary explanations and supporting documents. If the Online Platform Provider has no such information, no communication is required. The process for the Online Platform Provider ends.

The process for the Trusted Flagger starts. The Trusted Flagger shall, at least once a year, publish easily comprehensible and detailed reports on notices submitted during the relevant period. The reports shall cover the following 3 categories in parallel: (1) the number of notices categorised by the identity of the provider of hosting services, (2) the number of notices categorised by the type of allegedly illegal content notified, (3) the number of notices categorised by the action taken by the provider. All 3 categories shall be included before the next step. The reports shall include an explanation of the procedures in place to ensure that the trusted flagger retains its independence. The Trusted Flagger shall send those reports to the awarding Digital Services Coordinator. The Trusted Flagger shall make those reports publicly available. Upon receiving the findings of an investigation and the intention to revoke from the Digital Services Coordinator, the Trusted Flagger shall react to the investigation findings. The process for the Trusted Flagger ends.

The process for the Commission starts. Upon receiving the names, addresses and email addresses from the Digital Services Coordinator, the Commission shall publish the information in a publicly available database in an easily accessible and machine-readable format. The Commission shall keep the database up to date. The Commission or the Board shall consult before issuing guidelines under paragraph 8. The Commission shall, where necessary, after consulting the Board, issue guidelines to assist providers of online platforms and Digital Services Coordinators in the application of paragraphs 2, 6 and 7. The process for the Commission ends.

The process for the Board starts. Upon receiving the names, addresses and email addresses from the Digital Services Coordinator, the Commission or the Board shall consult before the Commission issues guidelines under paragraph 8. The process for the Board ends.
