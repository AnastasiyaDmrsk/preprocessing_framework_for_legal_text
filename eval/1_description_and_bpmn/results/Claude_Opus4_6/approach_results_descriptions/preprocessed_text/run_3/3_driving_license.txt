The process contains the following actors: Member State, Applicant, Commission.

The process starts with an applicant applying for the issuance or renewal of a driving licence.

The process for the Member State starts. The Member State shall ensure that applicants undergo a medical examination before a driving licence is issued for the first time, except where the issuance concerns driving licences in category AM only if so required by the Member State in accordance with Article 10(1), point (b) [t1]. The Member State shall require a medical examination for driving licences of categories C, CE, C1, C1E, D, D1, DE or D1E, regardless of whether a medical examination was undertaken for another category [t3]. The Member State shall require a medical examination for category AM only if so required by the Member State [t3]. The Member State shall assess whether alternative measures are used instead of a medical examination. If alternative measures are used, the Member State shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel: the Member State may require the applicant or holder of the driving licence to fill in a self-assessment form covering the medical conditions, for driving licence categories AM, A, A1, A2, B, B1 and BE, notwithstanding paragraphs 1 and 2, to the extent not otherwise provided for by Annex III [t4]; the Member State may establish a national system of assessment of fitness to drive for driving licence categories AM, A, A1, A2, B, B1 and BE, applicable after the licence has been issued following a prior medical examination or self-assessment [t6]. Both activities shall be completed before the next step. If alternative measures are not used, the Member State shall ensure the applicant undergoes a medical examination as required under paragraphs 1 and 2.

The Member State may provide for appropriate measures to address non-compliance with the requirement to fill in a self-assessment form, or for knowingly providing information in the self-assessment form that is incorrect or incomplete, or for failing to meet any requirement established in accordance with paragraph 3, point (b) [t7]. The Member State may apply the national system to monitor fitness to drive during periods of administrative validity, if the Member State has established a national system of assessment of fitness to drive [t8].

The Member State shall assess whether, on the basis of information acquired pursuant to the various alternative measures, the applicant or holder of a driving licence is likely to have one or more of the medical conditions listed in Annex III. If the applicant is likely to have the medical conditions, the Member State shall ensure that the applicant or holder of the driving licence undergoes a medical examination prior to issuing or renewing the driving licence [t9]. If the applicant is not likely to have the medical conditions, the Member State shall proceed to the next step.

The Member State may take measures to raise awareness about the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness for driving, if awareness is required [t10]. The Member State may take measures to improve the knowledge of the health sector, if knowledge improvement is required [t11]. The Member State may adopt guidelines for medical practitioners, if adopting of guidelines is required [t12]. If the Member State adopts guidelines for medical practitioners, the Member State shall inform the Commission of guidelines adopted [t13]. The Member State may develop public awareness campaigns, if awareness campaigns are required [t15]. If the Member State develops public awareness campaigns, the Member State shall inform the Commission of any public awareness campaigns developed [t16]. The Member State may set standards stricter than those set out in Annex III, if stricter standards are required [t17]. The process for the Member State ends.

The process for the Applicant starts. The Applicant shall undergo a medical examination before a driving licence is issued for the first time or renewed [t2]. The Applicant shall undergo a medical examination for driving licences of categories C, CE, C1, C1E, D, D1, DE or D1E [t2]. The Applicant shall undergo a medical examination for category AM, except where the medical examination is required only if so required by the Member State [t2]. The Applicant shall assess whether alternative measures are used instead of a medical examination. If alternative measures are used, the Applicant shall fill in a self-assessment form for driving licence categories AM, A, A1, A2, B, B1 and BE, to the extent not otherwise provided for by Annex III [t5]. If alternative measures are not used, the Applicant shall undergo the medical examination. The process for the Applicant ends.

The process for the Commission starts. Upon receiving information from a Member State of the adoption of guidelines for medical practitioners, the Commission shall make available to the other Member States the guidelines adopted [t14]. Upon receiving information from a Member State of public awareness campaigns developed, the Commission shall make available to the other Member States the information [t17]. The process for the Commission ends.
