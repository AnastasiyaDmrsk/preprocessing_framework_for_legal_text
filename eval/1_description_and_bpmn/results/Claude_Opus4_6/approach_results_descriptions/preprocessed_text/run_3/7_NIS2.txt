The process contains the following actors: Competent Authority, Essential and Important Entity, Trust Service Provider, CSIRT or Competent Authority, CSIRT, CSIRT or Competent Authority or Single Point of Contact, Single Point of Contact, ENISA, Commission, Cooperation Group.

The process starts with the detection of an incident that has a significant impact on the provision of services by an essential or important entity.

The process for the Competent Authority starts. The Competent Authority shall ensure that entities notify the CSIRT of significant incidents. The Competent Authority shall ensure that entities report information enabling determination of cross-border impact. The Competent Authority shall ensure that the competent authority forwards the notification to the CSIRT upon receipt, if entities notify the competent authority of a significant incident. The Competent Authority shall assess whether the entities concerned notify the competent authority of a significant incident. If the entities concerned notify the competent authority, the Competent Authority shall forward the notification to the CSIRT upon receipt. If the entities concerned notify the CSIRT directly, no forwarding is required. The Competent Authority shall assess whether a cross-border or cross-sectoral significant incident has occurred. If a cross-border or cross-sectoral significant incident has occurred, the Competent Authority shall ensure that single points of contact are provided with relevant information in due time. The Competent Authority shall ensure that entities communicate measures to recipients affected by a significant cyber threat, if applicable. The Competent Authority shall carry out the following five activities in parallel: first, the Competent Authority shall ensure that entities submit an early warning to the CSIRT; second, the Competent Authority shall ensure that entities submit an incident notification to the CSIRT; third, the Competent Authority shall ensure that entities submit an intermediate report to the CSIRT; fourth, the Competent Authority shall ensure that entities submit a final report to the CSIRT; fifth, the Competent Authority shall assess whether the incident is ongoing at the time of submission of the final report, and if the incident is ongoing at the time of submission of the final report, the Competent Authority shall ensure that entities provide a progress report and a final report within one month of their handling of the incident, and if the incident is not ongoing, no additional progress report is required. All five activities shall be completed before the next step. The Competent Authority shall assess whether the CSIRT is the initial recipient of the notification. If the CSIRT is not the initial recipient of the notification, the Competent Authority shall cooperate with the CSIRT on guidance, upon request of the notifying entity. The process for the Competent Authority ends.

The process for the Essential and Important Entity starts. The Essential and Important Entity shall assess whether an incident has caused or is capable of causing severe operational disruption of the services or financial loss for the entity concerned, and whether an incident has affected or is capable of affecting other natural or legal persons by causing considerable material or non-material damage. If either condition is met, the Essential and Important Entity shall notify the CSIRT or the competent authority of the significant incident. If neither condition is met, no notification is required. The Essential and Important Entity shall assess whether the significant incident is likely to adversely affect the provision of services to recipients. If the significant incident is likely to adversely affect the provision of services, the Essential and Important Entity shall notify the recipients of their services of the significant incident. The Essential and Important Entity shall report any information to the CSIRT or the competent authority to determine any cross-border impact of the incident. The Essential and Important Entity shall assess whether the recipients are potentially affected by a significant cyber threat. If applicable, the Essential and Important Entity shall communicate to the recipients any measures or remedies to take in response to that threat. The Essential and Important Entity shall carry out the following five activities in parallel: first, the Essential and Important Entity shall submit to the CSIRT or the competent authority an early warning without undue delay and in any event within 24 hours of becoming aware of the significant incident; second, the Essential and Important Entity shall submit to the CSIRT or the competent authority an incident notification without undue delay and in any event within 72 hours of becoming aware of the significant incident; third, upon the request of a CSIRT or the competent authority, the Essential and Important Entity shall submit an intermediate report on relevant status updates; fourth, the Essential and Important Entity shall submit to the CSIRT or the competent authority a final report not later than one month after the submission of the incident notification, except where the incident is ongoing at the time of the submission of the final report, in which case the entity shall provide a progress report at that time and a final report within one month of their handling of the incident; fifth, the Essential and Important Entity shall assess whether the incident is ongoing at the time of the submission of the final report, and if the incident is ongoing, the Essential and Important Entity shall provide a progress report at the time of submission of the final report, except where the incident is still ongoing at the time of the final report, in which case a progress report replaces the final report at that stage with the final report due within one month of handling. All five activities shall be completed before the notification process concludes. The Essential and Important Entity may request additional technical support from the CSIRT if required. The Essential and Important Entity shall assess whether public awareness is necessary to prevent a significant incident or to deal with an ongoing significant incident, or whether disclosure is otherwise in the public interest. If public awareness is necessary and upon request by the CSIRT or the competent authority, the Essential and Important Entity may inform the public about the significant incident after consulting the entity concerned and, where appropriate, in the case of CSIRTs or competent authorities of other Member States concerned. The process for the Essential and Important Entity ends.

The process for the Trust Service Provider starts. The Trust Service Provider shall notify the CSIRT or the competent authority with regard to significant incidents that have an impact on the provision of its trust services, without undue delay and in any event within 24 hours of becoming aware of the significant incident, except that by way of derogation from paragraph 4, first subparagraph, point (b), trust service providers are subject to a 24-hour notification deadline instead of 72 hours. The Trust Service Provider may request guidance or operational advice on the implementation of possible mitigation measures from the CSIRT or the competent authority if required. The process for the Trust Service Provider ends.

The process for the CSIRT or Competent Authority starts. Upon receiving the notification from the Essential and Important Entity or the Trust Service Provider, the CSIRT or Competent Authority shall determine any cross-border impact of the incident. The CSIRT or Competent Authority shall assess whether a cross-border or cross-sectoral significant incident has occurred. If a cross-border or cross-sectoral significant incident has occurred, the CSIRT or Competent Authority shall provide relevant information to the Single Point of Contact in due time. The CSIRT or Competent Authority shall provide a response to the notifying entity, including initial feedback on the significant incident, without undue delay and where possible within 24 hours of receiving the early warning. The CSIRT or Competent Authority shall assess whether the notifying entity requests guidance or operational advice. If the notifying entity requests guidance or operational advice, the CSIRT or Competent Authority shall provide guidance or operational advice on the implementation of possible mitigation measures, except where the CSIRT is not the initial recipient of the notification, in which case the guidance shall be provided by the competent authority in cooperation with the CSIRT. The CSIRT or Competent Authority may request an intermediate report on relevant status updates from the notifying entity if required. The CSIRT or Competent Authority shall assess whether the significant incident is suspected to be of criminal nature. If the significant incident is suspected to be of criminal nature, the CSIRT or Competent Authority shall provide guidance on reporting the significant incident to law enforcement authorities. The CSIRT or Competent Authority shall assess whether public awareness is necessary to prevent a significant incident or to deal with an ongoing significant incident, or whether disclosure is otherwise in the public interest. If public awareness is necessary, the CSIRT or Competent Authority may consult the entity concerned, where appropriate in the case of CSIRTs or competent authorities of other Member States concerned. If public awareness is necessary and after consulting the entity concerned, the CSIRT or Competent Authority may inform the public about the significant incident. If public awareness is necessary and after consulting the entity concerned, the CSIRT or Competent Authority may require the entity to inform the public about the significant incident. The CSIRT or Competent Authority may request the Single Point of Contact to forward notifications to the single points of contact of other affected Member States if required. The CSIRT or Competent Authority shall provide to the competent authorities information about significant incidents, incidents, cyber threats, and near misses notified in accordance with this Article and by entities identified as critical entities. The process for the CSIRT or Competent Authority ends.

The process for the CSIRT starts. Upon receiving a request for additional technical support from the entity concerned, the CSIRT shall provide additional technical support. The process for the CSIRT ends.

The process for the CSIRT or Competent Authority or Single Point of Contact starts. The CSIRT or Competent Authority or Single Point of Contact shall assess whether the significant incident concerns two or more Member States. If the significant incident concerns two or more Member States, the CSIRT or Competent Authority or Single Point of Contact shall inform the other affected Member States and ENISA of the significant incident, where appropriate. The CSIRT or Competent Authority or Single Point of Contact shall preserve the entity's security, commercial interests, and the confidentiality of the information when informing other affected Member States and ENISA. The process for the CSIRT or Competent Authority or Single Point of Contact ends.

The process for the Single Point of Contact starts. Upon receiving a request from the CSIRT or the competent authority, the Single Point of Contact shall forward notifications to the single points of contact of other affected Member States. The Single Point of Contact shall submit to ENISA a summary report every three months, including anonymised and aggregated data on significant incidents, incidents, cyber threats, and near misses. The process for the Single Point of Contact ends.

The process for ENISA starts. Upon receiving the summary report from the Single Point of Contact, ENISA may adopt technical guidance on the parameters of the information in order to contribute to the provision of comparable information, if required. ENISA shall inform the Cooperation Group and the CSIRTs network about its findings on notifications received every six months. The process for ENISA ends.

The process for the Commission starts. The Commission may adopt implementing acts specifying the type of information, the format, and the procedure of a notification and of a communication if required. The Commission shall, by 17 October 2024, adopt implementing acts specifying the cases in which an incident shall be considered to be significant with regard to DNS service providers, TLD name registries, cloud computing service providers, data centre service providers, content delivery network providers, managed service providers, managed security service providers, as well as providers of online marketplaces, of online search engines, and of social networking services platforms. The Commission may adopt implementing acts specifying the cases in which an incident shall be considered to be significant with regard to other essential and important entities if required. The Commission shall exchange advice and cooperate with the Cooperation Group on the draft implementing acts. The Commission shall adopt implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure when adopting implementing acts under paragraph 11. The process for the Commission ends.

The process for the Cooperation Group starts. Upon receiving the draft implementing acts from the Commission, the Cooperation Group shall exchange advice and cooperate with the Commission on the draft. The process for the Cooperation Group ends.
