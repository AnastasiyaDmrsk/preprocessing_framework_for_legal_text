The process contains the following actors: Health Professional, Applicant / Representative, Competent Authority, Commission.

The process starts with the need to exchange health data before a transfer is carried out.

The process for the Health Professional starts. The Health Professional shall obtain explicit consent from the Applicant / Representative prior to transmitting the information referred to in paragraph 1, except where the applicant is physically or legally incapable of giving his or her consent, when such transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests of the applicant or of another person. The Health Professional shall transmit to the Member State responsible information on any special needs of the person to be transferred, for the sole purpose of the provision of medical care or treatment, in so far as the information is available to the competent authority, after having obtained the explicit consent of the applicant / representative or, if the applicant is physically or legally incapable of giving his or her consent, when such transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests of the applicant or of another person. The Health Professional may include information on physical or mental health in specific cases. The Health Professional shall carry out the processing of personal health data. The Health Professional shall exchange information with the health professionals or other persons referred. The Health Professional shall use exchanged information only for the purposes set out in paragraph 1. The Health Professional shall not further process the information. The process for the Health Professional ends.

The process for the Applicant / Representative starts. Upon receiving the request for explicit consent from the Health Professional, the Applicant / Representative shall assess whether to provide consent. If the Applicant / Representative agrees, the Applicant / Representative may provide consent. If the Applicant / Representative does not agree, the Applicant / Representative may refuse consent. The lack of consent, including a refusal to consent, shall not constitute an obstacle to the transfer. The process for the Applicant / Representative ends.

The process for the Competent Authority starts. Upon receiving the transmitted information on special needs from the Health Professional, the Competent Authority shall ensure that those special needs are adequately addressed, including any essential medical care that may be required. The process for the Competent Authority ends.

The process for the Commission starts. The Commission shall draw up the common health certificate. The Commission shall adopt implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure laid down. The Commission shall adopt uniform conditions and practical arrangements for exchanging the information. The Commission shall adopt those implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure laid down. The process for the Commission ends.
