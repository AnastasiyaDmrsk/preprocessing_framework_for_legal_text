The process contains the following actors: Processor, Controller, Supervisory Authority.

The process starts with the detection of a personal data breach.

The process for the Processor starts. The Processor shall notify the Controller of a personal data breach after becoming aware of a personal data breach [t3]. The process for the Processor ends.

The process for the Controller starts upon becoming aware of the personal data breach, including upon receiving the notification from the Processor. The Controller shall assess whether the personal data breach is unlikely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons. If the personal data breach is unlikely to result in a risk, the Controller shall proceed directly to documenting the breach. If the personal data breach is likely to result in a risk, the Controller shall notify the personal data breach to the supervisory authority, not later than 72 hours after having become aware of it [t1]. The Controller shall assess whether the notification was made within 72 hours after having become aware. If the notification was not made within 72 hours, the Controller shall accompany the notification with reasons for the delay [t2]. If the notification was made within 72 hours, the Controller shall proceed to providing the required information. The Controller shall carry out the following 4 activities in parallel: (a) describe the nature of the personal data breach, the categories and approximate number of data subjects concerned, and the categories and approximate number of personal data records concerned; (b) communicate the name and contact details of the data protection officer or other contact point where more information can be obtained; (c) describe the likely consequences of the personal data breach; (d) describe the measures taken or proposed to be taken to address the personal data breach, including, where appropriate, measures to mitigate its possible adverse effects. All 4 activities shall be completed before the Controller proceeds. The Controller shall assess whether it is possible to provide all the information at the same time. If it is not possible to provide all the information at the same time, the Controller may provide the information in phases without undue further delay [t4]. If it is possible to provide all the information at the same time, the Controller shall provide the information together with the notification. The Controller shall document any personal data breaches, comprising the facts relating to the personal data breach, its effects and the remedial action taken [t5]. The Controller shall then assess whether the personal data breach is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons. If the personal data breach is not likely to result in a high risk, the process for the Controller ends. If the personal data breach is likely to result in a high risk, the Controller shall assess whether any of the following exception conditions are met: (a) the Controller has implemented appropriate technical and organisational protection measures that were applied to the personal data affected and render it unintelligible to unauthorised persons, such as encryption; (b) the Controller has taken subsequent measures which ensure that the high risk to the rights and freedoms of data subjects is no longer likely to materialise. If any of those exception conditions are met, the Controller shall not be required to communicate the breach to the data subject, and the process for the Controller ends. If none of those exception conditions are met, the Controller shall assess whether communication to the data subject would involve disproportionate effort. If the communication would involve disproportionate effort, the Controller shall carry out a public communication or similar measure whereby the data subjects are informed in an equally effective manner [t8], and the process for the Controller ends. If the communication would not involve disproportionate effort, the Controller shall communicate the personal data breach to the data subject in clear and plain language, describing the nature of the breach and containing at least the information and measures referred to in the notification requirements under points (b), (c) and (d) [t7]. The process for the Controller ends.

The process for the Supervisory Authority starts upon receiving the notification of the personal data breach from the Controller. The Supervisory Authority shall verify compliance with the documentation obligations of the Controller [t6]. The Supervisory Authority shall consider the likelihood of the personal data breach resulting in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons [t9]. The Supervisory Authority shall assess whether the Controller has already communicated the personal data breach to the data subject. If the Controller has already communicated, the process for the Supervisory Authority ends. If the Controller has not already communicated and the personal data breach is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons, the Supervisory Authority shall assess whether to require communication or whether the paragraph 3 conditions are met. The Supervisory Authority may require the Controller to communicate the data breach to the data subject [t10], except where the conditions referred to in paragraph 3 are met. Alternatively, the Supervisory Authority may decide that any of the conditions referred to in paragraph 3 are met [t11]. The process for the Supervisory Authority ends.
