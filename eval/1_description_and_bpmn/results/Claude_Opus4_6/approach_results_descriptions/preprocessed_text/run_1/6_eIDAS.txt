The process contains the following actors: Member State, Supervisory Body, Another Supervisory Body, Conformity Assessment Body, Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider, Commission.

The process starts with the obligation for Member States to establish a supervisory framework under the Regulation.

The process for the Member State starts. The Member State shall designate a supervisory body. The Member State shall give the necessary powers and adequate resources to the supervisory body. The Member State shall notify to the Commission the names and the addresses of the supervisory bodies. The Member State may require the supervisory body to establish, maintain and update a trust infrastructure if trust infrastructure is required. The Member State may authorise their supervisory bodies to carry out joint investigations if needed. If the Member State authorises its supervisory bodies to carry out joint investigations, the Member State shall agree upon the arrangements and procedures for such joint actions. If the Member State authorises its supervisory bodies to carry out joint investigations, the Member State shall establish the arrangements and procedures for such joint actions. The process for the Member State ends.

The process for the Supervisory Body starts upon designation by the Member State. The Supervisory Body shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel: (1) The Supervisory Body shall supervise qualified trust service providers. (2) The Supervisory Body shall take action in relation to non-qualified trust service providers when informed that those do not meet the requirements. Both activities shall be completed before proceeding to the ongoing supervisory tasks.

The Supervisory Body shall carry out the following 10 activities in parallel: (1) The Supervisory Body shall cooperate with other supervisory bodies. The Supervisory Body shall provide other supervisory bodies with assistance. (2) The Supervisory Body shall analyse the conformity assessment reports. (3) The Supervisory Body shall inform other supervisory bodies and the public about breaches of security or loss of integrity. (4) By 31 March each year, the Supervisory Body shall submit to the Commission a report on its previous year's main activities and a summary of breach notifications. (5) The Supervisory Body shall assess whether to carry out audits or to request a conformity assessment. If the Supervisory Body carries out audits, the Supervisory Body shall carry out audits. If the Supervisory Body requests a conformity assessment, the Supervisory Body shall request a conformity assessment body to perform a conformity assessment of the qualified trust service providers. The Supervisory Body shall transmit the request to the Conformity Assessment Body. (6) The Supervisory Body shall cooperate with the data protection authorities. The Supervisory Body shall inform the data protection authorities about the results where personal data protection rules have been breached. (7) The Supervisory Body shall grant qualified status to trust service providers. The Supervisory Body shall withdraw qualified status from trust service providers. (8) The Supervisory Body shall inform the body responsible for the national trusted list about the status change, except where that body is also the supervisory body. (9) The Supervisory Body shall verify the existence and correct application of provisions on termination plans in cases where the qualified trust service provider ceases its activities. (10) The Supervisory Body shall require that trust service providers remedy any failure to fulfil the requirements. The Supervisory Body shall transmit the requirement to the Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider. All 10 activities shall be completed as part of ongoing supervisory duties.

Upon receiving a trust infrastructure requirement from the Member State, the Supervisory Body shall establish a trust infrastructure. The Supervisory Body shall maintain the trust infrastructure. The Supervisory Body shall update the trust infrastructure.

Upon receiving a justified request from Another Supervisory Body, the Supervisory Body shall assess whether to provide or refuse assistance. If the Supervisory Body is competent, the assistance is proportionate to supervisory activities, and providing the assistance is compatible with the Regulation, the Supervisory Body shall provide assistance. If the supervisory body is not competent to provide the requested assistance, the requested assistance is not proportionate to supervisory activities, or providing the requested assistance would be incompatible with the Regulation, the Supervisory Body shall refuse assistance.

If authorised by the Member State, the Supervisory Body shall carry out joint investigations.

Upon receiving a breach notification from a Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider, the Supervisory Body shall assess whether the breach of security or loss of integrity concerns two or more Member States. If the breach concerns two or more Member States, the Supervisory Body shall inform the supervisory bodies in other Member States concerned and ENISA of the significant incident. The Supervisory Body shall assess whether disclosure of the breach of security or loss of integrity is in the public interest. If in the public interest and the Supervisory Body discloses directly, the Supervisory Body shall inform the public about the breach of security or loss of integrity. If in the public interest and the Supervisory Body delegates disclosure to the trust service provider, the Supervisory Body shall require the trust service provider to inform the public about the breach. Once a year, the Supervisory Body shall provide ENISA with a summary of notifications of breach. The process for the Supervisory Body ends.

The process for Another Supervisory Body starts. Another Supervisory Body shall request assistance from the Supervisory Body. If authorised by the Member State, Another Supervisory Body shall carry out joint investigations. The process for Another Supervisory Body ends.

The process for the Conformity Assessment Body starts. Upon receiving a request from the Supervisory Body, the Conformity Assessment Body shall perform a conformity assessment of the qualified trust service providers. The process for the Conformity Assessment Body ends.

The process for the Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider starts. The Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall take appropriate technical and organisational measures to manage the risks. The Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall take measures to prevent and minimise the impact of security incidents. The Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall inform stakeholders of the adverse effects of any security incidents. The Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall assess whether the breach of security or loss of integrity has a significant impact on the trust service provided or on the personal data. If the breach has a significant impact, the Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall, without undue delay but in any event within 24 hours after having become aware of it, notify the supervisory body and other relevant bodies. If the breach of security or loss of integrity is likely to adversely affect a natural or legal person, the Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall notify the natural or legal person. If required by the Supervisory Body, the Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall inform the public about the breach of security or loss of integrity. If required by the Supervisory Body, the Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider shall remedy failures to fulfil the requirements. The process for the Qualified Trust Service Provider or Non-qualified Trust Service Provider ends.

The process for the Commission starts. Upon receiving the annual report from the Supervisory Body, the Commission shall make the annual report available to Member States. The Commission may define the formats and procedures for the report if adjustments are needed. The Commission shall adopt those implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure. The Commission shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel: (1) The Commission may further specify the measures if specification is required. (2) The Commission may define the formats and procedures for breach notifications if specification is required. Both activities shall be completed before the Commission adopts the corresponding implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure. The process for the Commission ends.
