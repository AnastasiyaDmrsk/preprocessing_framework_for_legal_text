The process contains the following actors: Commission, ENISA (with 2 lanes: ENISA, Management Board), ECCG, Interested Party.

The process starts with the identification of a need for a new or revised European cybersecurity certification scheme.

The process for the Commission starts. The Commission shall assess whether the candidate scheme is included in the Union rolling work programme. If the candidate scheme is included in the Union rolling work programme, the Commission shall assess whether a new candidate scheme or a review of an existing scheme is required. If a new candidate scheme is required, the Commission may request ENISA to prepare a candidate cybersecurity certification scheme on the basis of the Union rolling work programme. If a review of an existing scheme is required, the Commission may request ENISA to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme on the basis of the Union rolling work programme. If the candidate scheme is not included in the Union rolling work programme, the Commission shall assess whether a new candidate scheme or a review of an existing scheme is required in duly justified cases. If a new candidate scheme is required, the Commission may request ENISA to prepare a candidate cybersecurity certification scheme in duly justified cases. If a review of an existing scheme is required, the Commission may request ENISA to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme in duly justified cases. The Commission shall update the Union rolling work programme following a request under paragraph 2. The Commission shall transmit the request to ENISA. Upon receiving the candidate scheme from ENISA, the Commission may adopt implementing acts providing for a European cybersecurity certification scheme, in accordance with the examination procedure. Upon receiving the evaluation results from ENISA, the Commission may request ENISA to start the process of developing a revised candidate scheme in accordance with Article 48 and Article 49, if necessary. The process for the Commission ends.

The process for ENISA starts. Upon receiving a request, ENISA shall assess whether the request originates from the Commission or from the ECCG. If the request originates from the Commission, ENISA shall prepare a candidate cybersecurity certification scheme. If the request originates from the ECCG, ENISA shall assess whether to accept or refuse the request. If ENISA accepts the request, ENISA may prepare a candidate cybersecurity certification scheme. If ENISA refuses the request, ENISA shall give reasons for its refusal. The Management Board shall take the decision to refuse the request from the ECCG. The process for the Management Board lane ends. When preparing a candidate scheme, ENISA shall carry out the following 3 activities in parallel: ENISA shall consult all relevant stakeholders by means of a formal, open, transparent and inclusive consultation process. ENISA shall establish an ad hoc working group for each candidate scheme being prepared. ENISA shall cooperate with the ECCG. All 3 activities shall be completed before ENISA considers the ECCG opinion. ENISA shall take utmost account of the opinion of the ECCG before transmitting the candidate scheme prepared in accordance with paragraphs 3, 4 and 5 to the Commission, except where the opinion of the ECCG shall not bind ENISA, and except where the absence of an ECCG opinion shall not prevent ENISA from transmitting the candidate scheme to the Commission. ENISA shall transmit the candidate scheme to the Commission, except where the absence of the ECCG opinion shall not prevent transmission. At least every five years, ENISA shall evaluate each adopted European cybersecurity certification scheme, taking into account the feedback received from interested parties. ENISA shall transmit the evaluation results to the Commission and the ECCG. Upon receiving a request from the Commission or the ECCG to develop a revised candidate scheme, ENISA shall start developing a revised candidate scheme in accordance with Article 48 and Article 49. The process for ENISA ends.

The process for the ECCG starts. The ECCG shall assess whether a new candidate scheme or a review of an existing scheme is required in duly justified cases where the scheme is not included in the Union rolling work programme. If a new candidate scheme is required, the ECCG may request ENISA to prepare a candidate cybersecurity certification scheme in duly justified cases where the candidate scheme is not included in the Union rolling work programme. If a review of an existing scheme is required, the ECCG may request ENISA to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme in duly justified cases where the existing scheme is not included in the Union rolling work programme. Upon receiving a request for cooperation from ENISA during the preparation of the candidate scheme, the ECCG shall provide ENISA with assistance and expert advice in relation to the preparation of the candidate scheme. The ECCG shall adopt an opinion on the candidate scheme. The ECCG shall transmit the opinion to ENISA. Upon receiving the evaluation results from ENISA, the ECCG may request ENISA to start the process of developing a revised candidate scheme in accordance with Article 48 and Article 49, if necessary. The process for the ECCG ends.

The process for the Interested Party starts. Upon ENISA evaluating an adopted European cybersecurity certification scheme, the Interested Party may provide feedback to ENISA if required. The process for the Interested Party ends.
