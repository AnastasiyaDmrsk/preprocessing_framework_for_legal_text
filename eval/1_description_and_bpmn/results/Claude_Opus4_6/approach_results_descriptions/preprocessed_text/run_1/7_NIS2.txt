The process contains the following actors: Competent Authority, Essential and Important Entity (with lane: Trust Service Provider), CSIRT or Competent Authority (with lane: CSIRT), Single Point of Contact, ENISA, Commission, Cooperation Group.

The process starts with the detection of an incident that has a significant impact on the provision of services by an essential or important entity, simultaneously triggering the notification obligations for the Essential and Important Entity and the supervisory obligations for the Competent Authority.

The process for the Competent Authority starts. The Competent Authority shall ensure that entities notify the CSIRT of significant incidents. The Competent Authority shall ensure that entities report any information enabling the determination of any cross-border impact of the incident. The Competent Authority shall ensure that competent authority forwards notification to CSIRT upon receipt if entities notify competent authority of significant incident. The Competent Authority shall assess whether the entities concerned notify the competent authority of a significant incident. If the entities concerned notify the competent authority, the Competent Authority shall forward the notification to the CSIRT upon receipt. The Competent Authority shall assess whether the significant incident is cross-border or cross-sectoral. If the significant incident is cross-border or cross-sectoral, the Competent Authority shall ensure that single points of contact are provided with relevant information in due time. The Competent Authority shall ensure that entities communicate measures or remedies to the recipients affected by a significant cyber threat, if applicable. The Competent Authority shall ensure that entities submit an early warning to the CSIRT. The Competent Authority shall ensure that entities submit an incident notification to the CSIRT. The Competent Authority shall ensure that entities submit an intermediate report to the CSIRT. The Competent Authority shall ensure that entities submit a final report to the CSIRT. The Competent Authority shall assess whether the incident is ongoing at the time of submission of the final report. If the incident is ongoing at the time of submission of the final report, the Competent Authority shall ensure that entities provide a progress report and a final report within one month of their handling of the incident. The Competent Authority shall cooperate with the CSIRT on guidance if the CSIRT is not the initial recipient of the notification and upon request of the notifying entity. The process for the Competent Authority ends.

The process for the Essential and Important Entity starts. The Essential and Important Entity shall notify the CSIRT or the competent authority of the significant incident if the incident has caused or is capable of causing severe operational disruption of the services or financial loss for the entity concerned, or if the incident has affected or is capable of affecting other natural or legal persons by causing considerable material or non-material damage. The Essential and Important Entity shall notify the recipients of their services of significant incidents if the incident has caused or is capable of causing severe operational disruption of the services or financial loss for the entity concerned, if the incident has affected or is capable of affecting other natural or legal persons by causing considerable material or non-material damage, and if the significant incident is likely to adversely affect the provision of those services. The Essential and Important Entity shall report any information to the CSIRT or the competent authority to determine any cross-border impact of the incident. The Essential and Important Entity shall communicate to the recipients of their services any measures or remedies to take in response to a significant cyber threat, where applicable. The Essential and Important Entity shall carry out the following five activities in parallel: first, the Essential and Important Entity shall submit to the CSIRT or the competent authority an early warning without undue delay and in any event within 24 hours of becoming aware of the significant incident; second, the Essential and Important Entity shall submit to the CSIRT or the competent authority an incident notification without undue delay and in any event within 72 hours of becoming aware of the significant incident; third, the Essential and Important Entity shall submit an intermediate report on relevant status updates upon the request of the CSIRT or the competent authority; fourth, the Essential and Important Entity shall submit to the CSIRT or the competent authority a final report not later than one month after the submission of the incident notification, except where the incident is ongoing at the time of the submission of the final report, in which case the entity shall provide a progress report at that time and a final report within one month of their handling of the incident; fifth, the Essential and Important Entity shall assess whether the incident is ongoing at the time of the submission of the final report, and if the incident is ongoing, the Essential and Important Entity shall provide a progress report at the time of submission of the final report, except where the incident is still ongoing at the time of the final report, in which case a progress report replaces the final report at that stage, with the final report due within one month of handling. All five activities shall be completed before the reporting obligations are fulfilled. The Essential and Important Entity may request additional technical support from the CSIRT if required. The Essential and Important Entity may inform the public about the significant incident if public awareness is necessary to prevent a significant incident or to deal with an ongoing significant incident, or where disclosure of the significant incident is otherwise in the public interest, and after consulting the entity concerned, and upon request, and where appropriate in the case of CSIRTs or competent authorities of other Member States concerned. The process for the Essential and Important Entity ends.

The process for the Trust Service Provider lane starts. The Trust Service Provider shall notify the CSIRT or the competent authority with regard to significant incidents that have an impact on the provision of its trust services, without undue delay and in any event within 24 hours of becoming aware of the significant incident, except where by way of derogation from paragraph 4 first subparagraph point (b) trust service providers are subject to a 24-hour notification deadline instead of 72 hours. The Trust Service Provider may request guidance or operational advice on the implementation of possible mitigation measures from the CSIRT or the competent authority if required. The process for the Trust Service Provider lane ends.

The process for the CSIRT or Competent Authority starts. Upon receiving the notification and any cross-border impact information from the Essential and Important Entity, the CSIRT or the Competent Authority shall determine any cross-border impact of the incident. The CSIRT or the Competent Authority shall assess whether the significant incident is cross-border or cross-sectoral. If the significant incident is cross-border or cross-sectoral, the CSIRT or the Competent Authority shall provide relevant information to the Single Point of Contact. The CSIRT or the Competent Authority may request an intermediate report on relevant status updates from the notifying entity if required. Upon receiving the early warning, the CSIRT or the Competent Authority shall provide a response to the notifying entity including initial feedback on the significant incident, without undue delay and where possible within 24 hours of receiving the early warning. The CSIRT or the Competent Authority shall provide guidance or operational advice on the implementation of possible mitigation measures upon request of the notifying entity, except where the CSIRT is not the initial recipient of the notification, in which case the guidance shall be provided by the competent authority in cooperation with the CSIRT. The CSIRT shall provide additional technical support if the entity concerned so requests. The CSIRT or the Competent Authority shall assess whether the significant incident is suspected to be of criminal nature. If the significant incident is suspected to be of criminal nature, the CSIRT or the Competent Authority shall provide guidance on reporting the significant incident to law enforcement authorities. The CSIRT or the Competent Authority shall assess whether the significant incident concerns two or more Member States. If appropriate and where the significant incident concerns two or more Member States, the CSIRT or the Competent Authority or the Single Point of Contact shall inform the other affected Member States and ENISA of the significant incident. The CSIRT or the Competent Authority or the Single Point of Contact shall preserve the entity's security, commercial interests, and the confidentiality of the information when informing other affected Member States and ENISA. The CSIRT or the Competent Authority shall assess whether public awareness is necessary to prevent a significant incident or to deal with an ongoing significant incident, or whether disclosure of the significant incident is otherwise in the public interest. If public awareness is necessary or disclosure is in the public interest, the CSIRT or the Competent Authority may consult the entity concerned, and where appropriate the CSIRTs or competent authorities of other Member States concerned. The CSIRT or the Competent Authority may inform the public about the significant incident after consulting the entity concerned, and where appropriate in the case of CSIRTs or competent authorities of other Member States concerned, if public awareness is necessary to prevent a significant incident or to deal with an ongoing significant incident, or where disclosure is otherwise in the public interest. The CSIRT or the Competent Authority may require the entity to inform the public about the significant incident after consulting the entity concerned, and where appropriate in the case of CSIRTs or competent authorities of other Member States concerned, if public awareness is necessary to prevent a significant incident or to deal with an ongoing significant incident, or where disclosure is otherwise in the public interest. The CSIRT or the Competent Authority may request the Single Point of Contact to forward notifications to the single points of contact of other affected Member States if required. The CSIRT or the Competent Authority shall provide to the competent authorities information about significant incidents, incidents, cyber threats and near misses notified in accordance with this Article. The process for the CSIRT or Competent Authority ends.

The process for the Single Point of Contact starts. Upon receiving a request from the CSIRT or the competent authority, the Single Point of Contact shall forward notifications to the single points of contact of other affected Member States. The Single Point of Contact shall submit to ENISA every three months a summary report including anonymised and aggregated data on significant incidents, incidents, cyber threats and near misses. The process for the Single Point of Contact ends.

The process for ENISA starts. Upon receiving the summary report from the Single Point of Contact, ENISA may adopt technical guidance on the parameters of the information if required, in order to contribute to the provision of comparable information. ENISA shall inform the Cooperation Group and the CSIRTs network about its findings on notifications received every six months. The process for ENISA ends.

The process for the Commission starts. The Commission may adopt implementing acts further specifying the type of information, the format and the procedure of a notification and of a communication if required. The Commission shall adopt implementing acts further specifying the cases in which an incident shall be considered to be significant with regard to DNS service providers, TLD name registries, cloud computing service providers, data centre service providers, content delivery network providers, managed service providers, managed security service providers, as well as providers of online marketplaces, of online search engines and of social networking services platforms, by 17 October 2024. The Commission may adopt implementing acts further specifying the cases in which an incident shall be considered to be significant with regard to other essential and important entities if required. The Commission shall exchange advice and cooperate with the Cooperation Group on the draft implementing acts. The Commission shall adopt implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure. The process for the Commission ends.

The process for the Cooperation Group starts. Upon receiving the draft implementing acts from the Commission, the Cooperation Group shall exchange advice and cooperate with the Commission on the draft. The process for the Cooperation Group ends.
