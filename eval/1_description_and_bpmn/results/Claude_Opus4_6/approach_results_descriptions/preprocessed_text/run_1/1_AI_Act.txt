The process contains the following actors: Member State, SME, Board, AI Office.

The process starts with the entry into application of the AI Regulation, simultaneously triggering the Member State support process, the SME engagement process, the Board request process, and the AI Office support process.

The process for the Member State starts. The Member State shall carry out the following 4 activities in parallel:

First, the Member State shall provide SMEs with priority access to the AI regulatory sandbox, provided the SMEs have a registered office or a branch in the Union and fulfil the eligibility conditions and selection criteria. The Member State shall not preclude other SMEs from access to the AI regulatory sandbox, provided they fulfil the eligibility conditions and selection criteria.

Second, the Member State shall organise specific awareness raising and training activities on the application of the Regulation tailored to the needs of SMEs.

Third, the Member State shall utilise existing dedicated channels for communication with SMEs. The Member State shall establish new channels where appropriate. The Member State shall provide advice on the implementation of the Regulation. The Member State shall respond to queries about the implementation of the Regulation.

Fourth, the Member State shall facilitate the participation of SMEs in the standardisation development process.

All 4 activities shall be completed before proceeding. Upon receiving the specific interests and needs from the SME, the Member State shall take into account the specific interests and needs of SME providers when setting the fees for conformity assessment. The Member State shall set the fees for conformity assessment, reducing those fees proportionately to the size, market size and other relevant indicators of the SMEs. The process for the Member State ends.

The process for the SME starts. The SME may share its specific interests and needs with the Member State if required. The process for the SME ends.

The process for the Board starts. The Board may request standardised templates from the AI Office if required. The process for the Board ends.

The process for the AI Office starts. The AI Office shall carry out the following 4 activities in parallel:

First, upon receiving a request from the Board, the AI Office shall provide standardised templates for areas covered by the Regulation, as specified by the Board in its request.

Second, the AI Office shall develop a single information platform providing easy information in relation to the Regulation for all operators across the Union. The AI Office shall maintain the single information platform.

Third, the AI Office shall organise appropriate communication campaigns to raise awareness about the obligations arising from the Regulation.

Fourth, the AI Office shall evaluate the convergence of best practices in public procurement procedures in relation to AI systems. The AI Office shall promote the convergence of best practices in public procurement procedures in relation to AI systems.

All 4 activities shall be completed before the process for the AI Office ends.
