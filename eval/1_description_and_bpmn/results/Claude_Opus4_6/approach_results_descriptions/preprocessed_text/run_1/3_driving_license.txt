The process contains the following actors: Member State, Applicant, Commission.

The process starts with an applicant applying for the issuance or renewal of a driving licence.

The process for the Member State starts. Member State shall ensure that applicants undergo a medical examination before a driving licence is issued for the first time, except where the driving licence concerns category AM only if so required by the Member State in accordance with Article 10(1), point (b) [t1]. Member State shall require a medical examination for driving licences of categories C, CE, C1, C1E, D, D1, DE or D1E, regardless of whether a medical examination was undertaken for another category [t3]. Member State shall require a medical examination for category AM only if so required by the Member State [t3].

Member State shall assess whether alternative measures are used instead of a medical examination. If alternative measures are not used, the medical examination path applies. If alternative measures are used, Member State shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel: (1) Member State may require the applicant to fill in a self-assessment form for driving licence categories AM, A, A1, A2, B, B1 and BE, notwithstanding paragraphs 1 and 2, to the extent not otherwise provided for by Annex III [t4]; (2) Member State may establish a national system of assessment of fitness to drive for driving licence categories AM, A, A1, A2, B, B1 and BE, applicable after the licence has been issued following a prior medical examination or self-assessment [t6]. Both activities shall be completed before proceeding to the next step.

Member State may provide for appropriate measures to address non-compliance if the applicant fails to comply with the requirement to fill in a self-assessment form, or if the applicant knowingly provides information in the self-assessment form that is incorrect or incomplete, or if the applicant fails to meet any requirement established in accordance with paragraph 3, point (b) [t7].

Member State may apply the national system to monitor fitness to drive during periods of administrative validity if the Member State has established a national system of assessment of fitness to drive [t8].

Member State shall assess whether the applicant or holder of a driving licence is likely to have one or more of the medical conditions listed in Annex III. If the applicant is likely to have the medical conditions, Member State shall ensure that the applicant undergoes a medical examination prior to the issuance or renewal of the driving licence [t9]. If the applicant is not likely to have the medical conditions, the process continues.

Member State may take measures to raise awareness about the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness for driving if awareness is required [t10]. Member State may take measures to improve the knowledge of the health sector if knowledge improvement is required [t11]. Member State may adopt guidelines for medical practitioners if adopting of guidelines is required [t12]. If the Member State adopts guidelines for medical practitioners, Member State shall inform the Commission of guidelines adopted [t13]. Member State may develop public awareness campaigns if awareness campaigns are required [t15]. If the Member State develops public awareness campaigns, Member State shall inform the Commission of any public awareness campaigns developed [t16]. Member State may set standards stricter than those set out in Annex III if stricter standards are required [t17].

The process for the Member State ends.

The process for the Applicant starts. Applicant shall assess whether a medical examination is required. If a medical examination is required before a driving licence is issued for the first time or renewed, Applicant shall undergo a medical examination [t2]. If a medical examination is required for driving licences of categories C, CE, C1, C1E, D, D1, DE or D1E, Applicant shall undergo a medical examination [t2]. If the driving licence concerns category AM, Applicant shall undergo a medical examination only if so required by the Member State [t2], except where alternative measures are used. If alternative measures are used instead of a medical examination, Applicant shall fill in a self-assessment form for driving licence categories AM, A, A1, A2, B, B1 and BE, if required instead of a medical examination, to the extent not otherwise provided for by Annex III [t5].

The process for the Applicant ends.

The process for the Commission starts. Upon receiving information from a Member State of the adoption of guidelines for medical practitioners, Commission shall make available to the other Member States the guidelines adopted [t14]. Upon receiving information from a Member State of public awareness campaigns developed, Commission shall make available to the other Member States the information [t17].

The process for the Commission ends.
