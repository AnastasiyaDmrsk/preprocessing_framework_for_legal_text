The process contains the following actors: Health Professional, Applicant / Representative, Competent Authority, Commission.

The process starts with the need to exchange health data before a transfer is carried out, for the sole purpose of the provision of medical care or treatment concerning disabled persons, elderly people, pregnant women, minors and persons who have been subject to torture, rape or other serious forms of psychological, physical and sexual violence.

The process for the Health Professional starts. The Health Professional shall assess whether the applicant is physically or legally incapable of giving his or her consent. If the applicant is capable of giving consent, the Health Professional shall obtain explicit consent from the Applicant / Representative prior to transmitting the information referred to in paragraph 1. The Health Professional shall assess whether consent has been provided. If the Applicant / Representative has provided consent, the Health Professional shall transmit to the Member State responsible information on any special needs of the person to be transferred, for the sole purpose of the provision of medical care or treatment, in so far as the information is available to the competent authority in accordance with national law. The Health Professional may include information on that person's physical or mental health in specific cases. If the Applicant / Representative has refused consent, the Health Professional shall not transmit the information. If the applicant is physically or legally incapable of giving his or her consent, the Health Professional shall assess whether such transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests of the applicant or of another person. If such transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests of the applicant or of another person, the Health Professional shall transmit to the Member State responsible information on any special needs of the person to be transferred, for the sole purpose of the provision of medical care or treatment, in so far as the information is available to the competent authority in accordance with national law. The Health Professional may include information on that person's physical or mental health in specific cases. If such transmission is not necessary to protect vital interests, the Health Professional shall not transmit the information. The Health Professional shall carry out the processing of personal health data. The Health Professional shall exchange information only between health professionals or other persons referred. The Health Professional shall use exchanged information only for the purposes set out in paragraph 1. The Health Professional shall not further process the information. The process for the Health Professional ends.

The process for the Applicant / Representative starts. Upon being requested to provide explicit consent by the Health Professional, the Applicant / Representative shall assess whether to consent. If the Applicant / Representative agrees, the Applicant / Representative may provide consent to the Health Professional. If the Applicant / Representative disagrees, the Applicant / Representative may refuse consent. The lack of consent, including a refusal to consent, shall not constitute an obstacle to the transfer. The process for the Applicant / Representative ends.

The process for the Competent Authority starts. Upon receiving the transmitted information on special needs from the Health Professional, the Competent Authority shall ensure that those special needs are adequately addressed, including any essential medical care that may be required. The process for the Competent Authority ends.

The process for the Commission starts. The Commission shall draw up the common health certificate. The Commission shall adopt implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure laid down. The Commission shall adopt uniform conditions and practical arrangements for exchanging the information referred to in this Article. The Commission shall adopt those implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure laid down. The process for the Commission ends.
