The process contains the following actors: Manufacturer (with 3 lanes: Manufacturer of custom-made devices, Manufacturer of implantable devices, Quality Management System), Competent Authority, Patient / User or Successor or Health Insurance Company or Third Party.

The process starts with the Manufacturer placing devices on the market or putting them into service.

The process for the Manufacturer starts. When placing their devices on the market or putting them into service, the Manufacturer shall ensure that devices have been designed and manufactured IAW the requirements. The Manufacturer shall establish a system for risk management. The Manufacturer shall document a system for risk management. The Manufacturer shall implement a system for risk management. The Manufacturer shall maintain a system for risk management. The Manufacturer shall conduct a clinical evaluation.

The Manufacturer shall assess whether the device is a custom-made device. If the device is not a custom-made device, the Manufacturer shall draw up technical documentation. The Manufacturer shall keep up to date technical documentation. If the device is a custom-made device, the Manufacturer of custom-made devices shall draw up documentation. The Manufacturer of custom-made devices shall keep up to date documentation. The Manufacturer of custom-made devices shall keep available for competent authorities documentation.

The Manufacturer shall assess whether compliance with the applicable requirements has been demonstrated. If compliance has been demonstrated, the Manufacturer shall draw up an EU declaration of conformity, except where the Manufacturer is a manufacturer of custom-made devices or investigational devices. The Manufacturer shall affix the CE marking of conformity, except where the Manufacturer is a manufacturer of custom-made devices or investigational devices. If compliance has not been demonstrated, the Manufacturer shall not draw up an EU declaration of conformity or affix the CE marking.

The Manufacturer shall comply with the obligations relating to the UDI system.

The Manufacturer shall assess whether the device is an implantable device. If the device is not an implantable device, the Manufacturer shall keep the technical documentation, the EU declaration of conformity and a copy of any relevant certificate for a period of at least 10 years after the last device has been placed on the market. If the device is an implantable device, the Manufacturer of implantable devices shall keep the technical documentation, the EU declaration of conformity and a copy of any relevant certificate for a period of at least 15 years after the last device has been placed on the market. Upon request by a competent authority, the Manufacturer shall provide technical documentation in its entirety or a summary. The Manufacturer shall assess whether the manufacturer has a registered place of business outside the Union. If the manufacturer has a registered place of business outside the Union, the Manufacturer shall ensure that the authorised representative has the necessary documentation available to allow the authorised representative to fulfil the tasks mentioned in Article 11(3).

The Manufacturer shall carry out the following 3 activities in parallel: (1) the Manufacturer shall ensure that procedures are in place to keep series production in conformity with the requirements; (2) the Manufacturer shall take into account changes in device design or characteristics and changes in the harmonised standards; (3) the Manufacturer shall establish a quality management system, except where the Manufacturer is a manufacturer of investigational devices. The Manufacturer shall document a quality management system, except where the Manufacturer is a manufacturer of investigational devices. The Manufacturer shall implement a quality management system, except where the Manufacturer is a manufacturer of investigational devices. The Manufacturer shall maintain a quality management system, except where the Manufacturer is a manufacturer of investigational devices. The Manufacturer shall keep up to date a quality management system, except where the Manufacturer is a manufacturer of investigational devices. The Manufacturer shall improve a quality management system, except where the Manufacturer is a manufacturer of investigational devices. The Quality Management System shall cover all parts and elements of a manufacturer's organisation, except where the Manufacturer is a manufacturer of investigational devices. The Quality Management System shall govern the structure, responsibilities, procedures, processes and management resources, except where the Manufacturer is a manufacturer of investigational devices. The Quality Management System shall address at least the aspects set out in paragraph 9, except where the Manufacturer is a manufacturer of investigational devices. All 3 activities shall be completed before the next step.

The Manufacturer shall implement the post-market surveillance system. The Manufacturer shall keep up to date the post-market surveillance system. The Manufacturer shall ensure that the device is accompanied by the information set out in Annex I.

The Manufacturer shall assess whether the device is not in conformity with this Regulation. If the device is not in conformity with this Regulation, the Manufacturer shall bring the device into conformity, or withdraw the device, or recall the device. The Manufacturer shall inform the distributors of the device, the authorised representative and importers. The Manufacturer shall assess whether the device presents a serious risk. If the device presents a serious risk, the Manufacturer shall inform the competent authorities and the notified body of the non-compliance. If the device does not present a serious risk, the Manufacturer shall not inform the competent authorities under this provision.

The Manufacturer shall have a system for recording and reporting of incidents and corrective actions.

Upon request by a competent authority, the Manufacturer shall provide the competent authority with all the information and documentation necessary to demonstrate the conformity of the device. If requested by a competent authority, the Manufacturer shall provide samples of the device. If requested by a competent authority, the Manufacturer shall grant access to the device. At the request of a competent authority, the Manufacturer shall cooperate with a competent authority on any corrective action taken.

The Manufacturer shall assess whether the manufacturer has their devices designed or manufactured by another legal or natural person. If the manufacturer has their devices designed or manufactured by another legal or natural person, the Manufacturer shall ensure that the information on the person identity is part of the submitted information.

The Manufacturer shall have measures to provide sufficient financial coverage in respect of their potential liability. The process for the Manufacturer ends.

The process for the Competent Authority starts. The Competent Authority may request technical documentation if required. The Competent Authority may request documentation necessary to demonstrate the conformity of the device if required. The Competent Authority may require that the manufacturer provide samples of the device free of charge if required. The Competent Authority may require to grant access to the device if required. The Competent Authority may request collaboration with manufacturer if required. Upon receiving information from the Manufacturer that the device presents a serious risk and is not in conformity, the Competent Authority shall process the notification of non-compliance. The Competent Authority shall assess whether the manufacturer fails to cooperate or the information and documentation provided is incomplete or incorrect. If the manufacturer fails to cooperate or the information and documentation provided is incomplete or incorrect, the Competent Authority may take all appropriate measures to prohibit or restrict the device. The Competent Authority may withdraw the device from that market if the manufacturer fails to cooperate or the information and documentation provided is incomplete or incorrect. The Competent Authority may recall the device until the manufacturer cooperates or provides information if the manufacturer fails to cooperate or the information and documentation provided is incomplete or incorrect. The Competent Authority shall assess whether the competent authority considers or has reason to believe that a device has caused damage. If the competent authority considers or has reason to believe that a device has caused damage, upon request, the Competent Authority shall facilitate the provision of the information and documentation to the potentially injured patient or user and the patient's or user's successor in title, the patient's or user's health insurance company or other third parties affected by the damage, without prejudice to data protection rules, except where there is an overriding public interest in disclosure, except where the disclosure of the information and documentation is ordinarily dealt with in the context of legal proceedings. The process for the Competent Authority ends.

The process for the Patient / User or Successor or Health Insurance Company or Third Party starts. The Patient / User or Successor or Health Insurance Company or Third Party may request provision of information related to device damage if a competent authority considers or has reason to believe that a device has caused damage. The Patient / User or Successor or Health Insurance Company or Third Party may claim compensation for damage caused by a defective device. The process for the Patient / User or Successor or Health Insurance Company or Third Party ends.
