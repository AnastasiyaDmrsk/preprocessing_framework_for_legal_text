The process contains the following actors: Online Platform Provider, Digital Services Coordinator, Trusted Flagger, Commission, Board.

The process starts with an entity submitting an application for trusted flagger status to the Digital Services Coordinator.

The process for the Digital Services Coordinator starts. The Digital Services Coordinator shall verify that the applicant meets all of the following 3 conditions in parallel: (a) the applicant has particular expertise and competence, (b) the applicant represents collective interests and is independent from any provider of online platforms, (c) the applicant carries out its activities in a diligent, accurate and objective manner. All 3 conditions shall be verified before the next step. The Digital Services Coordinator shall award the status of trusted flagger, upon application by any entity, if the applicant is established and meets all of the conditions. The Digital Services Coordinator shall assess whether the communication obligation is triggered by an award of trusted flagger status in accordance with paragraph 2, or by a suspension of trusted flagger status in accordance with paragraph 6, or by a revocation of trusted flagger status in accordance with paragraph 7. If any of these events has occurred, the Digital Services Coordinator shall communicate to the Commission and the Board the names, addresses and email addresses of the entities awarded, suspended, or revoked trusted flagger status. Upon receiving information from a provider of online platforms indicating that a trusted flagger has submitted insufficiently precise, inaccurate or inadequately substantiated notices, the Digital Services Coordinator shall assess whether there are legitimate reasons to open an investigation. If the Digital Services Coordinator considers that there are legitimate reasons, the Digital Services Coordinator shall suspend the status of trusted flagger during the period of the investigation. The Digital Services Coordinator shall carry out the investigation without undue delay following suspension of trusted flagger status. Before revoking the trusted flagger status, the Digital Services Coordinator shall afford the entity an opportunity to react to the findings of its investigation and its intention to revoke the status. The Digital Services Coordinator shall assess whether the entity still meets the conditions set out in paragraph 2. If the entity no longer meets the conditions, the Digital Services Coordinator shall revoke the trusted flagger status. The process for the Digital Services Coordinator ends.

The process for the Online Platform Provider starts. The Online Platform Provider shall take the necessary technical and organisational measures for notices submitted by trusted flaggers. The Online Platform Provider shall give priority to notices, if the notices are submitted within the trusted flagger's designated area of expertise and through the mechanisms referred to in Article 16. The Online Platform Provider shall process notices, if the notices are submitted within the trusted flagger's designated area of expertise and through the mechanisms referred to in Article 16. The Online Platform Provider shall decide upon notices, if the notices are submitted within the trusted flagger's designated area of expertise and through the mechanisms referred to in Article 16. The Online Platform Provider shall assess whether a trusted flagger has submitted a significant number of insufficiently precise, inaccurate or inadequately substantiated notices. If a trusted flagger has submitted such notices, the Online Platform Provider shall communicate that information to the Digital Services Coordinator, providing the necessary explanations and supporting documents. The process for the Online Platform Provider ends.

The process for the Trusted Flagger starts. At least once a year, the Trusted Flagger shall publish easily comprehensible and detailed reports on notices submitted during the relevant period, covering the following 3 categories in parallel: (a) notices categorised by the identity of the provider of hosting services, (b) notices categorised by the type of allegedly illegal content notified, (c) notices categorised by the action taken by the provider. All 3 categories shall be covered before the next step. The reports shall include an explanation of the procedures in place to ensure that the trusted flagger retains its independence. The Trusted Flagger shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel: send those reports to the Digital Services Coordinator, and make those reports publicly available. Both activities shall be completed before the next step. Upon receiving the findings of the Digital Services Coordinator's investigation and the intention to revoke, the Trusted Flagger shall react to the investigation findings. The process for the Trusted Flagger ends.

The process for the Commission starts. Upon receiving the names, addresses and email addresses from the Digital Services Coordinator, the Commission shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel: publish the information in a publicly available database in an easily accessible and machine-readable format, and keep the database up to date. Both activities shall be completed before the next step. The Commission shall assess whether guidelines are necessary. If necessary, the Commission or Board shall consult before issuing guidelines. Upon completing the consultation, the Commission shall issue guidelines to assist providers of online platforms and Digital Services Coordinators in the application of paragraphs 2, 6 and 7. The process for the Commission ends.

The process for the Board starts. Upon receiving a consultation request from the Commission, the Commission or Board shall consult before the Commission issues guidelines under paragraph 8. The process for the Board ends.
