The process contains the following actors: the Commission, ENISA (with 2 lanes: ENISA, Management Board), the ECCG, Relevant Stakeholders, the Ad Hoc Working Group.

The process starts with the identification of a need for a new or revised European cybersecurity certification scheme by the Commission or the ECCG.

The process for the Commission starts. The Commission shall assess whether the certification scheme is included in the Union rolling work programme. If the scheme is included in the Union rolling work programme, the Commission may request ENISA to prepare a candidate scheme or to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme on the basis of the Union rolling work programme. If the scheme is not included in the Union rolling work programme, the Commission may, in duly justified cases, request ENISA to prepare a candidate scheme or to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme not included in the Union rolling work programme. The Commission shall send the request to ENISA. Upon receiving the candidate scheme from ENISA, the Commission shall assess whether to adopt implementing acts. If the Commission decides to adopt, the Commission may adopt implementing acts providing for a European cybersecurity certification scheme for ICT products, ICT services and ICT processes which meets the requirements set out in Articles 51, 52 and 54. The Commission shall adopt those implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure referred to in Article 66(2). If the Commission decides not to adopt, the process for the Commission ends. After adoption, the Commission may, if necessary, request ENISA to start the process of developing a revised candidate scheme in accordance with Article 48 and Article 49. The process for the Commission ends with the Implementing Acts Adopted end event or with the Non-Adoption end event.

The process for the ECCG starts. In duly justified cases, the ECCG may request ENISA to prepare a candidate scheme or to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme which is not included in the Union rolling work programme. The ECCG shall send the request to ENISA. The Union rolling work programme shall be updated accordingly. The ECCG shall provide ENISA with assistance and expert advice in relation to the preparation of the candidate scheme. The ECCG shall adopt an opinion on the candidate scheme. The ECCG shall transmit the opinion to ENISA. After adoption of the scheme, the ECCG may, if necessary, request ENISA to start the process of developing a revised candidate scheme in accordance with Article 48 and Article 49. The process for the ECCG ends with the Opinion Adopted end event.

The process for ENISA starts. Upon receiving the request, ENISA shall assess whether the request originates from the Commission or from the ECCG. If the request originates from the Commission pursuant to Article 48, ENISA shall prepare a candidate scheme which meets the requirements set out in Articles 51, 52 and 54. If the request originates from the ECCG pursuant to Article 48(2), ENISA shall assess whether to accept the request. If ENISA accepts the request, ENISA may prepare a candidate scheme which meets the requirements set out in Articles 51, 52 and 54. If ENISA refuses the request, ENISA shall give reasons for its refusal. The Management Board shall take the decision to refuse such a request, and the process for ENISA ends with the Request Refused end event. Once preparation begins, ENISA shall carry out the following 3 activities in parallel: (1) ENISA shall consult all relevant stakeholders by means of a formal, open, transparent and inclusive consultation process; (2) ENISA shall establish an ad hoc working group in accordance with Article 20(4) for the purpose of providing ENISA with specific advice and expertise; (3) ENISA shall closely cooperate with the ECCG. All 3 activities shall be completed before ENISA proceeds to finalize the candidate scheme. Upon receiving the opinion from the ECCG, ENISA shall take utmost account of the opinion of the ECCG, except where the opinion of the ECCG shall not bind ENISA, nor shall the absence of such an opinion prevent ENISA from transmitting the candidate scheme. ENISA shall transmit the candidate scheme to the Commission. At least every five years, ENISA shall evaluate each adopted European cybersecurity certification scheme, taking into account the feedback received from interested parties. The process for ENISA ends with the Candidate Scheme Transmitted end event or, following evaluation, with the Evaluation Completed end event.

The process for Relevant Stakeholders starts. Upon receiving the consultation request from ENISA, Relevant Stakeholders shall participate in the formal, open, transparent and inclusive consultation process. Relevant Stakeholders shall provide input to ENISA on the candidate scheme. The process for Relevant Stakeholders ends with the Consultation Input Provided end event.

The process for the Ad Hoc Working Group starts. Upon being established by ENISA in accordance with Article 20(4), the Ad Hoc Working Group shall provide ENISA with specific advice and expertise on the candidate scheme. The process for the Ad Hoc Working Group ends with the Advice Provided end event.
