The process contains the following actors: the Member State, the Applicant or Holder of a Driving Licence, the Commission.

The process starts with the Applicant or Holder of a Driving Licence submitting an application for the issuance or renewal of a driving licence to the Member State.

The process for the Applicant or Holder of a Driving Licence starts. The Applicant or Holder of a Driving Licence shall submit the application for the issuance or renewal of a driving licence to the Member State. Upon being required by the Member State to undergo a medical examination, the Applicant or Holder of a Driving Licence shall undergo a medical examination in which the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness covering all medical conditions set out in Annex III are applied. Alternatively, if the Member State applies an alternative measure under paragraph 3, the Applicant or Holder of a Driving Licence shall fill in a self-assessment form covering the medical conditions set out in Annex III when applying for the issuance or renewal of a driving licence. The Applicant or Holder of a Driving Licence may face appropriate measures provided by the Member State in case of non-compliance with the requirement to fill in the self-assessment form, or for knowingly providing information in the self-assessment form that is incorrect or incomplete, or for failing to meet any requirement established under the national system of assessment of fitness to drive. If, on the basis of information acquired through the alternative measures, it becomes apparent that the Applicant or Holder of a Driving Licence is likely to have one or more of the medical conditions listed in Annex III, the Applicant or Holder of a Driving Licence shall undergo a medical examination prior to the issuance or renewal of the driving licence. The process for the Applicant or Holder of a Driving Licence ends.

The process for the Member State starts. Upon receiving the application, the Member State shall assess whether the application concerns the first issuance or the renewal of a driving licence.

If the application concerns the first issuance of a driving licence, the Member State shall assess whether the application concerns categories C, CE, C1, C1E, D, D1, DE or D1E. If the application concerns categories C, CE, C1, C1E, D, D1, DE or D1E, the Member State shall ensure that the applicant undergoes a medical examination in which the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness covering all medical conditions set out in Annex III are applied, regardless of whether a medical examination was undertaken for another category. If the application does not concern categories C, CE, C1, C1E, D, D1, DE or D1E, the Member State shall assess whether the application concerns category AM. If the application concerns category AM, the Member State shall assess whether it requires a medical examination for category AM in accordance with Article 10(1), point (b). If the Member State requires a medical examination for category AM, the Member State shall ensure that the applicant undergoes a medical examination in which the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness covering all medical conditions set out in Annex III are applied. If the Member State does not require a medical examination for category AM, the Member State shall not require a medical examination for the issuance of the driving licence in category AM. If the application concerns categories A, A1, A2, B, B1 or BE, the Member State shall ensure that the applicant undergoes a medical examination in which the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness covering all medical conditions set out in Annex III are applied.

If the application concerns the renewal of a driving licence, the Member State shall ensure that the applicant undergoes a medical examination covering the medical conditions set out in Annex III, except where the application concerns category AM and the Member State does not require a medical examination for category AM in accordance with Article 10(1), point (b).

For categories AM, A, A1, A2, B, B1 and BE, and to the extent not otherwise provided for by Annex III such as in the case of the appropriate assessment of eyesight for applicants in accordance with Annex III, point 3, the Member State may, instead of requiring a medical examination, apply one or both of the following two alternative measures in parallel: the Member State may require the applicant or holder of the driving licence to fill in a self-assessment form covering the medical conditions set out in Annex III when applying for the issuance or renewal of a driving licence; the Member State may establish a national system of assessment of fitness to drive to ensure that significant changes in physical or mental fitness are reacted to in order to comply with the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness set out in Annex III, after the driving licence has been issued following a medical examination or self-assessment. Both alternative measures shall be completed before the Member State proceeds to assess the results.

The Member State may apply the national system of assessment of fitness to drive in such a way that it allows the monitoring of fitness to drive during periods of administrative validity.

The Member State may provide for appropriate measures to address non-compliance with the requirement to fill in the self-assessment form, or for knowingly providing information in the self-assessment form that is incorrect or incomplete, or for failing to meet any requirement established under the national system of assessment of fitness to drive.

The Member State shall assess whether, on the basis of information acquired pursuant to the alternative measures set out in paragraph 3, it becomes apparent that the applicant or holder of a driving licence is likely to have one or more of the medical conditions listed in Annex III. If it becomes apparent that the applicant or holder is likely to have one or more of the medical conditions listed in Annex III, the Member State shall ensure that the applicant or holder of the driving licence undergoes a medical examination prior to issuing or renewing the driving licence. If it does not become apparent that the applicant or holder is likely to have one or more of the medical conditions listed in Annex III, the Member State shall proceed to issue or renew the driving licence.

The Member State may set stricter standards for the issuance or any subsequent renewal of driving licences than those set out in Annex III.

The Member State may take measures to raise awareness and improve the knowledge of the health sector and of driving licence holders about the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness for driving set out in Annex III. The Member State shall assess whether it adopts guidelines for medical practitioners to help identify driving licence holders who no longer meet the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness to drive. If the Member State adopts such guidelines, the Member State shall inform the Commission thereof. The Member State shall assess whether it develops public awareness campaigns to inform citizens about mental or physical health conditions that may impair fitness to drive. If the Member State develops such public awareness campaigns, the Member State shall inform the Commission thereof. The Member State shall issue or renew the driving licence. The process for the Member State ends.

The process for the Commission starts. Upon receiving information from a Member State that it has adopted guidelines for medical practitioners, the Commission shall make the guidelines available to the other Member States. Upon receiving information from a Member State that it has developed public awareness campaigns, the Commission shall make the information available to the other Member States. The process for the Commission ends.
