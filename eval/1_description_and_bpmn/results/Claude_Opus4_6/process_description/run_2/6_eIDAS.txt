The process contains the following actors: Requesting Supervisory Body, Requested Supervisory Body, Member States, Trust Service Providers, Notified Supervisory Body, Supervisory Bodies in Other Member States, ENISA, Other Relevant Bodies, Natural or Legal Person, the Commission.

The process starts with two independent events simultaneously triggering two subprocesses: a Requesting Supervisory Body issuing a justified request for assistance triggers the mutual assistance subprocess, and Trust Service Providers commencing the provision of trust services triggers the security requirements and breach notification subprocess.

The process for the Requesting Supervisory Body starts. The Requesting Supervisory Body shall cooperate with other supervisory bodies with a view to exchanging good practice. The Requesting Supervisory Body shall send a justified request for assistance to the Requested Supervisory Body. The Requesting Supervisory Body shall receive the response from the Requested Supervisory Body. The process for the Requesting Supervisory Body ends.

The process for the Requested Supervisory Body starts upon receiving a justified request from the Requesting Supervisory Body. The Requested Supervisory Body shall assess whether grounds for refusal exist. If no grounds for refusal exist, the Requested Supervisory Body shall provide assistance to the Requesting Supervisory Body so that supervisory activities can be carried out in a consistent manner. The assistance may cover, in particular, information requests and supervisory measures, such as requests to carry out inspections related to the conformity assessment reports as referred to in Articles 20 and 21. If grounds for refusal exist, the Requested Supervisory Body may refuse the request on any of the following grounds: (a) the supervisory body is not competent to provide the requested assistance; (b) the requested assistance is not proportionate to the supervisory activities of the supervisory body carried out in accordance with Article 17; (c) providing the requested assistance would be incompatible with this Regulation. The Requested Supervisory Body shall transmit the result to the Requesting Supervisory Body. The process for the Requested Supervisory Body ends.

The process for Member States starts. Member States may, where appropriate, authorise their respective supervisory bodies to carry out joint investigations in which staff from other Member States' supervisory bodies is involved. If Member States authorise joint investigations, the Member States concerned shall agree upon and establish the arrangements and procedures for such joint actions in accordance with their national law. The process for Member States ends.

The process for Trust Service Providers starts with the provision of trust services. Trust Service Providers shall take appropriate technical and organisational measures to manage the risks posed to the security of the trust services they provide. Trust Service Providers shall ensure that the level of security is commensurate to the degree of risk, having regard to the latest technological developments. Trust Service Providers shall take measures to prevent and minimise the impact of security incidents. Trust Service Providers shall take measures to inform stakeholders of the adverse effects of any such incidents. Trust Service Providers shall assess whether a breach of security or loss of integrity that has a significant impact on the trust service provided or on the personal data maintained therein has occurred. If no such breach has occurred, the process for Trust Service Providers returns to ongoing security management. If such a breach has occurred, Trust Service Providers shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel: (1) Trust Service Providers shall notify the Notified Supervisory Body of the breach without undue delay but in any event within 24 hours after having become aware of it; (2) Trust Service Providers shall notify Other Relevant Bodies, such as the competent national body for information security or the data protection authority, of the breach without undue delay but in any event within 24 hours after having become aware of it, where applicable. All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step. Trust Service Providers shall assess whether the breach of security or loss of integrity is likely to adversely affect a natural or legal person to whom the trust service has been provided. If the breach is likely to adversely affect a natural or legal person, Trust Service Providers shall notify the Natural or Legal Person of the breach of security or loss of integrity without undue delay. If the breach is not likely to adversely affect a natural or legal person, no notification to the Natural or Legal Person is required. The process for Trust Service Providers ends.

The process for the Notified Supervisory Body starts upon receiving a breach notification from Trust Service Providers. The Notified Supervisory Body shall assess whether the breach of security or loss of integrity concerns two or more Member States. If the breach concerns two or more Member States, the Notified Supervisory Body shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel: (1) the Notified Supervisory Body shall inform the Supervisory Bodies in Other Member States concerned; (2) the Notified Supervisory Body shall inform ENISA. All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step. If the breach does not concern two or more Member States, no cross-border notification is required. The Notified Supervisory Body shall assess whether disclosure of the breach of security or loss of integrity is in the public interest. If disclosure is in the public interest, the Notified Supervisory Body shall inform the public or require the Trust Service Provider to do so. If disclosure is not in the public interest, no public disclosure is required. Once a year, the Notified Supervisory Body shall provide ENISA with a summary of notifications of breach of security and loss of integrity received from Trust Service Providers. The process for the Notified Supervisory Body ends.

The process for Supervisory Bodies in Other Member States starts upon receiving information about a breach from the Notified Supervisory Body. The process for Supervisory Bodies in Other Member States ends.

The process for ENISA starts upon receiving breach information from the Notified Supervisory Body. ENISA shall receive the annual summary of breach notifications from the Notified Supervisory Body. The process for ENISA ends.

The process for Other Relevant Bodies starts upon receiving a breach notification from Trust Service Providers, where applicable. The process for Other Relevant Bodies ends.

The process for the Natural or Legal Person starts upon receiving a breach notification from Trust Service Providers. The process for the Natural or Legal Person ends.

The process for the Commission starts. The Commission shall assess whether implementing acts are required. If implementing acts are required, the Commission may, by means of implementing acts, carry out the following 2 activities in parallel: (1) the Commission may further specify the measures referred to in paragraph 1 of Article 19; (2) the Commission may define the formats and procedures, including deadlines, applicable for the purpose of paragraph 2 of Article 19. All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step. The Commission shall adopt those implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure referred to in Article 48(2). If no implementing acts are required, no further action by the Commission is needed. The process for the Commission ends.
