# Process Description: Article 10 -- General Obligations of Manufacturers (MDR)

The process contains the following actors: Manufacturers, the Commission, Competent Authorities, Natural or Legal Persons.

The process starts with the Manufacturer placing a device on the market or putting it into service.

## Manufacturers

The Manufacturer shall ensure that the device has been designed and manufactured in accordance with the requirements of this Regulation.

The Manufacturer shall establish, document, implement and maintain a system for risk management as described in Section 3 of Annex I.

The Manufacturer shall conduct a clinical evaluation in accordance with the requirements set out in Article 61 and Annex XIV, including a PMCF.

The Manufacturer shall assess whether the device is a custom-made device. If the device is not a custom-made device, the Manufacturer shall draw up and keep up to date technical documentation for the device, including the elements set out in Annexes II and III, such as to allow the conformity of the device with the requirements of this Regulation to be assessed. If the device is a custom-made device, the Manufacturer shall draw up, keep up to date and keep available for Competent Authorities documentation in accordance with Section 2 of Annex XIII.

The Manufacturer shall assess whether compliance with the applicable requirements has been demonstrated following the applicable conformity assessment procedure and whether the device is other than a custom-made or investigational device. If compliance has been demonstrated and the device is other than a custom-made or investigational device, the Manufacturer shall draw up an EU declaration of conformity in accordance with Article 19. The Manufacturer shall affix the CE marking of conformity in accordance with Article 20.

The Manufacturer shall comply with the obligations relating to the UDI system referred to in Article 27. The Manufacturer shall comply with the registration obligations referred to in Articles 29 and 31.

The Manufacturer shall assess whether the device is an implantable device. If the device is an implantable device, the Manufacturer shall keep the technical documentation, the EU declaration of conformity and, if applicable, a copy of any relevant certificate issued in accordance with Article 56, available for Competent Authorities for a period of at least 15 years after the last device has been placed on the market. If the device is not an implantable device, the Manufacturer shall keep the technical documentation, the EU declaration of conformity and, if applicable, a copy of any relevant certificate issued in accordance with Article 56, available for Competent Authorities for a period of at least 10 years after the last device covered by the EU declaration of conformity has been placed on the market.

Upon request by a Competent Authority, the Manufacturer shall provide the technical documentation in its entirety or a summary thereof to that Competent Authority.

The Manufacturer shall assess whether it has a registered place of business outside the Union. If the Manufacturer has a registered place of business outside the Union, the Manufacturer shall ensure that the authorised representative has the necessary documentation permanently available in order to allow the authorised representative to fulfil the tasks mentioned in Article 11(3).

The Manufacturer shall ensure that procedures are in place to keep series production in conformity with the requirements of this Regulation. The Manufacturer shall adequately take into account in a timely manner changes in device design or characteristics and changes in the harmonised standards or CS by reference to which the conformity of a device is declared.

The Manufacturer shall assess whether the device is an investigational device. If the device is not an investigational device, the Manufacturer shall establish, document, implement, maintain, keep up to date and continually improve a quality management system that shall ensure compliance with this Regulation in a manner that is proportionate to the risk class and the type of device. The quality management system shall address at least the following 13 aspects in parallel: (a) a strategy for regulatory compliance, including compliance with conformity assessment procedures and procedures for management of modifications to the devices covered by the system; (b) identification of applicable general safety and performance requirements and exploration of options to address those requirements; (c) responsibility of the management; (d) resource management, including selection and control of suppliers and sub-contractors; (e) risk management as set out in Section 3 of Annex I; (f) clinical evaluation in accordance with Article 61 and Annex XIV, including PMCF; (g) product realisation, including planning, design, development, production and service provision; (h) verification of UDI assignments made in accordance with Article 27(3) and ensuring consistency and validity of information provided in accordance with Article 29; (i) setting-up, implementation and maintenance of a post-market surveillance system in accordance with Article 83; (j) handling communication with Competent Authorities, notified bodies, other economic operators, customers and/or other stakeholders; (k) processes for reporting of serious incidents and field safety corrective actions in the context of vigilance; (l) management of corrective and preventive actions and verification of their effectiveness; (m) processes for monitoring and measurement of output, data analysis and product improvement. All 13 aspects shall be addressed before the quality management system can be considered compliant.

The Manufacturer shall implement and keep up to date the post-market surveillance system in accordance with Article 83.

The Manufacturer shall ensure that the device is accompanied by the information set out in Section 23 of Annex I in an official Union language(s) determined by the Member State in which the device is made available to the user or patient. The Manufacturer shall ensure that the particulars on the label are indelible, easily legible and clearly comprehensible to the intended user or patient.

The Manufacturer shall assess whether it considers or has reason to believe that a device which it has placed on the market or put into service is not in conformity with this Regulation. If the Manufacturer considers or has reason to believe the device is not in conformity, the Manufacturer shall immediately take the necessary corrective action to bring that device into conformity, to withdraw it or to recall it, as appropriate. The Manufacturer shall inform the distributors of the device in question and, where applicable, the authorised representative and importers accordingly. The Manufacturer shall assess whether the device presents a serious risk. If the device presents a serious risk, the Manufacturer shall immediately inform the Competent Authorities of the Member States in which it made the device available of the non-compliance and of any corrective action taken. The Manufacturer shall, where applicable, immediately inform the notified body that issued a certificate for the device in accordance with Article 56 of the non-compliance and of any corrective action taken.

The Manufacturer shall have a system for recording and reporting of incidents and field safety corrective actions as described in Articles 87 and 88.

Upon request by a Competent Authority, the Manufacturer shall provide all the information and documentation necessary to demonstrate the conformity of the device, in an official Union language determined by the Member State concerned. The Manufacturer shall cooperate with a Competent Authority, at its request, on any corrective action taken to eliminate or, if that is not possible, mitigate the risks posed by devices which it has placed on the market or put into service.

The Manufacturer shall assess whether it has its devices designed or manufactured by another legal or natural person. If the Manufacturer has its devices designed or manufactured by another legal or natural person, the Manufacturer shall include information on the identity of that person in the information to be submitted in accordance with Article 30(1).

The Manufacturer shall, in a manner that is proportionate to the risk class, type of device and the size of the enterprise, have measures in place to provide sufficient financial coverage in respect of its potential liability under Directive 85/374/EEC, without prejudice to more protective measures under national law.

The process for the Manufacturer ends.

## The Commission

The process for the Commission starts.

The Commission may adopt delegated acts in accordance with Article 115 amending Annexes II and III in the light of technical progress.

The process for the Commission ends.

## Competent Authorities

The process for the Competent Authorities starts.

Upon receiving a request or upon its own initiative, the Competent Authority shall request technical documentation or a summary thereof from the Manufacturer. The Competent Authority shall receive the technical documentation or summary from the Manufacturer.

The Competent Authority of the Member State in which the Manufacturer has its registered place of business may require that the Manufacturer provide samples of the device free of charge or, where that is impracticable, grant access to the device.

The Competent Authority shall assess whether the Manufacturer fails to cooperate or whether the information and documentation provided is incomplete or incorrect. If the Manufacturer fails to cooperate or the information and documentation provided is incomplete or incorrect, the Competent Authority may, in order to ensure the protection of public health and patient safety, take all appropriate measures to prohibit or restrict the device's being made available on its national market, to withdraw the device from that market or to recall it, until the Manufacturer cooperates or provides complete and correct information.

The Competent Authority shall assess whether it considers or has reason to believe that a device has caused damage. If the Competent Authority considers or has reason to believe that a device has caused damage, the Competent Authority shall, upon request, facilitate the provision of the information and documentation to the potentially injured patient or user and, as appropriate, the patient's or user's successor in title, the patient's or user's health insurance company or other third parties affected by the damage caused to the patient or user, without prejudice to data protection rules and, unless there is an overriding public interest in disclosure, without prejudice to the protection of intellectual property rights. The Competent Authority need not comply with this obligation where disclosure of the information and documentation is ordinarily dealt with in the context of legal proceedings.

The process for the Competent Authorities ends.

## Natural or Legal Persons

The process for Natural or Legal Persons starts.

Natural or Legal Persons may claim compensation for damage caused by a defective device in accordance with applicable Union and national law.

The process for Natural or Legal Persons ends.
