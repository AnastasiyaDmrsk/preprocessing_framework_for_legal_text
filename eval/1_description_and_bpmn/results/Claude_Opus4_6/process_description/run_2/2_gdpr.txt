The process contains the following actors: Processor, Controller, Supervisory Authority, Data Subject.

The process starts with the occurrence of a personal data breach.

The process for the Processor starts. The Processor shall become aware of a personal data breach. The Processor shall notify the Controller of the personal data breach without undue delay after becoming aware of it. The process for the Processor ends.

The process for the Controller starts. Upon receiving notification from the Processor or upon becoming aware of a personal data breach directly, the Controller shall assess whether the personal data breach is unlikely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons. If the personal data breach is unlikely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons, the Controller shall not notify the Supervisory Authority, and the Controller shall proceed to document the breach. If the personal data breach is likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons, the Controller shall prepare the notification to the Supervisory Authority. The Controller shall prepare the notification including the following 4 elements in parallel: (1) describe the nature of the personal data breach including, where possible, the categories and approximate number of data subjects concerned and the categories and approximate number of personal data records concerned; (2) communicate the name and contact details of the data protection officer or other contact point where more information can be obtained; (3) describe the likely consequences of the personal data breach; (4) describe the measures taken or proposed to be taken by the Controller to address the personal data breach, including, where appropriate, measures to mitigate its possible adverse effects. All 4 elements shall be completed before the notification is transmitted to the Supervisory Authority. Where it is not possible to provide all information at the same time, the Controller may provide the information in phases without undue further delay. The Controller shall notify the personal data breach to the Supervisory Authority without undue delay and, where feasible, not later than 72 hours after having become aware of it. The Controller shall assess whether the notification to the Supervisory Authority is made within 72 hours. If the notification is not made within 72 hours, the Controller shall accompany the notification by reasons for the delay. The Controller shall document the personal data breach, comprising the facts relating to the personal data breach, its effects and the remedial action taken. The documentation shall enable the Supervisory Authority to verify compliance with this Article. The Controller shall assess whether the personal data breach is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons. If the breach is not likely to result in a high risk, the Controller shall not communicate the breach to the Data Subject. If the breach is likely to result in a high risk, the Controller shall assess whether any of the following exemption conditions are met: (a) the Controller has implemented appropriate technical and organisational protection measures, and those measures were applied to the personal data affected by the personal data breach, in particular those that render the personal data unintelligible to any person who is not authorised to access it, such as encryption; (b) the Controller has taken subsequent measures which ensure that the high risk to the rights and freedoms of data subjects is no longer likely to materialise; (c) it would involve disproportionate effort. If condition (a) or condition (b) is met, the Controller shall not be required to communicate the personal data breach to the Data Subject. If condition (c) is met, the Controller shall instead make a public communication or similar measure whereby the data subjects are informed in an equally effective manner. If none of the exemption conditions are met, the Controller shall communicate the personal data breach to the Data Subject without undue delay. The communication to the Data Subject shall describe in clear and plain language the nature of the personal data breach. The communication shall contain at least the name and contact details of the data protection officer or other contact point where more information can be obtained. The communication shall contain a description of the likely consequences of the personal data breach. The communication shall contain a description of the measures taken or proposed to be taken by the Controller to address the personal data breach, including, where appropriate, measures to mitigate its possible adverse effects. The process for the Controller ends.

The process for the Supervisory Authority starts. Upon receiving the notification of a personal data breach from the Controller, the Supervisory Authority shall receive the notification. The Supervisory Authority shall be enabled to verify compliance with the Controller's documentation obligations. The Supervisory Authority shall assess whether the Controller has already communicated the personal data breach to the Data Subject. If the Controller has already communicated the breach to the Data Subject, the process for the Supervisory Authority ends. If the Controller has not communicated the personal data breach to the Data Subject, the Supervisory Authority shall assess the likelihood of the personal data breach resulting in a high risk. The Supervisory Authority may require the Controller to communicate the personal data breach to the Data Subject. The Supervisory Authority may alternatively decide that any of the exemption conditions referred to in Article 34(3) are met. The process for the Supervisory Authority ends.

The process for the Data Subject starts. Upon receiving the communication of a personal data breach from the Controller, the Data Subject shall receive the communication describing in clear and plain language the nature of the breach, the contact details of the data protection officer, the likely consequences, and the measures taken or proposed. The process for the Data Subject ends.
