The process contains the following actors: the Transferring Member State (lane: Health Professional or person subject to an equivalent obligation of professional secrecy), the Member State responsible (lane: Health Professional or person subject to an equivalent obligation of professional secrecy), the Applicant and/or Representative, and the Commission.

The process starts with a transfer of a person to the Member State responsible being about to be carried out, triggering the need to exchange health data before the transfer for the sole purpose of the provision of medical care or treatment.

The process for the Transferring Member State starts. The Transferring Member State shall assess whether information on any special needs of the person to be transferred is available to the competent authority in accordance with national law. If the information is not available, the process for the Transferring Member State ends. If the information is available, the Transferring Member State shall assess whether the applicant is physically and legally capable of giving his or her consent. If the applicant is capable, the Transferring Member State shall request explicit consent from the Applicant and/or Representative for the transmission of health data to the Member State responsible. Upon receiving the response from the Applicant and/or Representative, the Transferring Member State shall assess whether explicit consent was given. If explicit consent was given, the Transferring Member State (Health Professional lane) shall transmit information on any special needs of the person to be transferred to the Member State responsible in a common health certificate with the necessary documents attached, which in specific cases may include information on that person's physical or mental health. The lack of consent, including a refusal to consent, shall not constitute an obstacle to the transfer. If explicit consent was not given, the Transferring Member State shall not transmit the health information to the Member State responsible. If the applicant is physically or legally incapable of giving his or her consent, the Transferring Member State shall assess whether transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests of the applicant or of another person. If the transmission is necessary to protect vital interests, the Transferring Member State (Health Professional lane) shall transmit information on any special needs of the person to be transferred to the Member State responsible in a common health certificate with the necessary documents attached. If the transmission is not necessary to protect vital interests, the Transferring Member State shall not transmit the health information to the Member State responsible. The exchange of information shall only take place between the health professionals or other persons subject to an equivalent obligation of professional secrecy. The information exchanged shall only be used for the purposes of medical care or treatment and shall not be further processed. The rules laid down in Article 34(8) to (12) shall apply to the exchange of information. The process for the Transferring Member State ends.

The process for the Applicant and/or Representative starts. Upon receiving the request for explicit consent from the Transferring Member State, the Applicant and/or Representative shall provide or refuse explicit consent for the transmission of health data to the Transferring Member State. The process for the Applicant and/or Representative ends.

The process for the Member State responsible starts. Upon receiving the information on special needs and the common health certificate from the Transferring Member State, the Member State responsible (Health Professional lane) shall ensure that those special needs are adequately addressed, including in particular any essential medical care that may be required. The exchange of information shall only take place between the health professionals or other persons subject to an equivalent obligation of professional secrecy. The information received shall only be used for the purposes of medical care or treatment and shall not be further processed. The rules laid down in Article 34(8) to (12) shall apply to the exchange of information. The process for the Member State responsible ends.

The process for the Commission starts. The Commission shall draw up the common health certificate by means of implementing acts. Those implementing acts shall be adopted in accordance with the examination procedure laid down in Article 44(2). The Commission shall adopt uniform conditions and practical arrangements for exchanging the information by means of implementing acts. Those implementing acts shall be adopted in accordance with the examination procedure laid down in Article 44(2). The process for the Commission ends.
