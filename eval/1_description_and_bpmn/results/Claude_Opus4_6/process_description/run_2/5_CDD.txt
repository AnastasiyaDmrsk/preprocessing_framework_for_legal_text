The process contains the following actors: Member States, Obliged Entities, Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU).

The process starts with the need for an obliged entity to establish a business relationship with a customer or to carry out a transaction.

The process for the Member States starts. Member States shall require that obliged entities verify the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner before the establishment of a business relationship or the carrying out of a transaction. Member States shall assess whether a derogation from the pre-verification requirement is warranted. If it is necessary not to interrupt the normal conduct of business and where there is little risk of money laundering or terrorist financing, Member States may allow verification of the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner to be completed during the establishment of a business relationship. If Member States allow the opening of an account with a credit institution or financial institution, Member States shall require that adequate safeguards are in place to ensure that no transactions are carried out by the customer or on its behalf until full compliance with the customer due diligence requirements laid down in points (a) and (b) of the first subparagraph of Article 13(1) is obtained. Member States shall require that, where an obliged entity is unable to comply with the customer due diligence requirements laid down in point (a), (b) or (c) of the first subparagraph of Article 13(1), the obliged entity shall not carry out a transaction through a bank account, shall not establish a business relationship, shall not carry out the transaction, shall terminate the business relationship, and shall consider making a suspicious transaction report to the FIU. Member States shall not apply the preceding prohibition to notaries, other independent legal professionals, auditors, external accountants and tax advisors, except where those persons ascertain the legal position of their client, or perform the task of defending or representing that client in, or concerning, judicial proceedings, including providing advice on instituting or avoiding such proceedings. Member States shall require that obliged entities apply customer due diligence measures not only to all new customers but also at appropriate times to existing customers on a risk-sensitive basis, including at times when the relevant circumstances of a customer change. The process for the Member States ends.

The process for the Obliged Entities starts. The obliged entity shall verify the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner before the establishment of a business relationship or the carrying out of a transaction. The obliged entity shall assess whether a derogation granted by the Member State applies. If the Member State allows verification during the establishment of the business relationship, the obliged entity shall complete the verification procedures as soon as practicable after initial contact. If the Member State allows the opening of an account with a credit institution or financial institution, the obliged entity shall ensure that adequate safeguards are in place so that no transactions are carried out by the customer or on its behalf until full compliance with the customer due diligence requirements laid down in points (a) and (b) of the first subparagraph of Article 13(1) is obtained. If no derogation applies, the obliged entity shall complete verification before the establishment of the business relationship or the carrying out of the transaction. The obliged entity shall assess whether it is able to comply with the customer due diligence requirements laid down in point (a), (b) or (c) of the first subparagraph of Article 13(1). If the obliged entity is able to comply, the obliged entity shall complete the customer due diligence requirements. If the obliged entity is unable to comply, the obliged entity shall carry out the following 4 activities in parallel: the obliged entity shall not carry out a transaction through a bank account, shall not establish a business relationship or carry out the transaction, shall terminate the business relationship, and shall consider making a suspicious transaction report to the FIU in relation to the customer in accordance with Article 33. All 4 activities shall be completed before the process ends, except where the obliged entity is a notary, other independent legal professional, auditor, external accountant or tax advisor only to the strict extent that those persons ascertain the legal position of their client, or perform the task of defending or representing that client in, or concerning, judicial proceedings, including providing advice on instituting or avoiding such proceedings. The obliged entity shall transmit the suspicious transaction report to the FIU. The obliged entity shall apply the customer due diligence measures not only to all new customers but also at appropriate times to existing customers on a risk-sensitive basis, including at times when the relevant circumstances of a customer change. The process for the Obliged Entities ends.

The process for the FIU starts. Upon receiving a suspicious transaction report from an obliged entity, the FIU shall receive the suspicious transaction report in accordance with Article 33. The process for the FIU ends.
