The process contains the following actors: Entity, Digital Services Coordinator, Provider of Online Platforms, the Commission, the Board.

The process starts with the Entity submitting an application for the status of trusted flagger to the Digital Services Coordinator of the Member State in which the Entity is established.

The process for the Entity starts. The Entity shall submit an application for the status of trusted flagger to the Digital Services Coordinator. The Entity shall demonstrate that it meets all of the following three conditions: (a) it has particular expertise and competence for the purposes of detecting, identifying and notifying illegal content, (b) it is independent from any provider of online platforms, and (c) it carries out its activities for the purposes of submitting notices diligently, accurately and objectively. Upon being awarded the status of trusted flagger by the Digital Services Coordinator, the Trusted Flagger shall submit notices within its designated area of expertise through the mechanisms referred to in Article 16 to the Provider of Online Platforms. At least once a year, the Trusted Flagger shall publish easily comprehensible and detailed reports on notices submitted in accordance with Article 16 during the relevant period. The Trusted Flagger shall categorise in those reports at least the number of notices by (a) the identity of the provider of hosting services, (b) the type of allegedly illegal content notified, and (c) the action taken by the provider. The Trusted Flagger shall include in those reports an explanation of the procedures in place to ensure that it retains its independence. The Trusted Flagger shall send those reports to the awarding Digital Services Coordinator. The Trusted Flagger shall make those reports publicly available, except that the information in those reports shall not contain personal data. If the Digital Services Coordinator affords the Trusted Flagger an opportunity to react to the findings of an investigation and the intention to revoke the Trusted Flagger's status, the Trusted Flagger shall react to those findings. The process for the Entity ends.

The process for the Digital Services Coordinator starts upon receiving the application from the Entity. The Digital Services Coordinator shall assess whether the Entity has demonstrated that it meets all of the following conditions: (a) the Entity has particular expertise and competence for detecting, identifying and notifying illegal content, (b) the Entity is independent from any provider of online platforms, and (c) the Entity carries out its activities for submitting notices diligently, accurately and objectively. If the Entity has demonstrated that it meets all conditions, the Digital Services Coordinator shall award the status of trusted flagger to the Entity. If the Entity has not demonstrated that it meets all conditions, the Digital Services Coordinator shall not award the status of trusted flagger. The Digital Services Coordinator shall communicate to the Commission and the Board the names, addresses and email addresses of the entities to which it has awarded the status of trusted flagger. Upon receiving the annual reports from the Trusted Flagger, the Digital Services Coordinator shall receive those reports. Upon receiving information from a Provider of Online Platforms indicating that a Trusted Flagger has submitted a significant number of insufficiently precise, inaccurate or inadequately substantiated notices through the mechanisms referred to in Article 16, including information gathered in connection to the processing of complaints through the internal complaint-handling systems referred to in Article 20(4), the Digital Services Coordinator shall assess whether there are legitimate reasons to open an investigation. If the Digital Services Coordinator considers that there are legitimate reasons to open an investigation, the Digital Services Coordinator shall suspend the status of trusted flagger during the period of the investigation. The Digital Services Coordinator shall carry out the investigation without undue delay. If the Digital Services Coordinator does not consider that there are legitimate reasons, the Digital Services Coordinator shall not open an investigation. The Digital Services Coordinator shall communicate to the Commission and the Board the names, addresses and email addresses of the entities whose trusted flagger status it has suspended. The Digital Services Coordinator shall determine, following an investigation either on its own initiative or on the basis of information received from third parties including information provided by a Provider of Online Platforms pursuant to paragraph 6, whether the Entity no longer meets the conditions set out in paragraph 2. If the Digital Services Coordinator determines that the Entity no longer meets the conditions, the Digital Services Coordinator shall afford the Entity an opportunity to react to the findings of the investigation and the intention to revoke the Entity's status as trusted flagger. After affording the Entity the opportunity to react, the Digital Services Coordinator shall revoke the status of trusted flagger. The Digital Services Coordinator shall communicate to the Commission and the Board the names, addresses and email addresses of the entities whose trusted flagger status it has revoked. If the Digital Services Coordinator determines that the Entity still meets the conditions, the Digital Services Coordinator shall not revoke the status. The process for the Digital Services Coordinator ends.

The process for the Provider of Online Platforms starts upon receiving notices from the Trusted Flagger through the mechanisms referred to in Article 16. The Provider of Online Platforms shall take the necessary technical and organisational measures to ensure that notices submitted by trusted flaggers, acting within their designated area of expertise, are given priority. The Provider of Online Platforms shall process and decide upon those notices without undue delay. The Provider of Online Platforms shall assess whether it has information indicating that a Trusted Flagger has submitted a significant number of insufficiently precise, inaccurate or inadequately substantiated notices through the mechanisms referred to in Article 16, including information gathered in connection to the processing of complaints through the internal complaint-handling systems referred to in Article 20(4). If the Provider of Online Platforms has such information, the Provider of Online Platforms shall communicate that information to the Digital Services Coordinator that awarded the status of trusted flagger to the entity concerned, providing the necessary explanations and supporting documents. If the Provider of Online Platforms does not have such information, the Provider of Online Platforms shall take no further action in this regard. The process for the Provider of Online Platforms ends.

The process for the Commission starts upon receiving from the Digital Services Coordinator the names, addresses and email addresses of the entities to which the Digital Services Coordinator has awarded the status of trusted flagger, or whose trusted flagger status has been suspended or revoked. The Commission shall publish the information referred to in paragraph 4 in a publicly available database in an easily accessible and machine-readable format. The Commission shall keep the database up to date. The Commission shall assess whether it is necessary to issue guidelines to assist providers of online platforms and Digital Services Coordinators in the application of paragraphs 2, 6 and 7. If the Commission considers it necessary, the Commission shall consult the Board. The Commission shall issue guidelines to assist providers of online platforms and Digital Services Coordinators in the application of paragraphs 2, 6 and 7. If the Commission does not consider it necessary, the Commission shall not issue guidelines. The process for the Commission ends.

The process for the Board starts upon receiving from the Digital Services Coordinator the names, addresses and email addresses of the entities to which the Digital Services Coordinator has awarded the status of trusted flagger, or whose trusted flagger status has been suspended or revoked. The Board shall receive and take note of that information. Upon being consulted by the Commission on the issuance of guidelines to assist providers of online platforms and Digital Services Coordinators in the application of paragraphs 2, 6 and 7, the Board shall provide its consultation to the Commission. The process for the Board ends.
