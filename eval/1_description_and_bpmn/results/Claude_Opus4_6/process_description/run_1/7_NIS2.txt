The process contains the following actors: Essential and important entities, Member State (with 3 lanes: CSIRT, Competent Authority, Single Point of Contact), Recipients of services, Other affected Member States, ENISA, Cooperation Group, CSIRTs network, Competent authorities under Directive (EU) 2022/2557, Law enforcement authorities, The Commission.
The process starts with an essential or important entity becoming aware of an incident that has a significant impact on the provision of its services. An incident shall be considered significant if (a) it has caused or is capable of causing severe operational disruption of the services or financial loss for the entity concerned, or (b) it has affected or is capable of affecting other natural or legal persons by causing considerable material or non-material damage.
Essential and important entities
The process for the Essential and important entities starts.
The entity shall notify, without undue delay, the CSIRT or, where applicable, the competent authority of the significant incident. The mere act of notification shall not subject the notifying entity to increased liability. The entity shall report any information enabling the CSIRT or, where applicable, the competent authority to determine any cross-border impact of the incident.
The entity shall assess whether the significant incident is likely to adversely affect the provision of services to recipients. If the significant incident is likely to adversely affect the provision of services, the entity shall notify, without undue delay, the recipients of their services of the significant incident. If the significant incident is not likely to adversely affect the provision of services, the entity shall proceed to the notification sequence.
For the purpose of the notification, the entity shall submit the following reports in sequence:
The entity shall submit an early warning to the CSIRT or, where applicable, the competent authority, without undue delay and in any event within 24 hours of becoming aware of the significant incident. The early warning shall, where applicable, indicate whether the significant incident is suspected of being caused by unlawful or malicious acts or could have a cross-border impact.
The entity shall assess whether it is a trust service provider. If the entity is a trust service provider and the significant incident has an impact on the provision of its trust services, the entity shall submit the incident notification to the CSIRT or, where applicable, the competent authority, without undue delay and in any event within 24 hours of becoming aware of the significant incident, by way of derogation from the 72-hour deadline. If the entity is not a trust service provider, the entity shall submit the incident notification to the CSIRT or, where applicable, the competent authority, without undue delay and in any event within 72 hours of becoming aware of the significant incident. The incident notification shall, where applicable, update the information provided in the early warning and indicate an initial assessment of the significant incident, including its severity and impact, as well as, where available, the indicators of compromise.
Upon request of the CSIRT or, where applicable, the competent authority, the entity shall submit an intermediate report on relevant status updates.
The entity shall submit a final report not later than one month after the submission of the incident notification. The final report shall include: (i) a detailed description of the incident, including its severity and impact; (ii) the type of threat or root cause that is likely to have triggered the incident; (iii) applied and ongoing mitigation measures; (iv) where applicable, the cross-border impact of the incident.
The entity shall assess whether the incident is ongoing at the time of the submission of the final report. If the incident is ongoing, the entity shall submit a progress report at that time instead of the final report. The entity shall submit a final report within one month of their handling of the incident. If the incident is not ongoing, the notification sequence is complete.
The entity shall assess whether a significant cyber threat potentially affects recipients of its services. If a significant cyber threat potentially affects recipients, the entity shall communicate, without undue delay, to the recipients of their services any measures or remedies that those recipients are able to take in response to that threat. Where appropriate, the entity shall also inform those recipients of the significant cyber threat itself. If no significant cyber threat potentially affects recipients, no communication to recipients is required.
The process for the Essential and important entities ends.
Member State -- CSIRT lane
The process for the CSIRT starts upon receiving a notification from the entity.
The CSIRT shall provide, without undue delay and where possible within 24 hours of receiving the early warning, a response to the notifying entity. The response shall include initial feedback on the significant incident.
The CSIRT shall assess whether the entity requests guidance on the implementation of possible mitigation measures. If the entity requests guidance, the CSIRT shall provide guidance or operational advice on the implementation of possible mitigation measures. If the entity does not request guidance, the CSIRT shall proceed without providing guidance.
The CSIRT shall assess whether the entity requests additional technical support. If the entity requests additional technical support, the CSIRT shall provide additional technical support. If the entity does not request additional technical support, the CSIRT shall proceed without providing technical support.
The CSIRT shall assess whether the significant incident is suspected to be of criminal nature. If the significant incident is suspected to be of criminal nature, the CSIRT shall provide guidance to the entity on reporting the significant incident to law enforcement authorities. If the significant incident is not suspected to be of criminal nature, the CSIRT shall proceed without providing guidance on law enforcement reporting.
The CSIRT shall assess whether the significant incident concerns two or more Member States. If the significant incident concerns two or more Member States, the CSIRT shall inform, without undue delay, the other affected Member States and ENISA of the significant incident. Such information shall include the type of information received in accordance with the notification requirements. The CSIRT shall, in accordance with Union or national law, preserve the entity's security and commercial interests as well as the confidentiality of the information provided. If the significant incident does not concern two or more Member States, no cross-border notification is required.
The CSIRT shall assess whether public awareness is necessary to prevent the significant incident or to deal with an ongoing significant incident, or whether disclosure is otherwise in the public interest. If public awareness is necessary or disclosure is in the public interest, the CSIRT may, after consulting the entity concerned, inform the public about the significant incident or require the entity to do so. If public awareness is not necessary, the CSIRT shall proceed without public disclosure.
The CSIRT shall assess whether any of the notifying entities are identified as critical entities under Directive (EU) 2022/2557. If the notifying entity is a critical entity under Directive (EU) 2022/2557, the CSIRT shall provide to the competent authorities under Directive (EU) 2022/2557 information about significant incidents, incidents, cyber threats and near misses notified by that entity. If the notifying entity is not a critical entity under Directive (EU) 2022/2557, no information is forwarded to those competent authorities.
The process for the CSIRT ends.
Member State -- Competent Authority lane
The process for the Competent Authority starts upon receiving a significant incident notification from the entity, where the entity notifies the competent authority instead of, or in addition to, the CSIRT.
The competent authority shall forward the notification to the CSIRT upon receipt.
The competent authority shall assess whether the CSIRT is the initial recipient of the notification. If the CSIRT is not the initial recipient, the competent authority shall provide guidance to the entity in cooperation with the CSIRT. If the CSIRT is the initial recipient, the competent authority shall not provide separate guidance.
The competent authority shall assess whether the significant incident is suspected to be of criminal nature. If the significant incident is suspected to be of criminal nature, the competent authority shall provide guidance to the entity on reporting the significant incident to law enforcement authorities. If the significant incident is not suspected to be of criminal nature, the competent authority shall proceed without providing guidance on law enforcement reporting.
The competent authority shall assess whether public awareness is necessary to prevent the significant incident or to deal with an ongoing significant incident, or whether disclosure is otherwise in the public interest. If public awareness is necessary or disclosure is in the public interest, the competent authority may, after consulting the entity concerned, inform the public about the significant incident or require the entity to do so. If public awareness is not necessary, the competent authority shall proceed without public disclosure.
The competent authority shall assess whether any of the notifying entities are identified as critical entities under Directive (EU) 2022/2557. If the notifying entity is a critical entity under Directive (EU) 2022/2557, the competent authority shall provide to the competent authorities under Directive (EU) 2022/2557 information about significant incidents, incidents, cyber threats and near misses notified by that entity. If the notifying entity is not a critical entity under Directive (EU) 2022/2557, no information is forwarded to those competent authorities.
The process for the Competent Authority ends.
Member State -- Single Point of Contact lane
The process for the Single Point of Contact starts.
In the case of a cross-border or cross-sectoral significant incident, the single point of contact shall receive relevant information notified in accordance with the notification requirements in due time.
At the request of the CSIRT or the competent authority, the single point of contact shall forward notifications received to the single points of contact of other affected Member States.
At least every three months, the single point of contact shall submit to ENISA a summary report including anonymised and aggregated data on significant incidents, incidents, cyber threats and near misses.
The process for the Single Point of Contact ends.
Recipients of services
The process for the Recipients of services starts upon receiving a notification from the entity.
Upon receiving notification of a significant incident from the entity, the recipients of services are informed of the significant incident that is likely to adversely affect the provision of services.
Upon receiving communication of measures or remedies from the entity regarding a significant cyber threat, the recipients of services are informed of those measures or remedies and, where appropriate, of the significant cyber threat itself.
The process for the Recipients of services ends.
Other affected Member States
The process for the Other affected Member States starts upon receiving information from the CSIRT, the competent authority, or the single point of contact.
Upon receiving information about a significant incident that concerns two or more Member States, the other affected Member States are informed of the significant incident, including the type of information received in accordance with the notification requirements.
Upon receiving forwarded notifications from the single point of contact, the single points of contact of the other affected Member States are informed.
The process for the Other affected Member States ends.
ENISA
The process for ENISA starts.
Upon receiving information about a cross-border significant incident from the CSIRT, competent authority, or single point of contact, ENISA is informed of the significant incident.
Upon receiving the summary report from the single point of contact, ENISA may adopt technical guidance on the parameters of the information to be included in the summary report, in order to contribute to the provision of comparable information.
At least every six months, ENISA shall inform the Cooperation Group and the CSIRTs network about its findings on notifications received.
The process for ENISA ends.
Cooperation Group
The process for the Cooperation Group starts upon receiving findings from ENISA.
Upon receiving findings on notifications from ENISA every six months, the Cooperation Group is informed.
The Commission shall exchange advice and cooperate with the Cooperation Group on draft implementing acts referred to in paragraph 11.
The process for the Cooperation Group ends.
CSIRTs network
The process for the CSIRTs network starts upon receiving findings from ENISA.
Upon receiving findings on notifications from ENISA every six months, the CSIRTs network is informed.
The process for the CSIRTs network ends.
Competent authorities under Directive (EU) 2022/2557
The process for the Competent authorities under Directive (EU) 2022/2557 starts upon receiving information from the CSIRT or competent authority.
Upon receiving information about significant incidents, incidents, cyber threats and near misses from the CSIRT or the competent authority regarding entities identified as critical entities under Directive (EU) 2022/2557, the competent authorities under Directive (EU) 2022/2557 are informed.
The process for the Competent authorities under Directive (EU) 2022/2557 ends.
Law enforcement authorities
The process for the Law enforcement authorities starts upon receiving a report from the entity.
Upon the entity reporting the significant incident following guidance from the CSIRT or competent authority, where the significant incident is suspected to be of criminal nature, the law enforcement authorities receive the report.
The process for the Law enforcement authorities ends.
The Commission
The process for the Commission starts.
The Commission may adopt implementing acts further specifying the type of information, the format and the procedure of a notification submitted pursuant to the reporting obligations and of a communication submitted pursuant to the cyber threat communication obligation.
By 17 October 2024, the Commission shall, with regard to DNS service providers, TLD name registries, cloud computing service providers, data centre service providers, content delivery network providers, managed service providers, managed security service providers, as well as providers of online marketplaces, of online search engines and of social networking services platforms, adopt implementing acts further specifying the cases in which an incident shall be considered to be significant. The Commission may adopt such implementing acts with regard to other essential and important entities.
The Commission shall exchange advice and cooperate with the Cooperation Group on the draft implementing acts.
The Commission shall adopt those implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure referred to in Article 39(2).
The process for the Commission ends.
