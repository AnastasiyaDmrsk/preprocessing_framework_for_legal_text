The process contains the following actors: Member States, Notified Bodies, the Board, the AI Office.

The process starts with the obligation to implement measures for providers and deployers, in particular SMEs, including start-ups, under the Regulation.

The process for the Member States starts. Member States shall carry out the following 4 activities in parallel:
- Member States shall assess whether SMEs, including start-ups, having a registered office or a branch in the Union fulfil the eligibility conditions and selection criteria for AI regulatory sandboxes. If the SMEs fulfil the eligibility conditions and selection criteria, Member States shall provide them with priority access to the AI regulatory sandboxes. If other SMEs, including start-ups, other than those referred to above also fulfil the eligibility conditions and selection criteria, Member States shall not preclude them from access to the AI regulatory sandbox.
- Member States shall organise specific awareness raising and training activities on the application of the Regulation tailored to the needs of SMEs including start-ups, deployers and, as appropriate, local public authorities.
- Member States shall utilise existing dedicated channels for communication with SMEs including start-ups, deployers, other innovators and, as appropriate, local public authorities to provide advice and respond to queries about the implementation of the Regulation, including as regards participation in AI regulatory sandboxes. Member States shall assess whether it is appropriate to establish new communication channels. If it is appropriate, Member States shall establish new dedicated channels for communication with SMEs including start-ups, deployers, other innovators and, as appropriate, local public authorities.
- Member States shall facilitate the participation of SMEs and other relevant stakeholders in the standardisation development process.
All 4 activities shall be completed before the process for the Member States ends. The process for the Member States ends.

The process for the Notified Bodies starts. Notified Bodies shall take into account the specific interests and needs of SME providers, including start-ups, when setting the fees for conformity assessment under Article 43. Notified Bodies shall reduce those fees proportionately to the size, market size and other relevant indicators of the SME providers. The process for the Notified Bodies ends.

The process for the Board starts. The Board shall send a request to the AI Office specifying the areas covered by the Regulation for which standardised templates are needed. The process for the Board ends.

The process for the AI Office starts. The AI Office shall carry out the following 4 activities in parallel:
- Upon receiving the request from the Board, the AI Office shall provide standardised templates for areas covered by the Regulation, as specified by the Board in its request.
- The AI Office shall develop and maintain a single information platform providing easy to use information in relation to the Regulation for all operators across the Union.
- The AI Office shall organise appropriate communication campaigns to raise awareness about the obligations arising from the Regulation.
- The AI Office shall evaluate and promote the convergence of best practices in public procurement procedures in relation to AI systems.
All 4 activities shall be completed before the process for the AI Office ends. The process for the AI Office ends.
