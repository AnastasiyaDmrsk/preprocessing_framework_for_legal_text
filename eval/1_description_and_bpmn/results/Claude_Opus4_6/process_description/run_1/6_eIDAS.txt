The process contains the following actors: Requesting Supervisory Body, Requested Supervisory Body, Member States, Trust Service Providers, Notified Supervisory Body, Supervisory Bodies in other Member States, Other Relevant Bodies, Natural or Legal Person, ENISA, The Commission.

The articles describe two distinct processes: the Mutual Assistance process (Article 18) and the Security Requirements and Breach Notification process (Article 19).

**Process 1: Mutual Assistance**

The process starts with the Requesting Supervisory Body identifying a need for assistance and sending a justified request to another supervisory body.

The process for the Requesting Supervisory Body starts. The Requesting Supervisory Body shall cooperate with other supervisory bodies with a view to exchanging good practice. The Requesting Supervisory Body shall send a justified request for assistance to the Requested Supervisory Body. The mutual assistance may cover, in particular, information requests and supervisory measures, such as requests to carry out inspections related to the conformity assessment reports as referred to in Articles 20 and 21. The process for the Requesting Supervisory Body ends with the request sent end event.

The process for the Requested Supervisory Body starts. Upon receiving the justified request from the Requesting Supervisory Body, the Requested Supervisory Body shall assess whether grounds for refusal apply. If no grounds for refusal apply, the Requested Supervisory Body shall provide assistance to the Requesting Supervisory Body so that the activities of supervisory bodies can be carried out in a consistent manner. If grounds for refusal apply, the Requested Supervisory Body shall refuse the request on any of the following grounds: (a) the supervisory body is not competent to provide the requested assistance; (b) the requested assistance is not proportionate to the supervisory activities of the supervisory body carried out in accordance with Article 17; (c) providing the requested assistance would be incompatible with this Regulation. The process for the Requested Supervisory Body ends with either the assistance provided end event or the request refused end event.

The process for the Member States starts. Where appropriate, the Member States may authorise their respective supervisory bodies to carry out joint investigations in which staff from other Member States' supervisory bodies is involved. The Member States concerned shall agree upon and establish the arrangements and procedures for such joint actions in accordance with their national law. The process for the Member States ends with the joint investigation arrangements established end event.

**Process 2: Security Requirements and Breach Notification**

The process starts with Trust Service Providers' ongoing obligation to manage security risks and is further triggered by the Trust Service Providers becoming aware of a breach of security or loss of integrity that has a significant impact on the trust service provided or on the personal data maintained therein.

The process for the Trust Service Providers starts. Trust Service Providers shall take appropriate technical and organisational measures to manage the risks posed to the security of the trust services they provide. Trust Service Providers shall ensure that the level of security is commensurate to the degree of risk, having regard to the latest technological developments. Trust Service Providers shall take measures to prevent and minimise the impact of security incidents. Trust Service Providers shall take measures to inform stakeholders of the adverse effects of any such incidents.

Upon becoming aware of a breach of security or loss of integrity that has a significant impact, Trust Service Providers shall, without undue delay but in any event within 24 hours, notify the Notified Supervisory Body. Trust Service Providers shall, where applicable, notify Other Relevant Bodies, such as the competent national body for information security or the data protection authority.

Trust Service Providers shall assess whether the breach of security or loss of integrity is likely to adversely affect a natural or legal person to whom the trust service has been provided. If the breach is likely to adversely affect a natural or legal person, Trust Service Providers shall notify the Natural or Legal Person of the breach without undue delay. If the breach is not likely to adversely affect a natural or legal person, no notification to the Natural or Legal Person is required. The process for the Trust Service Providers ends with the breach notifications completed end event.

The process for the Notified Supervisory Body starts. Upon receiving the breach notification from Trust Service Providers, the Notified Supervisory Body shall assess whether the breach of security or loss of integrity concerns two or more Member States. If the breach concerns two or more Member States, the Notified Supervisory Body shall inform the Supervisory Bodies in other Member States concerned. The Notified Supervisory Body shall inform ENISA. If the breach does not concern two or more Member States, no cross-border notification is required.

The Notified Supervisory Body shall assess whether disclosure of the breach of security or loss of integrity is in the public interest. If disclosure is in the public interest, the Notified Supervisory Body shall inform the public or shall require the Trust Service Provider to do so. If disclosure is not in the public interest, no public disclosure is required.

Once a year, the Notified Supervisory Body shall provide ENISA with a summary of notifications of breach of security and loss of integrity received from Trust Service Providers. The process for the Notified Supervisory Body ends with the annual summary provided end event.

The process for the Supervisory Bodies in other Member States starts. Upon receiving information from the Notified Supervisory Body, the Supervisory Bodies in other Member States shall receive notification of the cross-border breach. The process for the Supervisory Bodies in other Member States ends with the cross-border notification received end event.

The process for Other Relevant Bodies starts. Upon receiving notification from Trust Service Providers, Other Relevant Bodies shall receive the breach notification. The process for Other Relevant Bodies ends with the breach notification received end event.

The process for the Natural or Legal Person starts. Upon receiving notification from Trust Service Providers, the Natural or Legal Person shall receive notification of the breach of security or loss of integrity. The process for the Natural or Legal Person ends with the breach notification received end event.

The process for ENISA starts. Upon receiving information from the Notified Supervisory Body regarding a cross-border breach, ENISA shall receive that information. ENISA shall receive the annual summary of breach notifications from the Notified Supervisory Body. The process for ENISA ends with the annual summary received end event.

The process for The Commission starts. The Commission may, by means of implementing acts, further specify the measures referred to in paragraph 1. The Commission may, by means of implementing acts, define the formats and procedures, including deadlines, applicable for the purpose of paragraph 2. The Commission shall adopt those implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure referred to in Article 48(2). The process for The Commission ends with the implementing acts adopted end event.
