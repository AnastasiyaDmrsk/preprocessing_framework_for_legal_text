The process contains the following actors: the Commission, ENISA (with 2 lanes: ENISA, Management Board), the ECCG, and the Ad hoc working group.

The process starts with the identification of a need to prepare or review a European cybersecurity certification scheme.

The process for the Commission starts. The Commission shall assess whether the certification scheme is included in the Union rolling work programme. If the scheme is included in the Union rolling work programme, the Commission may request ENISA to prepare a candidate scheme or to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme on the basis of the Union rolling work programme. If the scheme is not included in the Union rolling work programme, the Commission may, in duly justified cases, request ENISA to prepare a candidate scheme or to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme. In that case, the Union rolling work programme shall be updated accordingly. Upon receiving the candidate scheme from ENISA, the Commission may adopt implementing acts providing for a European cybersecurity certification scheme for ICT products, ICT services and ICT processes which meets the requirements set out in Articles 51, 52 and 54. The Commission shall adopt those implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure referred to in Article 66(2). If necessary following ENISA's evaluation of an adopted scheme, the Commission may request ENISA to start the process of developing a revised candidate scheme in accordance with Article 48. The process for the Commission ends.

The process for the ECCG starts. In duly justified cases, the ECCG may request ENISA to prepare a candidate scheme or to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme which is not included in the Union rolling work programme. In that case, the Union rolling work programme shall be updated accordingly. Upon receiving a request for assistance from ENISA during the preparation of the candidate scheme, the ECCG shall provide ENISA with assistance and expert advice in relation to the preparation of the candidate scheme. The ECCG shall adopt an opinion on the candidate scheme. The ECCG shall transmit the opinion to ENISA. If necessary following ENISA's evaluation of an adopted scheme, the ECCG may request ENISA to start the process of developing a revised candidate scheme in accordance with Article 48. The process for the ECCG ends.

The process for ENISA starts. ENISA shall assess whether the request originates from the Commission or from the ECCG. If the request originates from the Commission pursuant to Article 48, ENISA shall prepare a candidate scheme which meets the requirements set out in Articles 51, 52 and 54. If the request originates from the ECCG pursuant to Article 48(2), ENISA shall assess whether to accept the request. If ENISA accepts the request, ENISA may prepare a candidate scheme which meets the requirements set out in Articles 51, 52 and 54. If ENISA refuses the request, the Management Board shall take the decision to refuse such a request. ENISA shall give reasons for its refusal. When preparing a candidate scheme, ENISA shall consult all relevant stakeholders by means of a formal, open, transparent and inclusive consultation process. ENISA shall establish an ad hoc working group in accordance with Article 20(4) for the purpose of providing ENISA with specific advice and expertise. ENISA shall request assistance and expert advice from the ECCG. ENISA shall closely cooperate with the ECCG. Upon receiving the opinion of the ECCG, ENISA shall take utmost account of the opinion of the ECCG before transmitting the candidate scheme to the Commission, except that the opinion of the ECCG shall not bind ENISA, and except that the absence of such an opinion shall not prevent ENISA from transmitting the candidate scheme to the Commission. ENISA shall transmit the candidate scheme to the Commission. At least every five years, ENISA shall evaluate each adopted European cybersecurity certification scheme, taking into account the feedback received from interested parties. The process for ENISA ends.

The process for the Ad hoc working group starts. Upon establishment by ENISA, the Ad hoc working group shall provide ENISA with specific advice and expertise regarding the candidate scheme. The process for the Ad hoc working group ends.
