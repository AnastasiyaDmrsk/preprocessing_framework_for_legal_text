The process contains the following actors: the Applicant (and/or his or her representative), the Transferring Member State (lane: Health Professional), the Member State responsible (lane: Health Professional), and the Commission.

The process starts with the need to exchange health data before a transfer of a person is carried out, for the sole purpose of the provision of medical care or treatment, in particular concerning disabled persons, elderly people, pregnant women, minors, and persons who have been subject to torture, rape, or other serious forms of psychological, physical, and sexual violence.

The process for the Commission starts. The Commission shall, by means of implementing acts, draw up the common health certificate. The Commission shall adopt those implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure laid down in Article 44(2). The Commission shall, by means of implementing acts, adopt uniform conditions and practical arrangements for exchanging the health information referred to in paragraph 1. The Commission shall adopt those implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure laid down in Article 44(2). The process for the Commission ends.

The process for the Transferring Member State starts. The Transferring Member State shall assess whether the applicant is physically or legally capable of giving his or her consent. If the applicant is capable of giving consent, the Transferring Member State shall request explicit consent from the Applicant (and/or his or her representative) for the transmission of health information to the Member State responsible.

The process for the Applicant starts upon receiving the request for explicit consent from the Transferring Member State. The Applicant shall assess whether to give explicit consent for the transmission of health information. If the Applicant gives explicit consent, the Applicant shall communicate explicit consent to the Transferring Member State. If the Applicant does not give explicit consent, the Applicant shall communicate refusal of consent to the Transferring Member State. The process for the Applicant ends.

Upon receiving the Applicant's response, the Transferring Member State shall assess whether explicit consent has been obtained. If explicit consent has been obtained, the Health Professional of the Transferring Member State shall transmit to the Member State responsible information on any special needs of the person to be transferred, in so far as it is available to the competent authority in accordance with national law, which in specific cases may include information on that person's physical or mental health. The Health Professional shall transfer that information in a common health certificate with the necessary documents attached. If explicit consent has not been obtained, including a refusal to consent, the Transferring Member State shall not transmit the health information. The lack of consent, including a refusal to consent, shall not constitute an obstacle to the transfer of the person.

If the applicant is physically or legally incapable of giving his or her consent, the Transferring Member State shall assess whether the transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests of the applicant or of another person. If the transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests, the Health Professional of the Transferring Member State shall transmit to the Member State responsible information on any special needs of the person to be transferred, in so far as it is available to the competent authority in accordance with national law, which in specific cases may include information on that person's physical or mental health. The Health Professional shall transfer that information in a common health certificate with the necessary documents attached. If the transmission is not necessary to protect the vital interests, the Transferring Member State shall not transmit the health information.

The processing of the personal health data shall only be carried out by a Health Professional who is subject, under national law or rules established by national competent bodies, to the obligation of professional secrecy, or by another person subject to an equivalent obligation of professional secrecy. The exchange of information shall only take place between the Health Professionals or other persons subject to an equivalent obligation of professional secrecy. The information exchanged shall only be used for the purposes of the provision of medical care or treatment and shall not be further processed. The rules laid down in Article 34(8) to (12) shall apply to the exchange of information. The process for the Transferring Member State ends.

The process for the Member State responsible starts upon receiving the health information in the common health certificate from the Transferring Member State. The Member State responsible shall ensure that the special needs of the person transferred are adequately addressed. The Member State responsible shall provide any essential medical care that may be required. The process for the Member State responsible ends.
