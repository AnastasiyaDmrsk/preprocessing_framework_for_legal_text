The process contains the following actors: Entity, Provider of Online Platforms, Digital Services Coordinator, the Commission, the Board.

The process starts with the Entity submitting an application for the status of trusted flagger to the Digital Services Coordinator of the Member State in which the Entity is established.

The process for the Entity starts. The Entity shall submit an application for the status of trusted flagger to the Digital Services Coordinator. The Entity shall demonstrate that it meets all of the following 3 conditions in parallel: (1) it has particular expertise and competence for the purposes of detecting, identifying and notifying illegal content, (2) it is independent from any provider of online platforms, (3) it carries out its activities for the purposes of submitting notices diligently, accurately and objectively. Upon being awarded the status of trusted flagger by the Digital Services Coordinator, the Entity shall submit notices through the mechanisms referred to in Article 16, acting within its designated area of expertise. At least once a year, the Entity shall publish easily comprehensible and detailed reports on notices submitted in accordance with Article 16 during the relevant period. The Entity shall list in the report at least the number of notices categorised by the identity of the provider of hosting services, the type of allegedly illegal content notified, and the action taken by the provider. The Entity shall include in those reports an explanation of the procedures in place to ensure that the trusted flagger retains its independence. The Entity shall send those reports to the awarding Digital Services Coordinator. The Entity shall make the reports publicly available, except that the information in those reports shall not contain personal data. Upon receiving from the Digital Services Coordinator the findings of an investigation and the intention to revoke the Entity's trusted flagger status, the Entity shall react to the findings and the intention to revoke its status as trusted flagger. The process for the Entity ends.

The process for the Provider of Online Platforms starts. The Provider of Online Platforms shall take the necessary technical and organisational measures to ensure that notices submitted by trusted flaggers, acting within their designated area of expertise, through the mechanisms referred to in Article 16, are given priority. The Provider of Online Platforms shall process and decide upon those notices without undue delay. The Provider of Online Platforms shall assess whether it has information indicating that a trusted flagger has submitted a significant number of insufficiently precise, inaccurate or inadequately substantiated notices through the mechanisms referred to in Article 16, including information gathered in connection to the processing of complaints through the internal complaint-handling systems referred to in Article 20(4). If the Provider of Online Platforms has such information, the Provider of Online Platforms shall communicate that information to the Digital Services Coordinator that awarded the status of trusted flagger to the entity concerned. The Provider of Online Platforms shall provide the necessary explanations and supporting documents to the Digital Services Coordinator. If the Provider of Online Platforms does not have such information, no communication to the Digital Services Coordinator is required. The process for the Provider of Online Platforms ends.

The process for the Digital Services Coordinator starts. Upon receiving the application from the Entity, the Digital Services Coordinator shall assess whether the Entity meets all conditions set out in paragraph 2. If the Entity meets all conditions, the Digital Services Coordinator shall award the status of trusted flagger to the Entity. If the Entity does not meet all conditions, the Digital Services Coordinator shall not award the status of trusted flagger. The Digital Services Coordinator shall communicate to the Commission and the Board the names, addresses and email addresses of entities to which it has awarded the status of trusted flagger in accordance with paragraph 2, or whose trusted flagger status it has suspended in accordance with paragraph 6, or whose trusted flagger status it has revoked in accordance with paragraph 7. Upon receiving information from the Provider of Online Platforms pursuant to paragraph 6, the Digital Services Coordinator shall assess whether there are legitimate reasons to open an investigation. If the Digital Services Coordinator considers that there are legitimate reasons to open an investigation, the Digital Services Coordinator shall suspend the status of trusted flagger during the period of the investigation. The Digital Services Coordinator shall carry out the investigation without undue delay. Following an investigation, either on its own initiative or on the basis of information received from third parties, including the information provided by a Provider of Online Platforms pursuant to paragraph 6, the Digital Services Coordinator shall assess whether the entity no longer meets the conditions set out in paragraph 2. If the Digital Services Coordinator determines that the entity no longer meets the conditions, the Digital Services Coordinator shall afford the entity an opportunity to react to the findings of the investigation and the intention to revoke the entity's status as trusted flagger. The Digital Services Coordinator shall revoke the status of trusted flagger. If the Digital Services Coordinator determines that the entity still meets the conditions, the Digital Services Coordinator shall not revoke the status of trusted flagger. The process for the Digital Services Coordinator ends.

The process for the Commission starts. Upon receiving the information referred to in paragraph 4 from the Digital Services Coordinator, the Commission shall publish the information in a publicly available database in an easily accessible and machine-readable format. The Commission shall keep the database up to date. The Commission shall assess whether it is necessary to issue guidelines to assist providers of online platforms and Digital Services Coordinators in the application of paragraphs 2, 6 and 7. If it is necessary, the Commission shall consult the Board. The Commission shall issue the guidelines. If it is not necessary, no guidelines are issued. The process for the Commission ends.

The process for the Board starts. Upon receiving the communication from the Digital Services Coordinator pursuant to paragraph 4, the Board shall receive the names, addresses and email addresses of the entities to which trusted flagger status has been awarded, suspended or revoked. Upon being consulted by the Commission pursuant to paragraph 8, the Board shall provide input on the guidelines to assist providers of online platforms and Digital Services Coordinators in the application of paragraphs 2, 6 and 7. The process for the Board ends.
