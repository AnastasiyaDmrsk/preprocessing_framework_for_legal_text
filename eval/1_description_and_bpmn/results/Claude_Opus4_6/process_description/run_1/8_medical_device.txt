The process contains the following actors: Manufacturers, the European Commission, Competent Authorities of the Member States, Distributors, Importers, the Authorised Representative, the Notified Body, Natural or Legal Persons.

The process starts with the Manufacturer intending to place a device on the market or put it into service.

The process for the Manufacturers starts. Manufacturers shall ensure that devices have been designed and manufactured in accordance with the requirements of this Regulation when placing them on the market or putting them into service. Manufacturers shall establish, document, implement and maintain a system for risk management as described in Section 3 of Annex I. Manufacturers shall conduct a clinical evaluation in accordance with the requirements set out in Article 61 and Annex XIV, including a PMCF.

Manufacturers shall assess whether the device is a custom-made device. If the device is not a custom-made device, Manufacturers shall draw up and keep up to date technical documentation for those devices. The technical documentation shall allow the conformity of the device with the requirements of this Regulation to be assessed. The technical documentation shall include the elements set out in Annexes II and III. If the device is a custom-made device, Manufacturers shall draw up documentation in accordance with Section 2 of Annex XIII. Manufacturers shall keep up to date that documentation. Manufacturers shall keep available that documentation for Competent Authorities.

Manufacturers shall assess whether the device is a custom-made or investigational device. If the device is not a custom-made or investigational device, and where compliance with the applicable requirements has been demonstrated following the applicable conformity assessment procedure, Manufacturers shall draw up an EU declaration of conformity in accordance with Article 19. Manufacturers shall affix the CE marking of conformity in accordance with Article 20. If the device is a custom-made or investigational device, no EU declaration of conformity or CE marking obligation applies under this provision.

Manufacturers shall comply with the obligations relating to the UDI system referred to in Article 27. Manufacturers shall comply with the registration obligations referred to in Articles 29 and 31.

Manufacturers shall assess whether the device is an implantable device. If the device is an implantable device, Manufacturers shall keep the technical documentation, the EU declaration of conformity and, if applicable, a copy of any relevant certificate issued in accordance with Article 56, including any amendments and supplements, available for the Competent Authorities for a period of at least 15 years after the last device has been placed on the market. If the device is not an implantable device, Manufacturers shall keep that documentation available for the Competent Authorities for a period of at least 10 years after the last device covered by the EU declaration of conformity has been placed on the market.

Upon request by a Competent Authority, Manufacturers shall provide the technical documentation in its entirety or a summary thereof to that Competent Authority.

Manufacturers shall assess whether they have a registered place of business outside the Union. If the Manufacturer has a registered place of business outside the Union, the Manufacturer shall ensure that the Authorised Representative has the necessary documentation permanently available, in order to allow the Authorised Representative to fulfil the tasks mentioned in Article 11(3). If the Manufacturer has a registered place of business within the Union, no obligation regarding the Authorised Representative arises under this provision.

Manufacturers shall ensure that procedures are in place to keep series production in conformity with the requirements of this Regulation. Manufacturers shall adequately take into account, in a timely manner, changes in device design or characteristics and changes in the harmonised standards or CS by reference to which the conformity of a device is declared.

Manufacturers shall assess whether the device is an investigational device. If the device is not an investigational device, Manufacturers shall establish, document, implement, maintain, keep up to date and continually improve a quality management system that shall ensure compliance with this Regulation in the most effective manner and in a manner that is proportionate to the risk class and the type of device. The quality management system shall cover all parts and elements of a Manufacturer's organisation dealing with the quality of processes, procedures and devices. The quality management system shall govern the structure, responsibilities, procedures, processes and management resources required to implement the principles and actions necessary to achieve compliance with the provisions of this Regulation. The quality management system shall address at least the following 13 aspects in parallel: (a) a strategy for regulatory compliance, including compliance with conformity assessment procedures and procedures for management of modifications to the devices covered by the system; (b) identification of applicable general safety and performance requirements and exploration of options to address those requirements; (c) responsibility of the management; (d) resource management, including selection and control of suppliers and sub-contractors; (e) risk management as set out in Section 3 of Annex I; (f) clinical evaluation in accordance with Article 61 and Annex XIV, including PMCF; (g) product realisation, including planning, design, development, production and service provision; (h) verification of the UDI assignments made in accordance with Article 27(3) to all relevant devices and ensuring consistency and validity of information provided in accordance with Article 29; (i) setting-up, implementation and maintenance of a post-market surveillance system, in accordance with Article 83; (j) handling communication with Competent Authorities, Notified Bodies, other economic operators, customers and/or other stakeholders; (k) processes for reporting of serious incidents and field safety corrective actions in the context of vigilance; (l) management of corrective and preventive actions and verification of their effectiveness; (m) processes for monitoring and measurement of output, data analysis and product improvement. All 13 aspects shall be addressed before the quality management system is considered complete. If the device is an investigational device, no quality management system obligation applies under this provision.

Manufacturers shall implement and keep up to date the post-market surveillance system in accordance with Article 83.

Manufacturers shall ensure that the device is accompanied by the information set out in Section 23 of Annex I in an official Union language(s) determined by the Member State in which the device is made available to the user or patient. Manufacturers shall ensure that the particulars on the label are indelible, easily legible and clearly comprehensible to the intended user or patient.

Manufacturers shall assess whether they consider or have reason to believe that a device which they have placed on the market or put into service is not in conformity with this Regulation. If the device is not in conformity, Manufacturers shall immediately take the necessary corrective action to bring that device into conformity, to withdraw it or to recall it, as appropriate. Manufacturers shall inform the Distributors of the device in question. Manufacturers shall inform the Authorised Representative, where applicable. Manufacturers shall inform the Importers, where applicable. Manufacturers shall then assess whether the device presents a serious risk. If the device presents a serious risk, Manufacturers shall immediately inform the Competent Authorities of the Member States in which they made the device available, in particular of the non-compliance and of any corrective action taken. Manufacturers shall inform the Notified Body that issued a certificate for the device in accordance with Article 56, where applicable, in particular of the non-compliance and of any corrective action taken. If the device does not present a serious risk, no notification to Competent Authorities or the Notified Body is required under this provision. If the device is in conformity, no corrective action is required under this provision.

Manufacturers shall have a system for recording and reporting of incidents and field safety corrective actions as described in Articles 87 and 88.

Upon request by a Competent Authority, Manufacturers shall provide all the information and documentation necessary to demonstrate the conformity of the device, in an official Union language determined by the Member State concerned. Manufacturers shall cooperate with a Competent Authority, at its request, on any corrective action taken to eliminate or, if that is not possible, mitigate the risks posed by devices which they have placed on the market or put into service.

Manufacturers shall assess whether they have their devices designed or manufactured by another legal or natural person. If the Manufacturer has its devices designed or manufactured by another legal or natural person, the Manufacturer shall include the information on the identity of that person in the information submitted in accordance with Article 30(1). If the Manufacturer does not have its devices designed or manufactured by another person, no obligation arises under this provision.

Manufacturers shall have measures in place to provide sufficient financial coverage in respect of their potential liability under Directive 85/374/EEC, in a manner that is proportionate to the risk class, type of device and the size of the enterprise, except where more protective measures under national law apply. The process for the Manufacturers ends.

The process for the European Commission starts. The Commission is empowered to adopt delegated acts in accordance with Article 115 amending Annexes II and III in the light of technical progress. The process for the European Commission ends.

The process for the Competent Authorities of the Member States starts. Upon receiving a request for technical documentation from a Manufacturer, the Competent Authority shall indicate whether it requires the documentation in its entirety or a summary thereof. The Competent Authority of the Member State in which the Manufacturer has its registered place of business may require that the Manufacturer provide samples of the device free of charge or, where that is impracticable, grant access to the device. The Competent Authority shall assess whether the Manufacturer cooperates and whether the information and documentation provided is complete and correct. If the Manufacturer fails to cooperate or the information and documentation provided is incomplete or incorrect, the Competent Authority may, in order to ensure the protection of public health and patient safety, take all appropriate measures to prohibit or restrict the device's being made available on its national market, to withdraw the device from that market or to recall it, until the Manufacturer cooperates or provides complete and correct information. If the Manufacturer cooperates and the information is complete and correct, the Competent Authority shall proceed without restrictive measures under this provision. The Competent Authority shall assess whether it considers or has reason to believe that a device has caused damage. If the Competent Authority considers or has reason to believe that a device has caused damage, the Competent Authority shall, upon request, facilitate the provision of the information and documentation to the potentially injured patient or user and, as appropriate, the patient's or user's successor in title, the patient's or user's health insurance company or other third parties affected by the damage caused to the patient or user, except where data protection rules are prejudiced, and except where intellectual property rights are prejudiced unless there is an overriding public interest in disclosure. The Competent Authority shall assess whether disclosure of the information and documentation is ordinarily dealt with in the context of legal proceedings. If disclosure is ordinarily dealt with in the context of legal proceedings, the Competent Authority need not comply with the disclosure obligation. If disclosure is not dealt with in the context of legal proceedings, the Competent Authority shall comply with the disclosure obligation. If the Competent Authority does not consider or have reason to believe that a device has caused damage, no disclosure obligation arises under this provision. The process for the Competent Authorities of the Member States ends.

The process for the Distributors starts. Upon receiving notification of non-conformity from the Manufacturer, Distributors shall be informed accordingly of the non-compliance and any corrective action taken. The process for the Distributors ends.

The process for the Importers starts. Upon receiving notification of non-conformity from the Manufacturer, where applicable, Importers shall be informed accordingly of the non-compliance and any corrective action taken. The process for the Importers ends.

The process for the Authorised Representative starts. Upon receiving notification of non-conformity from the Manufacturer, where applicable, the Authorised Representative shall be informed accordingly of the non-compliance and any corrective action taken. Where the Manufacturer has a registered place of business outside the Union, the Authorised Representative shall have the necessary documentation permanently available in order to fulfil the tasks mentioned in Article 11(3). The process for the Authorised Representative ends.

The process for the Notified Body starts. Upon receiving notification of serious risk non-conformity from the Manufacturer, where applicable, the Notified Body shall be informed of the non-compliance and of any corrective action taken. The process for the Notified Body ends.

The process for Natural or Legal Persons starts. Natural or legal persons may claim compensation for damage caused by a defective device in accordance with applicable Union and national law. The process for Natural or Legal Persons ends.
