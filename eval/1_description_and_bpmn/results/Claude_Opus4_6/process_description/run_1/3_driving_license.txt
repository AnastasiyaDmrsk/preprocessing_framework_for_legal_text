The process contains the following actors: Member States, Applicant, The Commission.

The process starts with the Applicant applying for the first-time issuance or renewal of a driving licence.

The process for the Applicant starts. The Applicant shall submit an application for the issuance or renewal of a driving licence to the Member State. The Applicant shall await the determination by the Member State of the applicable assessment method. If the Member State requires a medical examination, the Applicant shall undergo a medical examination in which the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness covering all medical conditions set out in Annex III are applied. If the Member State requires the Applicant to fill in a self-assessment form as an alternative measure, the Applicant shall fill in a self-assessment form covering the medical conditions set out in Annex III when applying for the issuance or renewal of a driving licence. If, on the basis of information acquired from the alternative measures, the Member State determines that the Applicant is likely to have one or more medical conditions listed in Annex III, the Applicant shall undergo a medical examination prior to the issuance or renewal of the driving licence. The process for the Applicant ends.

The process for the Member States starts. Member States shall receive the application for the issuance or renewal of a driving licence from the Applicant. Member States shall assess whether the application is for the first-time issuance or for the renewal of a driving licence.

If the application is for the first-time issuance of a driving licence, Member States shall assess the category of the driving licence applied for. If the category is C, CE, C1, C1E, D, D1, DE or D1E, Member States shall ensure that the Applicant undergoes a medical examination covering all the medical conditions set out in Annex III, regardless of whether a medical examination was undertaken for another category. If the category is AM, Member States shall assess whether a medical examination for category AM is required in accordance with Article 10(1), point (b). If a medical examination is required for category AM, Member States shall ensure that the Applicant undergoes a medical examination. If a medical examination is not required for category AM, Member States shall not require a medical examination for the issuance of a category AM driving licence. If the category is A, A1, A2, B, B1 or BE, Member States shall ensure that the Applicant undergoes a medical examination covering all the medical conditions set out in Annex III.

If the application is for the renewal of a driving licence, Member States shall ensure that the Applicant undergoes a medical examination covering the medical conditions set out in Annex III. This shall apply to the renewal of driving licences in category AM only if so required by the Member State in accordance with Article 10(1), point (b).

Notwithstanding the above requirements for medical examinations, and to the extent not otherwise provided for by Annex III such as the appropriate assessment of eyesight in accordance with Annex III, point 3, Member States shall assess whether to require a medical examination or to apply alternative measures for categories AM, A, A1, A2, B, B1 and BE. If Member States choose to apply alternative measures, Member States may apply one or both of the following two measures: Member States may require the Applicant to fill in a self-assessment form covering the medical conditions set out in Annex III when applying for the issuance or renewal of a driving licence. Member States may establish a national system of assessment of fitness to drive to ensure that significant changes in physical or mental fitness are reacted to in order to comply with the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness set out in Annex III, after the driving licence has been issued to the Applicant following a medical examination or self-assessment. If Member States choose not to apply alternative measures, Member States shall require a medical examination as set out above.

Member States may provide for appropriate measures to address non-compliance with the requirement to fill in a self-assessment form. Member States may provide for appropriate measures to address the Applicant knowingly providing information in the self-assessment form that is incorrect or incomplete. Member States may provide for appropriate measures to address failure to meet any requirement established under the national system of assessment of fitness to drive.

Member States may apply the national system of assessment of fitness to drive in such a way that it allows the monitoring of fitness to drive during periods of administrative validity.

If, on the basis of information acquired pursuant to the alternative measures, it becomes apparent that the Applicant is likely to have one or more of the medical conditions listed in Annex III, Member States shall ensure that the Applicant undergoes a medical examination prior to issuing or renewing the driving licence.

Member States may take measures to raise awareness and improve the knowledge of the health sector and of driving licence holders about the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness for driving set out in Annex III. If Member States adopt guidelines for medical practitioners to help identify driving licence holders who no longer meet the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness to drive, Member States shall inform the Commission thereof. If Member States develop public awareness campaigns to inform citizens about mental or physical health conditions that may impair fitness to drive, Member States shall inform the Commission thereof.

Member States may apply stricter standards for the issuance or any subsequent renewal of driving licences than those set out in Annex III.

Member States shall issue or renew the driving licence upon the Applicant meeting all applicable requirements. The process for the Member States ends.

The process for The Commission starts. Upon receiving information from a Member State about guidelines adopted for medical practitioners, The Commission shall make the guidelines available to the other Member States. Upon receiving information from a Member State about public awareness campaigns to inform citizens about mental or physical health conditions that may impair fitness to drive, The Commission shall make the information available to the other Member States. The process for The Commission ends.
