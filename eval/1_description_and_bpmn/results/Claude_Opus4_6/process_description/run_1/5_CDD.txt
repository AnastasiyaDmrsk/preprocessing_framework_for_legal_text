The process contains the following actors: Member States, Obliged Entities (with 3 lanes: General Obliged Entities, Credit Institutions and Financial Institutions, Legal Professionals), and the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU).

The process starts with a customer seeking to establish a business relationship or to carry out a transaction with an obliged entity.

The process for Member States starts. Member States shall require that verification of the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner take place before the establishment of a business relationship or the carrying out of the transaction. Member States shall assess whether to grant a derogation from the pre-verification requirement. If a derogation is granted, it is necessary not to interrupt the normal conduct of business, and there is little risk of money laundering or terrorist financing, Member States may allow verification of the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner to be completed during the establishment of a business relationship. Member States shall require that those verification procedures be completed as soon as practicable after initial contact. If no derogation is granted, the pre-verification requirement remains in force. Member States shall assess whether to allow the opening of an account with a credit institution or financial institution before full CDD compliance. If adequate safeguards are in place to ensure that transactions are not carried out by the customer or on its behalf until full compliance with the CDD requirements laid down in points (a) and (b) of Article 13(1) is obtained, Member States may allow such account opening. If adequate safeguards are not in place, Member States shall not allow account opening before full CDD compliance. Member States shall require that, where an obliged entity is unable to comply with the CDD requirements laid down in points (a), (b), or (c) of Article 13(1), the obliged entity refrain from carrying out any transaction or establishing any business relationship, terminate the existing business relationship, and consider making a suspicious transaction report to the FIU. Member States shall not apply the preceding prohibition to notaries, other independent legal professionals, auditors, external accountants, and tax advisors, except only to the strict extent that those persons ascertain the legal position of their client or perform the task of defending or representing that client in or concerning judicial proceedings, including providing advice on instituting or avoiding such proceedings. Member States shall require that obliged entities apply the CDD measures not only to all new customers but also at appropriate times to existing customers on a risk-sensitive basis, including at times when the relevant circumstances of a customer change. The process for Member States ends.

The process for the General Obliged Entities lane starts upon receiving a customer request to establish a business relationship or to carry out a transaction. The obliged entity shall assess whether verification of the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner must be completed before the establishment of the business relationship or the carrying out of the transaction or whether a derogation applies. If no derogation applies, the obliged entity shall verify the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner before the establishment of the business relationship or the carrying out of the transaction. If a derogation applies, it is necessary not to interrupt the normal conduct of business, and there is little risk of money laundering or terrorist financing, the obliged entity may complete verification of the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner during the establishment of the business relationship. The obliged entity shall complete those verification procedures as soon as practicable after initial contact. The obliged entity shall assess whether it is able to comply with the CDD requirements laid down in points (a), (b), or (c) of Article 13(1). If the obliged entity is able to comply, the obliged entity shall proceed with the business relationship or the transaction. If the obliged entity is unable to comply with the CDD requirements, the obliged entity shall carry out the following 4 activities in parallel: the obliged entity shall not carry out a transaction through a bank account; the obliged entity shall not establish a business relationship or carry out the transaction; the obliged entity shall terminate the business relationship; the obliged entity shall consider making a suspicious transaction report to the FIU in relation to the customer in accordance with Article 33. All 4 activities shall be completed before the process continues. The obliged entity shall assess whether to make a suspicious transaction report. If the obliged entity decides to make a suspicious transaction report, the obliged entity shall transmit the suspicious transaction report to the FIU. If the obliged entity decides not to make a suspicious transaction report, no report is transmitted. At appropriate times and on a risk-sensitive basis, the obliged entity shall apply the CDD measures to existing customers, including at times when the relevant circumstances of a customer change. The process for the General Obliged Entities lane ends.

The process for the Credit Institutions and Financial Institutions lane starts upon receiving a customer request to open an account, including accounts that permit transactions in transferable securities. The credit institution or financial institution shall assess whether the Member State has granted a derogation allowing account opening before full CDD compliance and whether adequate safeguards are in place to ensure that transactions are not carried out by the customer or on its behalf until full compliance with the CDD requirements laid down in points (a) and (b) of Article 13(1) is obtained. If the derogation has been granted and adequate safeguards are in place, the credit institution or financial institution may open the account for the customer. The credit institution or financial institution shall ensure that no transactions are carried out by the customer or on its behalf until full CDD compliance is obtained. If the derogation has not been granted or adequate safeguards are not in place, the credit institution or financial institution shall complete verification of the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner before opening the account. The process for the Credit Institutions and Financial Institutions lane ends.

The process for the Legal Professionals lane starts when a notary, other independent legal professional, auditor, external accountant, or tax advisor is unable to comply with the CDD requirements laid down in points (a), (b), or (c) of Article 13(1). The legal professional shall assess whether the activity involves ascertaining the legal position of the client or performing the task of defending or representing the client in or concerning judicial proceedings, including providing advice on instituting or avoiding such proceedings. If the activity involves ascertaining the legal position or defending or representing the client in or concerning judicial proceedings, the prohibition on carrying out transactions, establishing business relationships, and the requirement to terminate the business relationship shall not apply to the legal professional. If the activity does not involve ascertaining the legal position or defending or representing the client in judicial proceedings, the legal professional shall not carry out a transaction through a bank account. The legal professional shall not establish a business relationship or carry out the transaction. The legal professional shall terminate the business relationship. The legal professional shall consider making a suspicious transaction report to the FIU in relation to the customer in accordance with Article 33. The process for the Legal Professionals lane ends.

The process for the FIU starts upon receiving a suspicious transaction report from an obliged entity in accordance with Article 33. The FIU shall receive the suspicious transaction report. The process for the FIU ends with the suspicious transaction report received end event.
