The process contains the following actors: Member States, Applicant or Holder of a Driving Licence, The Commission.

The process starts with an applicant applying for the issuance or renewal of a driving licence.

The process for the Member States starts. Member States shall assess whether the application concerns the first issuance or the renewal of a driving licence.

If the application concerns the first issuance of a driving licence, Member States shall assess whether the application concerns category AM. If the application concerns category AM, Member States shall assess whether a medical examination is required for category AM in accordance with Article 10(1), point (b). If a medical examination is required, Member States shall ensure the applicant undergoes a medical examination in which the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness covering all medical conditions set out in Annex III are applied. If a medical examination is not required for category AM, no medical examination obligation applies for the first issuance of category AM. If the application concerns a category other than AM, Member States shall ensure the applicant undergoes a medical examination in which the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness covering all medical conditions set out in Annex III are applied. A medical examination shall be required in respect of applications for driving licences of categories C, CE, C1, C1E, D, D1, DE, or D1E, regardless of whether a medical examination was undertaken for another category.

If the application concerns the renewal of a driving licence, Member States shall assess whether the renewal concerns category AM. If the renewal concerns category AM, Member States shall assess whether a medical examination is required for category AM in accordance with Article 10(1), point (b). If a medical examination is required, Member States shall ensure the applicant undergoes a medical examination covering the medical conditions set out in Annex III. If a medical examination is not required for category AM, no medical examination obligation applies for the renewal of category AM. If the renewal concerns a category other than AM, Member States shall ensure the applicant undergoes a medical examination covering the medical conditions set out in Annex III.

Notwithstanding the above medical examination requirements, and to the extent not otherwise provided for by Annex III such as in the case of the appropriate assessment of eyesight in accordance with Annex III, point 3, Member States shall assess whether the application or renewal concerns categories AM, A, A1, A2, B, B1, or BE. If the application or renewal concerns one of those categories, Member States may, instead of requiring a medical examination, apply one or both of the following two alternative measures: (a) Member States may require the applicant or holder of the driving licence to fill in a self-assessment form covering the medical conditions set out in Annex III when applying for the issuance or renewal of a driving licence. (b) Member States may establish a national system of assessment of fitness to drive to ensure that significant changes in physical or mental fitness are reacted to in order to comply with the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness set out in Annex III, after the driving licence has been issued to the applicant following a medical examination or self-assessment. If the application or renewal does not concern one of those categories, the medical examination requirement under paragraphs 1 or 2 applies.

Member States may provide for appropriate measures to address non-compliance with the requirement to fill in a self-assessment form. Member States may provide for appropriate measures to address knowingly providing information in the self-assessment form that is incorrect or incomplete. Member States may provide for appropriate measures to address failing to meet any requirement established in accordance with the national system of assessment of fitness to drive.

Member States may apply the national system of assessment of fitness to drive in such a way that it allows the monitoring of fitness to drive during periods of administrative validity.

Member States shall assess whether, on the basis of information acquired pursuant to the alternative measures set out above, it becomes apparent that the applicant or holder of a driving licence is likely to have one or more of the medical conditions listed in Annex III. If it becomes apparent that the applicant or holder is likely to have one or more of those medical conditions, Member States shall ensure that the applicant or holder of the driving licence undergoes a medical examination prior to issuing or renewing the driving licence. If it does not become apparent, Member States shall proceed with the issuance or renewal on the basis of the alternative measures.

Member States may take measures to raise awareness and improve the knowledge of the health sector and of driving licence holders about the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness for driving set out in Annex III.

Member States shall assess whether they adopt guidelines for medical practitioners to help identify driving licence holders who no longer meet the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness to drive. If Member States adopt such guidelines, Member States shall inform the Commission thereof.

Member States shall assess whether they develop public awareness campaigns to inform citizens about mental or physical health conditions that may impair fitness to drive. If Member States develop such campaigns, Member States shall inform the Commission thereof.

Member States may set the standards for the issuance or any subsequent renewal of driving licences stricter than those set out in Annex III.

The process for the Member States ends.

The process for the Applicant or Holder of a Driving Licence starts. The Applicant shall submit an application for the issuance or renewal of a driving licence to the Member State. Upon being required by the Member State to undergo a medical examination, the Applicant shall undergo a medical examination in which the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness covering all medical conditions set out in Annex III are applied. If the Member State applies the alternative measure of a self-assessment form instead of a medical examination, the Applicant or Holder shall fill in a self-assessment form covering the medical conditions set out in Annex III. If, on the basis of information acquired from the alternative measures, it becomes apparent that the Applicant or Holder is likely to have one or more of the medical conditions listed in Annex III, the Applicant or Holder shall undergo a medical examination prior to the issuance or renewal of the driving licence. The process for the Applicant or Holder of a Driving Licence ends.

The process for the Commission starts. Upon receiving information from a Member State that it has adopted guidelines for medical practitioners to help identify driving licence holders who no longer meet the minimum standards of physical and mental fitness to drive, the Commission shall make the guidelines available to the other Member States. Upon receiving information from a Member State that it has developed public awareness campaigns to inform citizens about mental or physical health conditions that may impair fitness to drive, the Commission shall make the information available to the other Member States. The process for the Commission ends.
