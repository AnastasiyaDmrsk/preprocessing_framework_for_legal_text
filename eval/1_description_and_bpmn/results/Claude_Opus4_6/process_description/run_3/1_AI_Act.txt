The process contains the following actors: Member States, the Board, the AI Office.

The process starts with the application of the Regulation, simultaneously triggering the processes for Member States, the Board, and the AI Office.

The process for Member States starts. Member States shall carry out the following 4 activities in parallel: (1) Member States shall provide SMEs, including start-ups, having a registered office or a branch in the Union, with priority access to the AI regulatory sandboxes, to the extent that they fulfil the eligibility conditions and selection criteria. Priority access shall not preclude other SMEs, including start-ups, from access to the AI regulatory sandbox, provided that they also fulfil the eligibility conditions and selection criteria. (2) Member States shall organise specific awareness raising and training activities on the application of this Regulation tailored to the needs of SMEs including start-ups, deployers and, as appropriate, local public authorities. (3) Member States shall utilise existing dedicated channels and, where appropriate, establish new ones for communication with SMEs including start-ups, deployers, other innovators and, as appropriate, local public authorities to provide advice and respond to queries about the implementation of this Regulation, including as regards participation in AI regulatory sandboxes. (4) Member States shall facilitate the participation of SMEs and other relevant stakeholders in the standardisation development process. All 4 activities shall be completed before proceeding. Member States shall take into account the specific interests and needs of SME providers, including start-ups, when setting the fees for conformity assessment under Article 43, reducing those fees proportionately to their size, market size and other relevant indicators. The process for Member States ends.

The process for the Board starts. The Board shall send a request to the AI Office specifying the areas covered by this Regulation for which standardised templates shall be provided. The process for the Board ends.

The process for the AI Office starts. The AI Office shall carry out the following 4 activities in parallel: (1) Upon receiving the request from the Board, the AI Office shall provide standardised templates for areas covered by this Regulation, as specified by the Board in its request. (2) The AI Office shall develop and maintain a single information platform providing easy-to-use information in relation to this Regulation for all operators across the Union. (3) The AI Office shall organise appropriate communication campaigns to raise awareness about the obligations arising from this Regulation. (4) The AI Office shall evaluate and promote the convergence of best practices in public procurement procedures in relation to AI systems. All 4 activities shall be completed. The process for the AI Office ends.
