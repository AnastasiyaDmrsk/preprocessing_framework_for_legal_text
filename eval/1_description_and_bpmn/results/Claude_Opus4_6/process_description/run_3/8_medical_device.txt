The process contains the following actors: Manufacturers, The European Commission, Competent Authorities, Distributors, Authorised Representative, Importers, Notified Body, Natural or Legal Persons.

The process starts with Manufacturers placing their devices on the market or putting them into service.

The process for the Manufacturers starts.

Manufacturers shall ensure that devices have been designed and manufactured in accordance with the requirements of this Regulation. Manufacturers shall establish, document, implement and maintain a system for risk management as described in Section 3 of Annex I. Manufacturers shall conduct a clinical evaluation in accordance with the requirements set out in Article 61 and Annex XIV, including a PMCF.

Manufacturers shall assess whether the device is a custom-made device. If the device is not a custom-made device, Manufacturers shall draw up and keep up to date technical documentation for those devices. The technical documentation shall be such as to allow the conformity of the device with the requirements of this Regulation to be assessed. The technical documentation shall include the elements set out in Annexes II and III. If the device is a custom-made device, Manufacturers shall draw up, keep up to date and keep available for Competent Authorities documentation in accordance with Section 2 of Annex XIII.

Manufacturers shall assess whether the device is other than a custom-made or investigational device and whether compliance with the applicable requirements has been demonstrated following the applicable conformity assessment procedure. If both conditions are met, Manufacturers shall draw up an EU declaration of conformity in accordance with Article 19. Manufacturers shall affix the CE marking of conformity in accordance with Article 20. If either condition is not met, Manufacturers shall proceed without drawing up an EU declaration of conformity or affixing the CE marking.

Manufacturers shall comply with the obligations relating to the UDI system referred to in Article 27. Manufacturers shall comply with the registration obligations referred to in Articles 29 and 31.

Manufacturers shall keep the technical documentation, the EU declaration of conformity and, if applicable, a copy of any relevant certificate, including any amendments and supplements, issued in accordance with Article 56, available for the Competent Authorities. Manufacturers shall assess whether the device is an implantable device. If the device is an implantable device, Manufacturers shall keep documentation available for a period of at least 15 years after the last device has been placed on the market. If the device is not an implantable device, Manufacturers shall keep documentation available for a period of at least 10 years after the last device covered by the EU declaration of conformity has been placed on the market.

Manufacturers shall assess whether their registered place of business is outside the Union. If the registered place of business is outside the Union, Manufacturers shall ensure that the Authorised Representative has the necessary documentation permanently available, in order to allow the Authorised Representative to fulfil the tasks mentioned in Article 11(3). If the registered place of business is within the Union, Manufacturers shall proceed without this obligation.

Manufacturers shall ensure that procedures are in place to keep series production in conformity with the requirements of this Regulation. Manufacturers shall adequately take into account in a timely manner changes in device design or characteristics and changes in the harmonised standards or CS by reference to which the conformity of a device is declared.

Manufacturers shall assess whether the device is other than an investigational device. If the device is other than an investigational device, Manufacturers shall establish, document, implement, maintain, keep up to date and continually improve a quality management system that shall ensure compliance with this Regulation in the most effective manner and in a manner that is proportionate to the risk class and the type of device. The quality management system shall cover all parts and elements of a manufacturer's organisation dealing with the quality of processes, procedures and devices. The quality management system shall govern the structure, responsibilities, procedures, processes and management resources required to implement the principles and actions necessary to achieve compliance with the provisions of this Regulation. The quality management system shall address at least the following 13 aspects in parallel: (a) a strategy for regulatory compliance, including compliance with conformity assessment procedures and procedures for management of modifications to the devices covered by the system; (b) identification of applicable general safety and performance requirements and exploration of options to address those requirements; (c) responsibility of the management; (d) resource management, including selection and control of suppliers and sub-contractors; (e) risk management as set out in Section 3 of Annex I; (f) clinical evaluation in accordance with Article 61 and Annex XIV, including PMCF; (g) product realisation, including planning, design, development, production and service provision; (h) verification of the UDI assignments made in accordance with Article 27(3) to all relevant devices and ensuring consistency and validity of information provided in accordance with Article 29; (i) setting-up, implementation and maintenance of a post-market surveillance system, in accordance with Article 83; (j) handling communication with Competent Authorities, Notified Bodies, other economic operators, customers and/or other stakeholders; (k) processes for reporting of serious incidents and field safety corrective actions in the context of vigilance; (l) management of corrective and preventive actions and verification of their effectiveness; (m) processes for monitoring and measurement of output, data analysis and product improvement. All 13 aspects shall be addressed before the quality management system is considered compliant. If the device is an investigational device, Manufacturers shall proceed without establishing a quality management system under this provision.

Manufacturers shall implement and keep up to date the post-market surveillance system in accordance with Article 83. Manufacturers shall ensure that the device is accompanied by the information set out in Section 23 of Annex I in an official Union language(s) determined by the Member State in which the device is made available to the user or patient. Manufacturers shall ensure that the particulars on the label are indelible, easily legible and clearly comprehensible to the intended user or patient.

Manufacturers shall assess whether they consider or have reason to believe that a device which they have placed on the market or put into service is not in conformity with this Regulation. If the device is not in conformity, Manufacturers shall immediately take the necessary corrective action to bring that device into conformity, to withdraw it or to recall it, as appropriate. Manufacturers shall inform the Distributors of the device in question accordingly. Manufacturers shall inform the Authorised Representative accordingly, where applicable. Manufacturers shall inform the Importers accordingly, where applicable. Manufacturers shall assess whether the device presents a serious risk. If the device presents a serious risk, Manufacturers shall immediately inform the Competent Authorities of the Member States in which they made the device available of the non-compliance and of any corrective action taken. Manufacturers shall immediately inform the Notified Body that issued a certificate for the device in accordance with Article 56, where applicable, of the non-compliance and of any corrective action taken. If the device does not present a serious risk, Manufacturers shall proceed without informing Competent Authorities or the Notified Body under this provision. If the device is in conformity, Manufacturers shall proceed without corrective action under this provision.

Manufacturers shall have a system for recording and reporting of incidents and field safety corrective actions as described in Articles 87 and 88.

Upon request by a Competent Authority, Manufacturers shall provide the Competent Authority with all the information and documentation necessary to demonstrate the conformity of the device, in an official Union language determined by the Member State concerned. Manufacturers shall cooperate with a Competent Authority, at its request, on any corrective action taken to eliminate or, if that is not possible, mitigate the risks posed by devices which they have placed on the market or put into service.

Manufacturers shall assess whether they have their devices designed or manufactured by another legal or natural person. If another person designs or manufactures the device, Manufacturers shall include information on the identity of that person as part of the information to be submitted in accordance with Article 30(1). If no other person is involved, Manufacturers shall proceed without this obligation.

Manufacturers shall have measures in place to provide sufficient financial coverage in respect of their potential liability under Directive 85/374/EEC, in a manner that is proportionate to the risk class, type of device and the size of the enterprise, without prejudice to more protective measures under national law.

The process for the Manufacturers ends.

The process for The European Commission starts.

The European Commission is empowered to adopt delegated acts in accordance with Article 115 amending, in the light of technical progress, the Annexes II and III.

The process for The European Commission ends.

The process for the Competent Authorities starts.

Upon request by the Competent Authority, the Manufacturer shall provide the technical documentation in its entirety or a summary thereof, as indicated by the Competent Authority. The Competent Authority of the Member State in which the Manufacturer has its registered place of business may require that the Manufacturer provide samples of the device free of charge or, where that is impracticable, grant access to the device.

The Competent Authority shall assess whether the Manufacturer fails to cooperate or whether the information and documentation provided is incomplete or incorrect. If the Manufacturer fails to cooperate or the information and documentation is incomplete or incorrect, the Competent Authority may, in order to ensure the protection of public health and patient safety, take all appropriate measures to prohibit or restrict the device's being made available on its national market, to withdraw the device from that market or to recall it, until the Manufacturer cooperates or provides complete and correct information. If the Manufacturer cooperates and the information and documentation is complete and correct, the Competent Authority shall proceed without taking restrictive measures under this provision.

The Competent Authority shall assess whether it considers or has reason to believe that a device has caused damage. If the Competent Authority considers or has reason to believe that a device has caused damage, the Competent Authority shall, upon request, facilitate the provision of the information and documentation to the potentially injured patient or user and, as appropriate, the patient's or user's successor in title, the patient's or user's health insurance company or other third parties affected by the damage caused to the patient or user, without prejudice to data protection rules and, unless there is an overriding public interest in disclosure, without prejudice to the protection of intellectual property rights. The Competent Authority shall assess whether the disclosure of the information and documentation is ordinarily dealt with in the context of legal proceedings. If the disclosure is ordinarily dealt with in the context of legal proceedings, the Competent Authority need not comply with the obligation to facilitate the provision of information. If the disclosure is not ordinarily dealt with in the context of legal proceedings, the Competent Authority shall comply with the obligation. If the Competent Authority does not consider or have reason to believe that a device has caused damage, the Competent Authority shall proceed without facilitating the provision of information under this provision.

The process for the Competent Authorities ends.

The process for the Distributors starts.

Upon receiving information from Manufacturers that a device is not in conformity with this Regulation, Distributors shall take note of the non-compliance and of any corrective action, withdrawal or recall communicated by Manufacturers.

The process for the Distributors ends.

The process for the Authorised Representative starts.

Upon receiving documentation from Manufacturers whose registered place of business is outside the Union, the Authorised Representative shall have the necessary documentation permanently available to fulfil the tasks mentioned in Article 11(3). Upon receiving information from Manufacturers that a device is not in conformity, where applicable, the Authorised Representative shall take note of the non-compliance and of any corrective action communicated by Manufacturers.

The process for the Authorised Representative ends.

The process for the Importers starts.

Upon receiving information from Manufacturers that a device is not in conformity, where applicable, Importers shall take note of the non-compliance and of any corrective action communicated by Manufacturers.

The process for the Importers ends.

The process for the Notified Body starts.

Upon receiving information from Manufacturers that a device presents a serious risk, where applicable, the Notified Body that issued a certificate for the device in accordance with Article 56 shall take note of the non-compliance and of any corrective action communicated by Manufacturers.

The process for the Notified Body ends.

The process for Natural or Legal Persons starts.

Natural or legal persons may claim compensation for damage caused by a defective device in accordance with applicable Union and national law.

The process for Natural or Legal Persons ends.
