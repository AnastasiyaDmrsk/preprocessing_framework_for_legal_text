The process contains the following actors: Requesting Supervisory Body, Requested Supervisory Body, Member States, Trust Service Providers, Notified Supervisory Body, Supervisory Bodies in Other Member States, ENISA, The Commission, Natural or Legal Person.

The process starts with two independent triggering events: (1) the sending of a justified request for assistance from one supervisory body to another supervisory body, and (2) the obligation of qualified and non-qualified trust service providers to manage risks to the security of their trust services, followed by the occurrence of a breach of security or loss of integrity with significant impact.

The process for the Requesting Supervisory Body starts. The Requesting Supervisory Body shall cooperate with other supervisory bodies with a view to exchanging good practice. The Requesting Supervisory Body shall send a justified request for assistance to the Requested Supervisory Body. The mutual assistance may cover, in particular, information requests and supervisory measures, such as requests to carry out inspections related to the conformity assessment reports as referred to in Articles 20 and 21. The process for the Requesting Supervisory Body ends.

The process for the Requested Supervisory Body starts upon receipt of a justified request from the Requesting Supervisory Body. The Requested Supervisory Body shall assess whether any ground for refusal applies. If the Requested Supervisory Body is not competent to provide the requested assistance, the Requested Supervisory Body may refuse the request. If the requested assistance is not proportionate to supervisory activities of the Requested Supervisory Body carried out in accordance with Article 17, the Requested Supervisory Body may refuse the request. If providing the requested assistance would be incompatible with this Regulation, the Requested Supervisory Body may refuse the request. If no ground for refusal applies, the Requested Supervisory Body shall provide the Requesting Supervisory Body with assistance so that the activities of supervisory bodies can be carried out in a consistent manner. The process for the Requested Supervisory Body ends.

The process for the Member States starts. Where appropriate, the Member States may authorise their respective supervisory bodies to carry out joint investigations in which staff from other Member States' supervisory bodies is involved. The Member States concerned shall agree upon and establish the arrangements and procedures for such joint actions in accordance with their national law. The process for the Member States ends.

The process for the Trust Service Providers starts. The Trust Service Providers shall take appropriate technical and organisational measures to manage the risks posed to the security of the trust services they provide. The Trust Service Providers shall ensure that the level of security is commensurate to the degree of risk, having regard to the latest technological developments. The Trust Service Providers shall take measures to prevent and minimise the impact of security incidents. The Trust Service Providers shall take measures to inform stakeholders of the adverse effects of any such incidents. Without undue delay but in any event within 24 hours after having become aware of a breach of security or loss of integrity that has a significant impact on the trust service provided or on the personal data maintained therein, the Trust Service Providers shall notify the Notified Supervisory Body. The Trust Service Providers shall, where applicable, notify other relevant bodies, such as the competent national body for information security or the data protection authority, of the breach. The Trust Service Providers shall assess whether the breach of security or loss of integrity is likely to adversely affect a natural or legal person to whom the trusted service has been provided. If the breach is likely to adversely affect a natural or legal person, the Trust Service Providers shall notify the Natural or Legal Person of the breach of security or loss of integrity without undue delay. If the breach is not likely to adversely affect a natural or legal person, no notification to the Natural or Legal Person is required. Where the Notified Supervisory Body requires the Trust Service Providers to disclose the breach to the public, the Trust Service Providers shall inform the public of the breach of security or loss of integrity. The process for the Trust Service Providers ends.

The process for the Notified Supervisory Body starts upon receiving the notification of breach of security or loss of integrity from the Trust Service Providers. The Notified Supervisory Body shall assess whether the breach of security or loss of integrity concerns two or more Member States. If the breach concerns two or more Member States, the Notified Supervisory Body shall inform the Supervisory Bodies in Other Member States concerned. The Notified Supervisory Body shall inform ENISA. If the breach does not concern two or more Member States, no cross-border notification is required. The Notified Supervisory Body shall assess whether disclosure of the breach of security or loss of integrity is in the public interest. If disclosure is in the public interest, the Notified Supervisory Body shall inform the public or require the Trust Service Providers to do so. If disclosure is not in the public interest, no public disclosure is required. The Notified Supervisory Body shall provide ENISA once a year with a summary of notifications of breach of security and loss of integrity received from trust service providers. The process for the Notified Supervisory Body ends.

The process for the Supervisory Bodies in Other Member States starts upon receiving information from the Notified Supervisory Body that a breach of security or loss of integrity concerns their Member State. The process for the Supervisory Bodies in Other Member States ends.

The process for ENISA starts upon receiving information from the Notified Supervisory Body regarding a cross-border breach of security or loss of integrity. ENISA shall receive the annual summary of notifications of breach of security and loss of integrity from the Notified Supervisory Body. The process for ENISA ends.

The process for the Commission starts. The Commission may, by means of implementing acts, carry out the following 2 activities in parallel: (1) further specify the measures referred to in paragraph 1 of Article 19; and (2) define the formats and procedures, including deadlines, applicable for the purpose of paragraph 2 of Article 19. Both activities shall be completed before the implementing acts are adopted. The Commission shall adopt those implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure referred to in Article 48(2). The process for the Commission ends.

The process for the Natural or Legal Person starts upon receiving notification of the breach of security or loss of integrity from the Trust Service Providers. The process for the Natural or Legal Person ends.
