The process contains the following actors: Transferring Member State (lane: Health Professional), Applicant and/or Representative, Member State responsible (lane: Health Professional), The Commission.

The process starts with the need to exchange health data before a transfer is carried out, for the sole purpose of the provision of medical care or treatment.

The process for the Transferring Member State starts. The Transferring Member State shall request explicit consent from the Applicant and/or Representative for the transmission of health information to the Member State responsible. The Transferring Member State shall assess whether explicit consent has been obtained from the Applicant and/or Representative. If explicit consent has been obtained, the Transferring Member State shall proceed to transmit the health information. If explicit consent has not been obtained, the Transferring Member State shall assess whether the Applicant is physically or legally incapable of giving his or her consent. If the Applicant is physically or legally incapable and the transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests of the Applicant or of another person, the Transferring Member State shall proceed to transmit the health information. If the Applicant is physically or legally incapable but the transmission is not necessary to protect the vital interests of the Applicant or of another person, the Transferring Member State shall not transmit the health information. If the Applicant is not physically or legally incapable, the lack of consent, including a refusal to consent, shall not constitute an obstacle to the transfer, except that the Transferring Member State shall not transmit the health information. When proceeding to transmit, the Health Professional of the Transferring Member State shall transmit information on any special needs of the person to be transferred to the Health Professional of the Member State responsible, in so far as it is available to the competent authority in accordance with national law, which in specific cases may include information on that person's physical or mental health. The Health Professional of the Transferring Member State shall transfer that information in a common health certificate with the necessary documents attached. The Health Professional of the Transferring Member State shall ensure that the information exchanged is only used for the purposes of provision of medical care or treatment and shall not be further processed. The rules laid down in Article 34(8) to (12) shall apply to the exchange of information. The process for the Transferring Member State ends.

The process for the Applicant and/or Representative starts. Upon receiving the request for explicit consent from the Transferring Member State, the Applicant and/or Representative shall assess whether to grant explicit consent. If the Applicant and/or Representative grants explicit consent, the Applicant and/or Representative shall communicate explicit consent to the Transferring Member State. If the Applicant and/or Representative refuses consent, the Applicant and/or Representative shall communicate refusal to the Transferring Member State. The process for the Applicant and/or Representative ends.

The process for the Member State responsible starts. Upon receiving the health information in the common health certificate from the Health Professional of the Transferring Member State, the Health Professional of the Member State responsible shall ensure that the special needs of the person are adequately addressed, including in particular any essential medical care that may be required. The Health Professional of the Member State responsible shall ensure that the information exchanged is only used for the purposes of provision of medical care or treatment and shall not be further processed. The rules laid down in Article 34(8) to (12) shall apply to the exchange of information. The process for the Member State responsible ends.

The process for The Commission starts. The Commission shall draw up the common health certificate by means of implementing acts. Those implementing acts shall be adopted in accordance with the examination procedure laid down in Article 44(2). The Commission shall adopt uniform conditions and practical arrangements for exchanging the health information by means of implementing acts. Those implementing acts shall be adopted in accordance with the examination procedure laid down in Article 44(2). The process for The Commission ends.
