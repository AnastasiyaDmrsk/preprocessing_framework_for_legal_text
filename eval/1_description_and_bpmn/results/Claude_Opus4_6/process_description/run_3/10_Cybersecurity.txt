The process contains the following actors: the Commission, ENISA (lane: Management Board), the ECCG, the ad hoc working group, relevant stakeholders, and interested parties.

The process starts when the Commission or, in duly justified cases, the ECCG identifies the need for a new or revised European cybersecurity certification scheme and issues a request to ENISA.

The process for the Commission starts. The Commission shall assess whether the request is based on the Union rolling work programme. If the request is based on the Union rolling work programme, the Commission may request ENISA to prepare a candidate scheme or to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme. If the request concerns a scheme not included in the Union rolling work programme and a duly justified case exists, the Commission may request ENISA to prepare a candidate scheme or to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme. The Commission shall update the Union rolling work programme accordingly. The Commission shall transmit the request to ENISA. Upon receiving the candidate scheme from ENISA, the Commission shall assess whether to adopt implementing acts. If the Commission decides to adopt, the Commission may adopt implementing acts providing for a European cybersecurity certification scheme for ICT products, ICT services and ICT processes which meets the requirements set out in Articles 51, 52 and 54. The Commission shall adopt those implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure referred to in Article 66(2). If the Commission decides not to adopt, no implementing acts are issued. If necessary, following ENISA's periodic evaluation, the Commission may request ENISA to start the process of developing a revised candidate scheme in accordance with Article 48 and Article 49. The process for the Commission ends.

The process for the ECCG starts. In duly justified cases, the ECCG may request ENISA to prepare a candidate scheme or to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme which is not included in the Union rolling work programme. The ECCG shall transmit the request to ENISA. Upon receiving a request for assistance from ENISA during the preparation of the candidate scheme, the ECCG shall provide ENISA with assistance and expert advice in relation to the preparation of the candidate scheme. The ECCG shall adopt an opinion on the candidate scheme. The ECCG shall transmit the opinion to ENISA. If necessary, following ENISA's periodic evaluation, the ECCG may request ENISA to start the process of developing a revised candidate scheme in accordance with Article 48 and Article 49. The process for the ECCG ends.

The process for ENISA starts. Upon receiving a request, ENISA shall assess whether the request originates from the Commission or from the ECCG. If the request originates from the Commission pursuant to Article 48, ENISA shall prepare a candidate scheme which meets the requirements set out in Articles 51, 52 and 54. If the request originates from the ECCG pursuant to Article 48(2), ENISA shall assess whether to accept or refuse the request. If ENISA accepts, ENISA may prepare a candidate scheme which meets the requirements set out in Articles 51, 52 and 54. If ENISA refuses, ENISA shall give reasons for its refusal. The Management Board shall take the decision to refuse such a request. The process for ENISA ends with the refusal end event. When preparing the candidate scheme, ENISA shall carry out the following 3 activities in parallel: (1) ENISA shall consult all relevant stakeholders by means of a formal, open, transparent and inclusive consultation process; (2) ENISA shall establish an ad hoc working group in accordance with Article 20(4) for the purpose of receiving specific advice and expertise; (3) ENISA shall closely cooperate with the ECCG and receive assistance and expert advice from the ECCG in relation to the preparation of the candidate scheme. All 3 activities shall be completed before ENISA proceeds to account for the ECCG opinion. ENISA shall take utmost account of the opinion of the ECCG, except where the opinion of the ECCG shall not bind ENISA, nor shall the absence of such an opinion prevent ENISA from transmitting the candidate scheme to the Commission. ENISA shall transmit the candidate scheme to the Commission. At least every five years, ENISA shall evaluate each adopted European cybersecurity certification scheme, taking into account the feedback received from interested parties. The process for ENISA ends.

The process for the ad hoc working group starts. Upon establishment by ENISA in accordance with Article 20(4), the ad hoc working group shall provide ENISA with specific advice and expertise relating to the preparation of the candidate scheme. The process for the ad hoc working group ends.

The process for relevant stakeholders starts. Upon invitation by ENISA through a formal, open, transparent and inclusive consultation process, relevant stakeholders shall participate in the consultation on the candidate scheme. The process for relevant stakeholders ends.

The process for interested parties starts. Upon ENISA's periodic evaluation of each adopted European cybersecurity certification scheme, interested parties shall provide feedback to ENISA. The process for interested parties ends.
