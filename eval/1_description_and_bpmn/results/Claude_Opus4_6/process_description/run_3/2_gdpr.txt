The process contains the following actors: Processor, Controller, Supervisory Authority, Data Subject.

The process starts with the occurrence of a personal data breach.

The process for the Processor starts. The Processor shall become aware of a personal data breach. The Processor shall notify the Controller of the personal data breach without undue delay after becoming aware of it. The process for the Processor ends.

The process for the Controller starts. Upon receiving the notification from the Processor, or upon becoming aware of a personal data breach through other means, the Controller shall assess whether the personal data breach is unlikely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons. If the personal data breach is unlikely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons, the Controller shall not notify the personal data breach to the Supervisory Authority. If the personal data breach is likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons, the Controller shall notify the personal data breach to the Supervisory Authority without undue delay and, where feasible, not later than 72 hours after having become aware of it. The Controller shall assess whether the notification to the Supervisory Authority is made within 72 hours. If the notification is made within 72 hours, the Controller shall transmit the notification to the Supervisory Authority. If the notification is not made within 72 hours, the Controller shall accompany the notification by reasons for the delay and shall transmit the notification to the Supervisory Authority. The notification shall contain at least the following 4 elements provided in parallel: (1) describe the nature of the personal data breach including, where possible, the categories and approximate number of data subjects concerned and the categories and approximate number of personal data records concerned; (2) communicate the name and contact details of the data protection officer or other contact point where more information can be obtained; (3) describe the likely consequences of the personal data breach; (4) describe the measures taken or proposed to be taken by the Controller to address the personal data breach, including, where appropriate, measures to mitigate its possible adverse effects. All 4 elements shall be completed before the notification is considered complete. Where it is not possible to provide the information at the same time, the Controller may provide the information in phases without undue further delay. The Controller shall document any personal data breaches, comprising the facts relating to the personal data breach, its effects and the remedial action taken. The Controller shall ensure that the documentation enables the Supervisory Authority to verify compliance with this Article. The Controller shall assess whether the personal data breach is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons. If the personal data breach is not likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons, the Controller shall not communicate the personal data breach to the Data Subject. If the personal data breach is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons, the Controller shall assess whether any of the following exemption conditions are met: (a) the Controller has implemented appropriate technical and organisational protection measures, and those measures were applied to the personal data affected by the personal data breach, in particular those that render the personal data unintelligible to any person who is not authorised to access it, such as encryption; (b) the Controller has taken subsequent measures which ensure that the high risk to the rights and freedoms of data subjects is no longer likely to materialise; (c) the communication would involve disproportionate effort. If none of the exemption conditions are met, the Controller shall communicate the personal data breach to the Data Subject without undue delay. The communication to the Data Subject shall describe in clear and plain language the nature of the personal data breach and shall contain at least the information and measures referred to in elements (2), (3) and (4) of the notification content. If exemption condition (a) is met, the Controller shall not communicate the personal data breach to the Data Subject. If exemption condition (b) is met, the Controller shall not communicate the personal data breach to the Data Subject. If exemption condition (c) is met, the Controller shall make a public communication or similar measure whereby the data subjects are informed in an equally effective manner instead of communicating to the Data Subject directly. The process for the Controller ends.

The process for the Supervisory Authority starts. Upon receiving the notification of the personal data breach from the Controller, the Supervisory Authority shall receive and process the notification. The Supervisory Authority may verify compliance with Article 33 using the documentation provided by the Controller. The Supervisory Authority shall assess whether the Controller has already communicated the personal data breach to the Data Subject. If the Controller has already communicated the personal data breach to the Data Subject, the Supervisory Authority shall take no further action regarding communication to the Data Subject. If the Controller has not already communicated the personal data breach to the Data Subject, the Supervisory Authority shall consider the likelihood of the personal data breach resulting in a high risk. The Supervisory Authority may require the Controller to communicate the personal data breach to the Data Subject. The Supervisory Authority may decide that any of the exemption conditions referred to in Article 34(3) are met. The process for the Supervisory Authority ends.

The process for the Data Subject starts. Upon receiving the communication of the personal data breach from the Controller, either directly or through a public communication or similar measure, the Data Subject shall receive the information about the nature of the personal data breach, the name and contact details of the data protection officer or other contact point, the likely consequences of the personal data breach, and the measures taken or proposed to be taken to address the personal data breach. The process for the Data Subject ends.
