The process contains the following actors: Member States, Obliged Entities (with 3 lanes: General Obliged Entities, Credit Institutions and Financial Institutions, Notaries and Other Specified Professionals), Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU).

The process starts with an obliged entity identifying the need to establish a business relationship with a customer or to carry out a transaction.

The process for the Member States starts. Member States shall require that obliged entities verify the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner before the establishment of a business relationship or the carrying out of the transaction. Member States shall assess whether a derogation from the pre-verification requirement is warranted under paragraph 2. If it is necessary not to interrupt the normal conduct of business and where there is little risk of money laundering or terrorist financing, Member States may allow verification of the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner to be completed during the establishment of the business relationship. If those conditions are not met, Member States shall maintain the pre-verification requirement. Member States shall assess whether a derogation for account opening under paragraph 3 is warranted. If adequate safeguards are in place to ensure that no transactions are carried out by the customer or on its behalf until full compliance with customer due diligence requirements under Article 13(1)(a) and (b) is obtained, Member States may allow the opening of an account with a credit institution or financial institution, including accounts that permit transactions in transferable securities. If adequate safeguards are not in place, Member States shall not allow the account opening under derogation. Member States shall require that, where an obliged entity is unable to comply with the customer due diligence requirements under Article 13(1)(a), (b) or (c), the obliged entity shall not carry out a transaction through a bank account, shall not establish a business relationship, shall not carry out the transaction, shall terminate the business relationship, and shall consider making a suspicious transaction report to the FIU in accordance with Article 33. Member States shall assess whether the obliged entity is a notary, other independent legal professional, auditor, external accountant, or tax advisor ascertaining the legal position of their client or performing the task of defending or representing that client in or concerning judicial proceedings, including providing advice on instituting or avoiding such proceedings. If the obliged entity falls within that category, Member States shall not apply the non-execution and termination requirement under paragraph 4 to those persons to the strict extent of that activity. If the obliged entity does not fall within that category, Member States shall apply the non-execution and termination requirement. Member States shall require that obliged entities apply customer due diligence measures not only to all new customers but also at appropriate times to existing customers on a risk-sensitive basis, including at times when the relevant circumstances of a customer change. The process for the Member States ends.

The process for the Obliged Entities starts. The General Obliged Entities lane shall verify the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner before the establishment of the business relationship or the carrying out of the transaction. The General Obliged Entities lane shall assess whether verification during the establishment of the business relationship has been allowed by the Member State under the paragraph 2 derogation. If the Member State has allowed such derogation and there is little risk of money laundering or terrorist financing, the General Obliged Entities lane shall complete verification of the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner as soon as practicable after initial contact. If the Member State has not allowed such derogation, the General Obliged Entities lane shall complete verification before the establishment of the business relationship or the carrying out of the transaction. The General Obliged Entities lane shall assess whether it is able to comply with the customer due diligence requirements under Article 13(1)(a), (b) or (c). If the General Obliged Entities lane is able to comply, the General Obliged Entities lane shall proceed with the business relationship or the transaction. If the General Obliged Entities lane is unable to comply, the General Obliged Entities lane shall carry out the following 4 activities in parallel: (1) not carry out a transaction through a bank account, (2) not establish a business relationship or not carry out the transaction, (3) terminate the business relationship, (4) consider making a suspicious transaction report to the FIU in accordance with Article 33. All 4 activities shall be completed before the process continues. The General Obliged Entities lane shall transmit the suspicious transaction report to the FIU, if the obliged entity decides to make such a report. The General Obliged Entities lane shall apply customer due diligence measures at appropriate times to existing customers on a risk-sensitive basis, including at times when the relevant circumstances of a customer change.

The Credit Institutions and Financial Institutions lane shall assess whether the Member State has allowed the opening of an account under the paragraph 3 derogation. If the Member State has allowed the derogation, the Credit Institutions and Financial Institutions lane may open an account, including accounts that permit transactions in transferable securities, provided that adequate safeguards are in place. The Credit Institutions and Financial Institutions lane shall ensure that no transactions are carried out by the customer or on its behalf until full compliance with customer due diligence requirements under Article 13(1)(a) and (b) is obtained. If the Member State has not allowed the derogation, the Credit Institutions and Financial Institutions lane shall not open an account until full compliance with customer due diligence requirements is obtained.

The Notaries and Other Specified Professionals lane shall assess whether the activity concerns ascertaining the legal position of a client or performing the task of defending or representing that client in or concerning judicial proceedings, including providing advice on instituting or avoiding such proceedings. If the activity falls within that scope, the Notaries and Other Specified Professionals lane shall not be subject to the non-execution and termination requirement under paragraph 4, to the strict extent of that activity. If the activity does not fall within that scope, the Notaries and Other Specified Professionals lane shall apply the non-execution and termination requirement as applicable to the General Obliged Entities lane. The process for the Obliged Entities ends.

The process for the FIU starts. Upon receiving a suspicious transaction report from an obliged entity in accordance with Article 33, the FIU shall process the suspicious transaction report. The process for the FIU ends with a suspicious transaction report received end event.
