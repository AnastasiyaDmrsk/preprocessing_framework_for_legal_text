The process contains the following actors: Applicant, Digital Services Coordinator, Providers of Online Platforms, Trusted Flagger, The Commission, The Board.

The process starts with the Applicant submitting an application for the status of trusted flagger to the Digital Services Coordinator of the Member State in which the Applicant is established.

The process for the Applicant starts. The Applicant shall submit an application for the status of trusted flagger to the Digital Services Coordinator. The Applicant shall demonstrate that it meets all of the following three conditions in parallel: (1) it has particular expertise and competence for the purposes of detecting, identifying and notifying illegal content, (2) it is independent from any provider of online platforms, and (3) it carries out its activities for the purposes of submitting notices diligently, accurately and objectively. All three conditions shall be demonstrated before the Digital Services Coordinator assesses the application. The process for the Applicant ends.

The process for the Digital Services Coordinator starts. The Digital Services Coordinator shall receive the application from the Applicant. The Digital Services Coordinator shall assess whether the Applicant meets all of the conditions set out in paragraph 2. If the Applicant meets all conditions, the Digital Services Coordinator shall award the status of trusted flagger to the Applicant. If the Applicant does not meet all conditions, the Digital Services Coordinator shall refuse the application. The Digital Services Coordinator shall communicate the name, address and email address of the entity to which it has awarded the status of trusted flagger to the Commission. The Digital Services Coordinator shall communicate the name, address and email address of the entity to which it has awarded the status of trusted flagger to the Board. The Digital Services Coordinator shall receive the annual report from the Trusted Flagger. Upon receiving information from a Provider of Online Platforms indicating that a Trusted Flagger has submitted a significant number of insufficiently precise, inaccurate or inadequately substantiated notices, including the necessary explanations and supporting documents, the Digital Services Coordinator shall assess whether there are legitimate reasons to open an investigation. If the Digital Services Coordinator considers that there are legitimate reasons to open an investigation, the Digital Services Coordinator shall suspend the status of trusted flagger during the period of the investigation. The Digital Services Coordinator shall carry out the investigation without undue delay. The Digital Services Coordinator shall communicate the name, address and email address of the entity whose trusted flagger status it has suspended to the Commission. The Digital Services Coordinator shall communicate the name, address and email address of the entity whose trusted flagger status it has suspended to the Board. The Digital Services Coordinator shall assess whether the entity still meets the conditions set out in paragraph 2, following an investigation either on its own initiative or on the basis of information received from third parties, including the information provided by a Provider of Online Platforms pursuant to paragraph 6. If the Digital Services Coordinator determines that the entity no longer meets the conditions set out in paragraph 2, the Digital Services Coordinator shall afford the entity an opportunity to react to the findings of its investigation and its intention to revoke the entity's status as trusted flagger. The Digital Services Coordinator shall receive the reaction from the Trusted Flagger. The Digital Services Coordinator shall revoke the status of trusted flagger. The Digital Services Coordinator shall communicate the name, address and email address of the entity whose trusted flagger status it has revoked to the Commission. The Digital Services Coordinator shall communicate the name, address and email address of the entity whose trusted flagger status it has revoked to the Board. The process for the Digital Services Coordinator ends.

The process for the Providers of Online Platforms starts. Providers of Online Platforms shall take the necessary technical and organisational measures to ensure that notices submitted by Trusted Flaggers, acting within their designated area of expertise, through the mechanisms referred to in Article 16, are given priority. Providers of Online Platforms shall process and decide upon those prioritised notices without undue delay. Providers of Online Platforms shall assess whether they have information indicating that a Trusted Flagger has submitted a significant number of insufficiently precise, inaccurate or inadequately substantiated notices through the mechanisms referred to in Article 16, including information gathered in connection to the processing of complaints through the internal complaint-handling systems referred to in Article 20(4). If a Provider of Online Platforms has such information, the Provider of Online Platforms shall communicate that information to the Digital Services Coordinator that awarded the status of trusted flagger to the entity concerned, providing the necessary explanations and supporting documents. If a Provider of Online Platforms does not have such information, no further action is required. The process for the Providers of Online Platforms ends.

The process for the Trusted Flagger starts. At least once a year, the Trusted Flagger shall publish an easily comprehensible and detailed report on notices submitted in accordance with Article 16 during the relevant period. The Trusted Flagger shall list in that report at least the number of notices categorised by the following three categories in parallel: (1) the identity of the provider of hosting services, (2) the type of allegedly illegal content notified, and (3) the action taken by the provider. All three categories shall be included before the report is finalised. The Trusted Flagger shall include in the report an explanation of the procedures in place to ensure that the Trusted Flagger retains its independence. The Trusted Flagger shall ensure that the information in the report does not contain personal data. The Trusted Flagger shall send the report to the awarding Digital Services Coordinator. The Trusted Flagger shall make the report publicly available. Upon receiving from the Digital Services Coordinator the findings of an investigation and the intention to revoke the Trusted Flagger's status, the Trusted Flagger shall react to the findings and the intention. The Trusted Flagger shall transmit its reaction to the Digital Services Coordinator. The process for the Trusted Flagger ends.

The process for the Commission starts. Upon receiving the information referred to in paragraph 4 from the Digital Services Coordinator, the Commission shall publish that information in a publicly available database in an easily accessible and machine-readable format. The Commission shall keep the database up to date. The Commission, after consulting the Board, shall assess whether it is necessary to issue guidelines to assist Providers of Online Platforms and Digital Services Coordinators in the application of paragraphs 2, 6 and 7. If the Commission considers it necessary, the Commission shall issue the guidelines. If the Commission does not consider it necessary, no further action is required. The process for the Commission ends.

The process for the Board starts. Upon receiving the information referred to in paragraph 4 from the Digital Services Coordinator, the Board shall take note of the names, addresses and email addresses of the entities that have been awarded, suspended or revoked of the status of trusted flagger. Upon being consulted by the Commission regarding the necessity and content of guidelines, the Board shall provide its input to the Commission. The process for the Board ends.
