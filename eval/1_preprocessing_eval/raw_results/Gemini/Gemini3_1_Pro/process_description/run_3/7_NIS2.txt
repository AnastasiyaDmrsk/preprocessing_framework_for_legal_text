The process contains the following actors: Member States, Essential and important entities, the Competent authority, the CSIRT, the Single point of contact, ENISA, and the Commission.

The process starts with a significant incident, simultaneously triggering subprocesses for the Member States, the Essential and important entities, and the Commission.

The process for the Member States starts.
Member States shall carry out the following 11 activities in parallel:
- Member States shall ensure that essential and important entities notify its CSIRT or its competent authority of any significant incident without undue delay.
- Member States shall ensure that essential and important entities report any information enabling the CSIRT or the competent authority to determine any cross-border impact of the incident.
- Member States shall ensure that the competent authority forwards the notification to the CSIRT upon receipt.
- Member States shall ensure that their single points of contact are provided in due time with relevant information notified in accordance with paragraph 4 in the case of a cross-border or cross-sectoral significant incident.
- Member States shall ensure that essential and important entities communicate to the recipients of their services potentially affected by a significant cyber threat any measures or remedies without undue delay.
- Member States shall ensure that the entities concerned submit an early warning to the CSIRT or the competent authority.
- Member States shall ensure that the entities concerned submit an incident notification.
- Member States shall ensure that the entities concerned submit an intermediate report upon the request of a CSIRT or the competent authority.
- Member States shall ensure that the entities concerned submit a final report not later than one month after the submission of the incident notification.
- Member States shall ensure that entities concerned provide a progress report in the event of an ongoing incident.
- Member States shall ensure that entities concerned provide a final report within one month of their handling of the incident in the event of an ongoing incident.
All 11 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Member States shall ensure that the mere act of notification does not subject the notifying entity to increased liability.
The process for the Member States ends with the reporting obligations established end event.

The process for the Essential and important entities starts.
Essential and important entities shall assess whether an incident has caused or is capable of causing severe operational disruption of the services or financial loss for the entity concerned, or has affected or is capable of affecting other natural or legal persons by causing considerable material or non-material damage.
If the incident has caused such disruption, loss, or damage, Essential and important entities shall consider the incident to be significant.
Essential and important entities shall send a notification of any significant incident to the CSIRT or the competent authority without undue delay.
Essential and important entities shall report any information enabling the CSIRT or the competent authority to determine any cross-border impact of the incident to the CSIRT or the competent authority.
Essential and important entities shall assess whether it is appropriate to notify the recipients of their services of significant incidents.
If it is appropriate, Essential and important entities shall notify the recipients of their services of significant incidents that are likely to adversely affect the provision of those services without undue delay.
Essential and important entities shall communicate any measures or remedies that recipients are able to take in response to a significant cyber threat to the recipients of their services potentially affected without undue delay.
Essential and important entities shall assess whether it is appropriate to inform the recipients of the significant cyber threat itself.
If it is appropriate, Essential and important entities shall inform those recipients of the significant cyber threat itself.
Essential and important entities shall send an early warning to the CSIRT or the competent authority without undue delay and in any event within 24 hours of becoming aware of the significant incident.
Essential and important entities shall indicate whether the significant incident is suspected of being caused by unlawful or malicious acts or could have a cross-border impact in the early warning.
Essential and important entities shall assess whether the entity is a trust service provider.
If the entity is a trust service provider, Essential and important entities shall send an incident notification to the CSIRT or the competent authority regarding significant incidents that have an impact on the provision of its trust services without undue delay and in any event within 24 hours of becoming aware of the significant incident.
If the entity is not a trust service provider, Essential and important entities shall send an incident notification to the CSIRT or the competent authority without undue delay and in any event within 72 hours of becoming aware of the significant incident.
Essential and important entities shall carry out the following 4 activities in parallel:
- Essential and important entities shall update the information referred to in point (a) in the incident notification.
- Essential and important entities shall indicate an initial assessment of the significant incident in the incident notification.
- Essential and important entities shall indicate the severity and impact of the significant incident in the incident notification.
- Essential and important entities shall indicate the indicators of compromise in the incident notification.
All 4 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Upon receiving a request for an intermediate report from a CSIRT or the competent authority, Essential and important entities shall send an intermediate report on relevant status updates to the CSIRT or the competent authority.
Essential and important entities shall send a final report to the CSIRT or the competent authority not later than one month after the submission of the incident notification.
Essential and important entities shall carry out the following 4 activities in parallel:
- Essential and important entities shall include a detailed description of the incident, including its severity and impact, in the final report.
- Essential and important entities shall include the type of threat or root cause that is likely to have triggered the incident in the final report.
- Essential and important entities shall include applied and ongoing mitigation measures in the final report.
- Essential and important entities shall include the cross-border impact of the incident in the final report.
All 4 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Essential and important entities shall assess whether there is an ongoing incident at the time of the submission of the final report.
If there is an ongoing incident, Essential and important entities shall send a progress report at that time to the CSIRT or the competent authority.
If there is an ongoing incident, Essential and important entities shall send a final report to the CSIRT or the competent authority within one month of their handling of the incident.
Upon receiving a requirement to inform the public from the CSIRT or the competent authority, Essential and important entities shall inform the public about the significant incident.
The process for the Essential and important entities ends with the reporting completed end event.

The process for the Competent authority starts.
Upon receiving the notification of a significant incident from the Essential and important entities, the Competent authority shall send the notification to the CSIRT upon receipt.
Upon receiving the early warning from the Essential and important entities, the Competent authority shall provide a response to the notifying entity without undue delay and where possible within 24 hours.
The Competent authority shall include initial feedback on the significant incident in the response.
Upon receiving a request from the entity, the Competent authority shall provide guidance or operational advice on the implementation of possible mitigation measures to the notifying entity.
The Competent authority shall assess whether the CSIRT is not the initial recipient of the notification.
If the CSIRT is not the initial recipient of the notification, the Competent authority shall provide the guidance in cooperation with the CSIRT.
The Competent authority shall assess whether the significant incident is suspected to be of criminal nature.
If the significant incident is suspected to be of criminal nature, the Competent authority shall provide guidance on reporting the significant incident to law enforcement authorities.
The Competent authority shall assess whether the significant incident concerns two or more Member States or it is otherwise appropriate to inform others.
If it is appropriate, the Competent authority shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
- The Competent authority shall inform the other affected Member States of the significant incident without undue delay.
- The Competent authority shall inform ENISA of the significant incident without undue delay.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
The Competent authority shall include the type of information received in accordance with paragraph 4 in the information.
The Competent authority shall preserve the entity’s security and commercial interests as well as the confidentiality of the information provided.
The Competent authority shall assess whether public awareness is necessary to prevent a significant incident or to deal with an ongoing significant incident, or whether disclosure of the significant incident is otherwise in the public interest.
If public awareness is necessary or disclosure is in the public interest, the Competent authority may consult the entity concerned.
If public awareness is necessary or disclosure is in the public interest, the Competent authority may inform the public about the significant incident.
If public awareness is necessary or disclosure is in the public interest, the Competent authority may send a requirement to inform the public to the Essential and important entities.
The Competent authority may send a request for an intermediate report to the Essential and important entities.
The Competent authority may send a request to forward notifications to the Single point of contact.
The Competent authority shall provide information about significant incidents, incidents, cyber threats and near misses to the competent authorities under Directive (EU) 2022/2557.
The process for the Competent authority ends with the incident handled end event.

The process for the CSIRT starts.
Upon receiving the notification from the Competent authority or the Essential and important entities, the CSIRT shall process the notification.
Upon receiving the early warning from the Essential and important entities, the CSIRT shall provide a response to the notifying entity without undue delay and where possible within 24 hours.
The CSIRT shall include initial feedback on the significant incident in the response.
Upon receiving a request from the entity, the CSIRT shall provide guidance or operational advice on the implementation of possible mitigation measures.
Upon receiving a request from the entity concerned, the CSIRT shall provide additional technical support.
The CSIRT shall assess whether the significant incident is suspected to be of criminal nature.
If the significant incident is suspected to be of criminal nature, the CSIRT shall provide guidance on reporting the significant incident to law enforcement authorities.
The CSIRT shall assess whether the significant incident concerns two or more Member States or it is otherwise appropriate to inform others.
If it is appropriate, the CSIRT shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
- The CSIRT shall inform the other affected Member States of the significant incident without undue delay.
- The CSIRT shall inform ENISA of the significant incident without undue delay.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
The CSIRT shall include the type of information received in accordance with paragraph 4 in the information.
The CSIRT shall preserve the entity’s security and commercial interests as well as the confidentiality of the information provided.
The CSIRT shall assess whether public awareness is necessary to prevent a significant incident or to deal with an ongoing significant incident, or whether disclosure of the significant incident is otherwise in the public interest.
If public awareness is necessary or disclosure is in the public interest, the CSIRT may consult the entity concerned.
If public awareness is necessary or disclosure is in the public interest, the CSIRT may inform the public about the significant incident.
If public awareness is necessary or disclosure is in the public interest, the CSIRT may send a requirement to inform the public to the Essential and important entities.
The CSIRT may send a request for an intermediate report to the Essential and important entities.
The CSIRT may send a request to forward notifications to the Single point of contact.
The CSIRT shall provide information about significant incidents, incidents, cyber threats and near misses to the competent authorities under Directive (EU) 2022/2557.
The process for the CSIRT ends with the incident handled end event.

The process for the Single point of contact starts.
The Single point of contact shall assess whether the significant incident concerns two or more Member States or it is otherwise appropriate to inform others.
If it is appropriate, the Single point of contact shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
- The Single point of contact shall inform the other affected Member States of the significant incident without undue delay.
- The Single point of contact shall inform ENISA of the significant incident without undue delay.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
The Single point of contact shall include the type of information received in accordance with paragraph 4 in the information.
The Single point of contact shall preserve the entity’s security and commercial interests as well as the confidentiality of the information provided.
Upon receiving a request to forward notifications from the CSIRT or the Competent authority, the Single point of contact shall forward notifications received to the single points of contact of other affected Member States.
Every three months, the Single point of contact shall send a summary report to ENISA.
The Single point of contact shall include anonymised and aggregated data on significant incidents, incidents, cyber threats and near misses in the summary report.
The process for the Single point of contact ends with the reporting completed end event.

The process for ENISA starts.
Upon receiving the summary report from the Single point of contact, ENISA may adopt technical guidance on the parameters of the information to be included in the summary report.
Every six months, ENISA shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
- ENISA shall inform the Cooperation Group about its findings on notifications received.
- ENISA shall inform the CSIRTs network about its findings on notifications received.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
The process for ENISA ends with the findings shared end event.

The process for the Commission starts.
The Commission may adopt implementing acts further specifying the type of information, the format and the procedure of a notification and of a communication.
By 17 October 2024, the Commission shall adopt implementing acts further specifying the cases in which an incident shall be considered to be significant with regard to DNS service providers, TLD name registries, cloud computing service providers, data centre service providers, content delivery network providers, managed service providers, managed security service providers, as well as providers of online marketplaces, of online search engines and of social networking services platforms.
The Commission may adopt such implementing acts with regard to other essential and important entities.
The Commission shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
- The Commission shall exchange advice with the Cooperation Group on the draft implementing acts.
- The Commission shall cooperate with the Cooperation Group on the draft implementing acts.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
The Commission shall adopt those implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure referred to in Article 39(2).
The process for the Commission ends with the implementing acts adopted end event.
