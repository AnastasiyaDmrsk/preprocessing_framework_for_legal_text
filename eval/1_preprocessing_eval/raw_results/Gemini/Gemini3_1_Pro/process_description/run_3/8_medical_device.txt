The process contains the following actors: Manufacturers, the Commission, the Competent authority, and Natural or legal persons.

The process starts with placing devices on the market or putting them into service, simultaneously triggering subprocesses for the Manufacturers, the Commission, the Competent authority, and Natural or legal persons.

The process for the Manufacturers starts.
When placing their devices on the market or putting them into service, Manufacturers shall ensure that they have been designed and manufactured in accordance with the requirements of this Regulation.
Manufacturers shall carry out the following 4 activities in parallel:
- Manufacturers shall establish a system for risk management as described in Section 3 of Annex I.
- Manufacturers shall document a system for risk management.
- Manufacturers shall implement a system for risk management.
- Manufacturers shall maintain a system for risk management.
All 4 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Manufacturers shall conduct a clinical evaluation in accordance with the requirements set out in Article 61 and Annex XIV, including a PMCF.
Manufacturers shall assess whether the devices are custom-made devices.
If the devices are not custom-made devices, Manufacturers shall carry out the following 3 activities in parallel:
- Manufacturers shall draw up technical documentation for those devices.
- Manufacturers shall keep up to date technical documentation for those devices.
- Manufacturers shall include the elements set out in Annexes II and III in the technical documentation.
All 3 activities shall be completed before the next step.
If the devices are custom-made devices, Manufacturers shall carry out the following 3 activities in parallel:
- Manufacturers shall draw up documentation in accordance with Section 2 of Annex XIII.
- Manufacturers shall keep up to date documentation in accordance with Section 2 of Annex XIII.
- Manufacturers shall keep available for competent authorities documentation in accordance with Section 2 of Annex XIII.
All 3 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Manufacturers shall assess whether compliance with the applicable requirements has been demonstrated following the applicable conformity assessment procedure and whether the devices are other than custom-made or investigational devices.
If compliance has been demonstrated and the devices are other than custom-made or investigational devices, Manufacturers shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
- Manufacturers shall draw up an EU declaration of conformity in accordance with Article 19.
- Manufacturers shall affix the CE marking of conformity in accordance with Article 20.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Manufacturers shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
- Manufacturers shall comply with the obligations relating to the UDI system referred to in Article 27.
- Manufacturers shall comply with the registration obligations referred to in Articles 29 and 31.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Manufacturers shall assess whether the device is an implantable device.
If the device is not an implantable device, Manufacturers shall keep the technical documentation, the EU declaration of conformity and a copy of any relevant certificate available for the competent authorities for a period of at least 10 years after the last device has been placed on the market.
If the device is an implantable device, Manufacturers shall keep the technical documentation, the EU declaration of conformity and a copy of any relevant certificate available for the competent authorities for a period of at least 15 years after the last device has been placed on the market.
Upon receiving a request for the technical documentation from the Competent authority, Manufacturers shall provide that technical documentation in its entirety or a summary thereof.
Manufacturers shall assess whether they have a registered place of business outside the Union.
If Manufacturers have a registered place of business outside the Union, Manufacturers shall ensure that the authorised representative has the necessary documentation permanently available.
Manufacturers shall ensure that procedures are in place to keep series production in conformity with the requirements of this Regulation.
Manufacturers shall adequately take into account changes in device design or characteristics and changes in the harmonised standards or CS in a timely manner.
Manufacturers shall assess whether the devices are other than investigational devices.
If the devices are other than investigational devices, Manufacturers shall carry out the following 6 activities in parallel:
- Manufacturers shall establish a quality management system.
- Manufacturers shall document a quality management system.
- Manufacturers shall implement a quality management system.
- Manufacturers shall maintain a quality management system.
- Manufacturers shall keep up to date a quality management system.
- Manufacturers shall continually improve a quality management system.
All 6 activities shall be completed before the next step.
If the devices are other than investigational devices, Manufacturers shall cover all parts and elements of a manufacturer's organisation dealing with the quality of processes, procedures and devices in the quality management system.
If the devices are other than investigational devices, Manufacturers shall govern the structure, responsibilities, procedures, processes and management resources required to implement the principles and actions necessary to achieve compliance in the quality management system.
If the devices are other than investigational devices, Manufacturers shall carry out the following 13 activities in parallel:
- Manufacturers shall address a strategy for regulatory compliance in the quality management system.
- Manufacturers shall address identification of applicable general safety and performance requirements in the quality management system.
- Manufacturers shall address responsibility of the management in the quality management system.
- Manufacturers shall address resource management in the quality management system.
- Manufacturers shall address risk management in the quality management system.
- Manufacturers shall address clinical evaluation in the quality management system.
- Manufacturers shall address product realisation in the quality management system.
- Manufacturers shall address verification of the UDI assignments and ensuring consistency and validity of information provided in the quality management system.
- Manufacturers shall address setting-up, implementation and maintenance of a post-market surveillance system in the quality management system.
- Manufacturers shall address handling communication with competent authorities, notified bodies, other economic operators, customers and/or other stakeholders in the quality management system.
- Manufacturers shall address processes for reporting of serious incidents and field safety corrective actions in the context of vigilance in the quality management system.
- Manufacturers shall address management of corrective and preventive actions and verification of their effectiveness in the quality management system.
- Manufacturers shall address processes for monitoring and measurement of output, data analysis and product improvement in the quality management system.
All 13 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Manufacturers shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
- Manufacturers shall implement the post-market surveillance system in accordance with Article 83.
- Manufacturers shall keep up to date the post-market surveillance system in accordance with Article 83.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Manufacturers shall ensure that the device is accompanied by the information set out in Section 23 of Annex I in an official Union language(s) determined by the Member State.
Manufacturers shall ensure that the particulars on the label are indelible, easily legible and clearly comprehensible to the intended user or patient.
Manufacturers shall assess whether they consider or have reason to believe that a device which they have placed on the market or put into service is not in conformity with this Regulation.
If Manufacturers consider or have reason to believe the device is not in conformity, Manufacturers shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
- Manufacturers shall immediately take the necessary corrective action to bring that device into conformity, to withdraw it or to recall it.
- Manufacturers shall inform the distributors of the device in question, the authorised representative and importers accordingly.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Manufacturers shall assess whether the device presents a serious risk.
If the device presents a serious risk, Manufacturers shall immediately inform the competent authorities of the Member States in which they made the device available and the notified body of the non-compliance and of any corrective action taken.
Manufacturers shall have a system for recording and reporting of incidents and field safety corrective actions as described in Articles 87 and 88.
Upon receiving a request to demonstrate the conformity of the device from the Competent authority, Manufacturers shall provide it with all the information and documentation necessary to demonstrate the conformity of the device in an official Union language determined by the Member State concerned.
Upon receiving a requirement to provide samples or grant access from the Competent authority, Manufacturers shall provide samples of the device free of charge or grant access to the device.
Upon receiving a request to cooperate on any corrective action taken from the Competent authority, Manufacturers shall cooperate with a competent authority on any corrective action taken.
Manufacturers shall assess whether they have their devices designed or manufactured by another legal or natural person.
If Manufacturers have their devices designed or manufactured by another legal or natural person, Manufacturers shall include the information on the identity of that person in the information to be submitted in accordance with Article 30(1).
Manufacturers shall have measures in place to provide sufficient financial coverage in respect of their potential liability under Directive 85/374/EEC.
The process for the Manufacturers ends with the obligations fulfilled end event.

The process for the Commission starts.
The Commission may adopt delegated acts in accordance with Article 115 amending the Annexes II and III in the light of technical progress.
The process for the Commission ends with the delegated acts adopted end event.

The process for the Competent authority starts.
The Competent authority may send a request for the technical documentation to the Manufacturers.
The Competent authority may send a request to demonstrate the conformity of the device to the Manufacturers.
The Competent authority may send a requirement to provide samples or grant access to the Manufacturers.
The Competent authority may send a request to cooperate on any corrective action taken to the Manufacturers.
The Competent authority shall assess whether the manufacturer fails to cooperate or the information and documentation provided is incomplete or incorrect.
If the manufacturer fails to cooperate or the information and documentation provided is incomplete or incorrect, the Competent authority may take all appropriate measures to prohibit or restrict the device's being made available on its national market, to withdraw the device from that market or to recall it.
The Competent authority shall assess whether it considers or has reason to believe that a device has caused damage.
If the Competent authority considers or has reason to believe that a device has caused damage, the Competent authority shall assess whether disclosure of the information and documentation is ordinarily dealt with in the context of legal proceedings.
If disclosure is not ordinarily dealt with in the context of legal proceedings, the Competent authority shall facilitate the provision of the information and documentation to the potentially injured patient or user and other affected third parties upon request, except where data protection rules or the protection of intellectual property rights apply without an overriding public interest in disclosure.
The process for the Competent authority ends with the supervision completed end event.

The process for Natural or legal persons starts.
Natural or legal persons may claim compensation for damage caused by a defective device in accordance with applicable Union and national law.
The process for Natural or legal persons ends with the compensation claimed end event.
