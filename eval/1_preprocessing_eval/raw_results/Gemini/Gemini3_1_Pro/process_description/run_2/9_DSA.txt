The process contains the following actors: Entities, the Digital Services Coordinator, Trusted flaggers, Providers of online platforms, the Commission, and the Board.

The process starts with the management of trusted flaggers, simultaneously triggering subprocesses for the Entities, the Digital Services Coordinator, Trusted flaggers, Providers of online platforms, the Commission, and the Board.

The process for the Entities starts.
Entities shall send an application for the status of 'trusted flagger' to the Digital Services Coordinator.
Entities shall carry out the following 3 activities in parallel:
- Entities shall demonstrate that they have particular expertise and competence for the purposes of detecting, identifying and notifying illegal content.
- Entities shall demonstrate that they are independent from any provider of online platforms.
- Entities shall demonstrate that they carry out their activities for the purposes of submitting notices diligently, accurately and objectively.
All 3 activities shall be completed before the next step.
The process for the Entities ends with the application submitted end event.

The process for the Digital Services Coordinator starts.
Upon receiving an application from the Entities, the Digital Services Coordinator shall assess whether the applicant has demonstrated that it meets all of the conditions.
If the applicant has demonstrated that it meets all of the conditions, the Digital Services Coordinator shall award the status of 'trusted flagger' to the applicant.
Upon receiving information indicating that a trusted flagger has submitted a significant number of insufficiently precise, inaccurate or inadequately substantiated notices from the Providers of online platforms, the Digital Services Coordinator shall assess whether there are legitimate reasons to open an investigation.
If it considers that there are legitimate reasons to open an investigation, the Digital Services Coordinator shall suspend the status of trusted flagger during the period of the investigation.
If it considers that there are legitimate reasons to open an investigation, the Digital Services Coordinator shall carry out that investigation without undue delay.
The Digital Services Coordinator shall assess whether it determines, following an investigation either on its own initiative or on the basis of information received from third parties, that the entity no longer meets the conditions set out in paragraph 2.
If it determines that the entity no longer meets the conditions, the Digital Services Coordinator shall send an opportunity to react to the findings of its investigation and its intention to revoke the entity’s status as trusted flagger to the Trusted flaggers.
If it determines that the entity no longer meets the conditions, the Digital Services Coordinator shall revoke that status.
The Digital Services Coordinator shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
- The Digital Services Coordinator shall send the names, addresses and email addresses of the entities to which they have awarded the status of the trusted flagger, suspended, or revoked to the Commission.
- The Digital Services Coordinator shall send the names, addresses and email addresses of the entities to which they have awarded the status of the trusted flagger, suspended, or revoked to the Board.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Upon receiving the reports from the Trusted flaggers, the Digital Services Coordinator shall process the reports.
The process for the Digital Services Coordinator ends with the status managed end event.

The process for the Trusted flaggers starts.
At least once a year, Trusted flaggers shall publish easily comprehensible and detailed reports on notices submitted in accordance with Article 16 during the relevant period.
Trusted flaggers shall carry out the following 3 activities in parallel:
- Trusted flaggers shall list at least the number of notices categorised by the identity of the provider of hosting services, the type of allegedly illegal content notified, and the action taken by the provider in the reports.
- Trusted flaggers shall include an explanation of the procedures in place to ensure that the trusted flagger retains its independence in the reports.
- Trusted flaggers shall ensure that the information in those reports does not contain personal data.
All 3 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Trusted flaggers shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
- Trusted flaggers shall send those reports to the Digital Services Coordinator.
- Trusted flaggers shall make those reports publicly available.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Upon receiving an opportunity to react to the findings of its investigation and its intention to revoke the entity’s status as trusted flagger from the Digital Services Coordinator, Trusted flaggers shall react to the findings and intention.
The process for the Trusted flaggers ends with the duties fulfilled end event.

The process for the Providers of online platforms starts.
Providers of online platforms shall take the necessary technical and organisational measures to ensure that notices submitted by trusted flaggers are given priority and are processed and decided upon without undue delay.
Providers of online platforms shall assess whether they have information indicating that a trusted flagger has submitted a significant number of insufficiently precise, inaccurate or inadequately substantiated notices.
If they have such information, Providers of online platforms shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
- Providers of online platforms shall send that information to the Digital Services Coordinator that awarded the status of trusted flagger.
- Providers of online platforms shall send the necessary explanations and supporting documents to the Digital Services Coordinator.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
The process for the Providers of online platforms ends with the notices processed end event.

The process for the Commission starts.
Upon receiving the names, addresses and email addresses of the entities from the Digital Services Coordinator, the Commission shall publish the information in a publicly available database, in an easily accessible and machine-readable format.
The Commission shall keep the database up to date.
The Commission shall assess whether it is necessary to issue guidelines to assist providers of online platforms and Digital Services Coordinators in the application of paragraphs 2, 6 and 7.
If it is necessary, the Commission shall send a consultation to the Board.
If it is necessary, the Commission shall issue guidelines to assist providers of online platforms and Digital Services Coordinators.
The process for the Commission ends with the guidelines managed end event.

The process for the Board starts.
Upon receiving the names, addresses and email addresses of the entities from the Digital Services Coordinator, the Board shall process the communication.
Upon receiving a consultation from the Commission, the Board shall provide advice.
The process for the Board ends with the consultation completed end event.
