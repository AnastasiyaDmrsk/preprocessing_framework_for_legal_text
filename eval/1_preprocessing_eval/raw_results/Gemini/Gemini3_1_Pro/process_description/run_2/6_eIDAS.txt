The process contains the following actors: Member States, the Commission, Another supervisory body, Trust service providers, and the Supervisory body.

The process starts with the supervision of trust services, simultaneously triggering subprocesses for the Member States, the Commission, Another supervisory body, Trust service providers, and the Supervisory body.

The process for the Member States starts.
Member States shall designate a supervisory body established in their territory or a supervisory body established in that other Member State upon mutual agreement with another Member State.
Member States shall give the necessary powers to the supervisory bodies for the exercise of their tasks.
Member States shall give adequate resources to the supervisory bodies for the exercise of their tasks.
Member States shall notify the names and the addresses of their respective designated supervisory bodies to the Commission.
Member States may require the supervisory body to establish a trust infrastructure in accordance with the conditions under national law.
Member States may require the supervisory body to maintain a trust infrastructure in accordance with the conditions under national law.
Member States may require the supervisory body to update a trust infrastructure in accordance with the conditions under national law.
Member States shall assess whether it is appropriate to carry out joint investigations.
If it is appropriate, Member States may authorise their respective supervisory bodies to carry out joint investigations in which staff from other Member States’ supervisory bodies is involved.
If it is appropriate, Member States shall agree upon the arrangements and procedures for such joint actions in accordance with their national law.
If it is appropriate, Member States shall establish the arrangements and procedures for such joint actions in accordance with their national law.
Upon receiving the annual report from the Commission, Member States shall access the annual report.
The process for the Member States ends with the supervision framework established end event.

The process for the Commission starts.
Upon receiving the names and the addresses of their respective designated supervisory bodies from the Member States, the Commission shall process the notification.
Upon receiving the report on its previous calendar year’s main activities together with a summary of breach notifications from the Supervisory body, the Commission shall make the annual report available to Member States.
The Commission may carry out the following 3 activities in parallel:
- The Commission may define the formats and procedures for the report referred to in paragraph 6 by means of implementing acts.
- The Commission may further specify the measures referred to in paragraph 1 by means of implementing acts.
- The Commission may define the formats and procedures, including deadlines, applicable for the purpose of paragraph 2 by means of implementing acts.
All 3 activities shall be completed before the next step.
The Commission shall adopt those implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure referred to in Article 48(2).
The process for the Commission ends with the implementing acts adopted end event.

The process for Another supervisory body starts.
Another supervisory body shall send a justified request for assistance to the Supervisory body.
Upon receiving assistance from the Supervisory body, Another supervisory body shall carry out the activities in a consistent manner.
The process for Another supervisory body ends with the request completed end event.

The process for the Trust service providers starts.
Trust service providers shall take appropriate technical and organisational measures to manage the risks posed to the security of the trust services they provide.
Having regard to the latest technological developments, Trust service providers shall ensure that the level of security is commensurate to the degree of risk.
Trust service providers shall carry out the following 3 activities in parallel:
- Trust service providers shall take measures to prevent the impact of security incidents.
- Trust service providers shall take measures to minimise the impact of security incidents.
- Trust service providers shall inform stakeholders of the adverse effects of any such incidents.
All 3 activities shall be completed before the next step.
Without undue delay but in any event within 24 hours after having become aware of it, Trust service providers shall notify any breach of security or loss of integrity that has a significant impact on the trust service provided or on the personal data maintained therein to the Supervisory body.
Without undue delay but in any event within 24 hours after having become aware of it, Trust service providers shall notify any breach of security or loss of integrity to other relevant bodies.
Trust service providers shall assess whether the breach of security or loss of integrity is likely to adversely affect a natural or legal person to whom the trusted service has been provided.
If the breach of security or loss of integrity is likely to adversely affect a natural or legal person, Trust service providers shall notify the breach of security or loss of integrity to the natural or legal person without undue delay.
Upon receiving a requirement to inform the public from the Supervisory body, Trust service providers shall inform the public.
Upon receiving a requirement to remedy any failure from the Supervisory body, Trust service providers shall remedy any failure to fulfil the requirements laid down in this Regulation.
The process for the Trust service providers ends with the security measures applied end event.

The process for the Supervisory body starts.
The Supervisory body shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
- The Supervisory body shall supervise qualified trust service providers established in the territory of the designating Member State to ensure that those qualified trust service providers and the qualified trust services that they provide meet the requirements laid down in this Regulation.
- The Supervisory body shall assess whether it is informed that non-qualified trust service providers or the trust services they provide allegedly do not meet the requirements laid down in this Regulation. If it is informed, the Supervisory body shall take action in relation to non-qualified trust service providers through ex post supervisory activities.
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
The Supervisory body shall carry out the following 9 activities in parallel:
- The Supervisory body shall cooperate with other supervisory bodies.
- The Supervisory body shall analyse the conformity assessment reports referred to in Articles 20(1) and 21(1).
- The Supervisory body shall carry out audits of the qualified trust service providers.
- The Supervisory body may send a request to perform a conformity assessment of the qualified trust service providers to a conformity assessment body.
- The Supervisory body shall cooperate with the data protection authorities by informing them without undue delay about the results of audits of qualified trust service providers where personal data protection rules appear to have been breached.
- The Supervisory body shall grant qualified status to trust service providers and to the services they provide.
- The Supervisory body shall withdraw qualified status in accordance with Articles 20 and 21.
- The Supervisory body shall assess whether the body responsible for the national trusted list is also the supervisory body. If the body responsible for the national trusted list is not the supervisory body, the Supervisory body shall notify its decisions to grant or to withdraw qualified status to the body responsible for the national trusted list.
- The Supervisory body shall verify the existence and correct application of provisions on termination plans in cases where the qualified trust service provider ceases its activities.
All 9 activities shall be completed before the next step.
The Supervisory body shall send a requirement to remedy any failure to fulfil the requirements laid down in this Regulation to the Trust service providers.
The Supervisory body shall cooperate with a view to exchanging good practice.
Upon receiving a justified request for assistance from Another supervisory body, the Supervisory body shall assess whether the supervisory body is not competent to provide the requested assistance, whether the requested assistance is not proportionate to supervisory activities, or whether providing the requested assistance would be incompatible with this Regulation.
If the supervisory body is not competent, the requested assistance is not proportionate, or providing the requested assistance would be incompatible, the Supervisory body may refuse that request.
If the request is not refused, the Supervisory body shall provide Another supervisory body with assistance.
Upon receiving the notification of any breach of security or loss of integrity from the Trust service providers, the Supervisory body shall inform other supervisory bodies and the public about breaches of security or loss of integrity.
The Supervisory body shall assess whether the breach of security or loss of integrity concerns two or more Member States.
If the breach of security or loss of integrity concerns two or more Member States, the Supervisory body shall notify the breach of security or loss of integrity to the supervisory bodies in other Member States concerned.
If the breach of security or loss of integrity concerns two or more Member States, the Supervisory body shall notify the breach of security or loss of integrity to ENISA.
The Supervisory body shall assess whether it determines that disclosure of the breach of security or loss of integrity is in the public interest.
If disclosure is in the public interest, the Supervisory body shall assess whether it informs the public directly.
If it informs the public directly, the Supervisory body shall inform the public.
If it does not inform the public directly, the Supervisory body shall send a requirement to inform the public to the Trust service providers.
By 31 March each year, the Supervisory body shall send a report on its previous calendar year’s main activities together with a summary of breach notifications received from trust service providers to the Commission.
Once a year, the Supervisory body shall send a summary of notifications of breach of security and loss of integrity received from trust service providers to ENISA.
The process for the Supervisory body ends with the supervision completed end event.
