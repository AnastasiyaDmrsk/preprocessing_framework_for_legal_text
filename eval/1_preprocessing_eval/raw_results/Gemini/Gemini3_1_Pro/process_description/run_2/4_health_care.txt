The process contains the following actors: the Commission, the Transferring Member State, and the Member State responsible.

The process starts with the need for the exchange of health data before a transfer is carried out, simultaneously triggering subprocesses for the Commission and the Transferring Member State.

The process for the Commission starts.
The Commission shall carry out the following 2 activities in parallel:
- The Commission shall draw up the common health certificate by means of implementing acts. The Commission shall adopt those implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure laid down in Article 44(2).
- The Commission shall adopt uniform conditions and practical arrangements for exchanging the information referred to in paragraph 1 by means of implementing acts. The Commission shall adopt those implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure laid down in Article 44(2).
All 2 activities shall be completed before the next step.
The process for the Commission ends with the implementing acts adopted end event.

The process for the Transferring Member State starts.
The Transferring Member State shall carry out the processing of personal health data only by a health professional who is subject to the obligation of professional secrecy or by another person subject to an equivalent obligation of professional secrecy.
The Transferring Member State shall assess whether the applicant is physically or legally incapable of giving his or her consent.
If the applicant is physically or legally incapable of giving his or her consent, the Transferring Member State shall assess whether the transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests of the applicant or of another person.
If the applicant is not physically or legally incapable of giving his or her consent, the Transferring Member State shall obtain the explicit consent of the applicant and/or of his or her representative.
The Transferring Member State shall ensure that the lack of consent, including a refusal to consent, does not constitute an obstacle to the transfer.
The Transferring Member State shall assess whether the explicit consent is obtained or whether the transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests.
If the explicit consent is obtained or the transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests, the Transferring Member State shall assess whether the information on any special needs of the person to be transferred is available to the competent authority in accordance with national law.
If the information is available, the Transferring Member State shall transmit information on any special needs of the person to be transferred, which in specific cases may include information on that person’s physical or mental health, to the Member State responsible for the sole purpose of the provision of medical care or treatment.
The Transferring Member State shall transfer that information in a common health certificate with the necessary documents attached.
The Transferring Member State shall exchange the information only between the health professionals or other persons referred to in paragraph 3.
The Transferring Member State shall use the information exchanged only for the purposes set out in paragraph 1.
The Transferring Member State shall not further process the information exchanged.
The Transferring Member State shall apply the rules laid down in Article 34(8) to (12) to the exchange of information.
The process for the Transferring Member State ends with the health data transmitted end event.

The process for the Member State responsible starts.
Upon receiving information on any special needs of the person to be transferred, the Member State responsible shall ensure that those special needs are adequately addressed.
The Member State responsible shall include any essential medical care that may be required when addressing those special needs.
The Member State responsible shall exchange the information only between the health professionals or other persons referred to in paragraph 3.
The Member State responsible shall use the information exchanged only for the purposes set out in paragraph 1.
The Member State responsible shall not further process the information exchanged.
The Member State responsible shall apply the rules laid down in Article 34(8) to (12) to the exchange of information.
The process for the Member State responsible ends with the special needs addressed end event.
