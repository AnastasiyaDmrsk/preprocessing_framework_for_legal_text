The process contains the following actors: Transferring Member State, Commission, Member State responsible, Applicant, Representative, Health professional, Other person.

The process starts with a need to exchange health data before a transfer is carried out.

The process for the Transferring Member State starts.
For the sole purpose of the provision of medical care or treatment, in particular concerning specific individuals, the transferring Member State shall, in so far as it is available to the competent authority in accordance with national law, transmit to the Member State responsible information on any special needs of the person to be transferred.
The transferring Member State shall transfer information on that person’s physical or mental health, which in specific cases may be included.
The transferring Member State shall transfer that information in a common health certificate with the necessary documents attached.
The transferring Member State shall only transmit the information to the Member State responsible after having obtained the explicit consent of the applicant and/or of his or her representative.
The transferring Member State shall only transmit the information to the Member State responsible when such transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests of the applicant or of another person, if the applicant is physically or legally incapable of giving his or her consent.
The transferring Member State shall not allow the lack of consent, including a refusal to consent, to constitute an obstacle to the transfer.
The transferring Member State shall exchange information only with the health professionals or other persons.
The transferring Member State shall ensure that the information exchanged shall only be used for the purposes set out in paragraph 1 and shall not be further processed.
The process for the Transferring Member State ends.

The process for the Commission starts.
The Commission shall draw up the common health certificate.
The Commission shall adopt uniform conditions and practical arrangements for exchanging the information.
The process for the Commission ends.

The process for the Member State responsible starts.
The Member State responsible shall ensure that those special needs are adequately addressed, including in particular any essential medical care that may be required.
The Member State responsible shall exchange information only with the health professionals or other persons.
The Member State responsible shall ensure that the information exchanged shall only be used for the purposes set out in paragraph 1 and shall not be further processed.
The process for the Member State responsible ends.

The process for the Applicant starts.
The Transferring Member State shall obtain the explicit consent of the applicant.
The Transferring Member State shall only transmit the information to the Member State responsible when transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests of the applicant or of another person, if the applicant is physically or legally incapable of giving his or her consent.
The lack of consent, including a refusal to consent, shall not constitute an obstacle to the transfer.
The process for the Applicant ends.

The process for the Representative starts.
The Transferring Member State shall obtain the explicit consent of the representative.
The Transferring Member State shall only transmit the information to the Member State responsible when transmission is necessary to protect the vital interests of the applicant or of another person, if the applicant is physically or legally incapable of giving his or her consent.
The lack of consent, including a refusal to consent, shall not constitute an obstacle to the transfer.
The process for the Representative ends.

The process for the Health professional starts.
The processing of personal health data shall only be carried out by a health professional who is subject, under national law or rules established by national competent bodies, to the obligation of professional secrecy.
The Health professional shall exchange information only with the health professionals or other persons.
The Health professional shall ensure that the information exchanged shall only be used for the purposes set out in paragraph 1 and shall not be further processed.
The process for the Health professional ends.

The process for the Other person starts.
The processing of personal health data shall only be carried out by another person subject to an equivalent obligation of professional secrecy.
The Other person shall exchange information only with the health professionals or other persons.
The Other person shall ensure that the information exchanged shall only be used for the purposes set out in paragraph 1 and shall not be further processed.
The process for the Other person ends.
