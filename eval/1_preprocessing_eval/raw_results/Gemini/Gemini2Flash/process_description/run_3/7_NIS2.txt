The process contains the following actors: Member States, Essential and important entities, CSIRT, Competent authority, Recipients of services, Single points of contact, Other affected Member States, ENISA, Cooperation Group, Commission, Trust service provider, Law enforcement authorities.

The process starts with the occurrence of an incident that has a significant impact on the provision of services.

The process for the Member States starts.
Each Member State shall ensure that essential and important entities notify its CSIRT or, where applicable, its competent authority in accordance with paragraph 4 of any incident that has a significant impact on the provision of their services as referred to in paragraph 3 (significant incident).
Each Member State shall ensure that those entities report any information enabling the CSIRT or the competent authority to determine any cross-border impact of the incident.
If the entities concerned notify the competent authority of a significant incident, the Member State shall ensure that that competent authority forwards the notification to the CSIRT upon receipt.
In the case of a cross-border or cross-sectoral significant incident, Member States shall ensure that their single points of contact are provided in due time with relevant information notified in accordance with paragraph 4.
Where applicable, Member States shall ensure that essential and important entities communicate, without undue delay, to the recipients of their services that are potentially affected by a significant cyber threat any measures or remedies that those recipients are able to take in response to that threat.
Member States shall ensure that, for the purpose of notification under paragraph 1, the entities concerned submit to the CSIRT or, where applicable, the competent authority: an early warning within 24 hours, an incident notification within 72 hours, an intermediate report (upon request), and a final report not later than one month after the submission of the incident notification.
In the event of an ongoing incident at the time of the submission of the final report, Member States shall ensure that entities concerned provide a progress report at that time and a final report within one month of their handling of the incident.
The process for the Member States ends.

The process for the Essential and important entities starts.
Essential and important entities shall notify its CSIRT or, where applicable, its competent authority of any incident that has a significant impact on the provision of their services as referred to in paragraph 3 (significant incident), in accordance with paragraph 4.
Where appropriate, essential and important entities shall notify the recipients of their services of significant incidents that are likely to adversely affect the provision of those services, without undue delay.
Essential and important entities shall report any information enabling the CSIRT or the competent authority to determine any cross-border impact of the incident.
Where applicable, essential and important entities shall communicate to the recipients of their services that are potentially affected by a significant cyber threat any measures or remedies that those recipients are able to take in response to that threat, without undue delay.
Where appropriate, essential and important entities shall also inform those recipients of the significant cyber threat itself.
For the purpose of notification, the entities concerned submit to the CSIRT or, where applicable, the competent authority: an early warning, which, where applicable, shall indicate whether the significant incident is suspected of being caused by unlawful or malicious acts or could have a cross-border impact, without undue delay and in any event within 24 hours of becoming aware of the significant incident.
For the purpose of notification, the entities concerned submit to the CSIRT or, where applicable, the competent authority an incident notification, which, where applicable, shall update the information referred to in point (a) and indicate an initial assessment of the significant incident, including its severity and impact, as well as, where available, the indicators of compromise, without undue delay and in any event within 72 hours of becoming aware of the significant incident.
Upon the request of a CSIRT or, where applicable, the competent authority, essential and important entities submit an intermediate report on relevant status updates.
Essential and important entities submit a final report not later than one month after the submission of the incident notification, including specific information.
In the event of an ongoing incident at the time of the submission of the final report, entities concerned provide a progress report at that time.
Essential and important entities provide a final report within one month of their handling of the incident in the event of an ongoing incident at the time of the submission of the final report.
The process for the Essential and important entities ends.

The process for the CSIRT starts.
The CSIRT shall provide a response to the notifying entity, including initial feedback on the significant incident and, upon request of the entity, guidance or operational advice on the implementation of possible mitigation measures, without undue delay and where possible within 24 hours of receiving the early warning.
If the CSIRT is not the initial recipient of the notification, the competent authority shall provide guidance in cooperation with the CSIRT.
The CSIRT shall provide additional technical support if the entity concerned so requests.
Where the significant incident is suspected to be of criminal nature, the CSIRT shall also provide guidance on reporting the significant incident to law enforcement authorities.
Where appropriate, and in particular where the significant incident concerns two or more Member States, the CSIRT shall inform the other affected Member States and ENISA of the significant incident, without undue delay.
The CSIRT shall include the type of information received in accordance with paragraph 4 when informing the other affected Member States and ENISA.
The CSIRT shall, in accordance with Union or national law, preserve the entity’s security and commercial interests as well as the confidentiality of the information provided.
Where public awareness is necessary to prevent a significant incident or to deal with an ongoing significant incident, or where disclosure of the significant incident is otherwise in the public interest, a Member State’s CSIRT may, after consulting the entity concerned, inform the public about the significant incident or require the entity to do so.
At the request of the CSIRT, the single point of contact shall forward notifications received pursuant to paragraph 1 to the single points of contact of other affected Member States.
The CSIRTs shall provide to the competent authorities under Directive (EU) 2022/2557 information about significant incidents, incidents, cyber threats and near misses notified in accordance with paragraph 1 of this Article and with Article 30 by entities identified as critical entities under Directive (EU) 2022/2557.
The process for the CSIRT ends.

The process for the Competent authority starts.
If the entities concerned notify the competent authority of a significant incident, the competent authority shall forward the notification to the CSIRT upon receipt.
If the CSIRT is not the initial recipient of the notification, the competent authority shall provide guidance in cooperation with the CSIRT.
Where the significant incident is suspected to be of criminal nature, the competent authority shall also provide guidance on reporting the significant incident to law enforcement authorities.
Where appropriate, and in particular where the significant incident concerns two or more Member States, the competent authority shall inform the other affected Member States and ENISA of the significant incident, without undue delay.
The competent authority shall include the type of information received in accordance with paragraph 4 when informing the other affected Member States and ENISA.
The competent authority shall, in accordance with Union or national law, preserve the entity’s security and commercial interests as well as the confidentiality of the information provided.
Where public awareness is necessary to prevent a significant incident or to deal with an ongoing significant incident, or where disclosure of the significant incident is otherwise in the public interest, a Member State’s competent authority may, after consulting the entity concerned, inform the public about the significant incident or require the entity to do so.
The competent authorities shall provide to the competent authorities under Directive (EU) 2022/2557 information about significant incidents, incidents, cyber threats and near misses notified in accordance with paragraph 1 of this Article and with Article 30 by entities identified as critical entities under Directive (EU) 2022/2557.
The process for the Competent authority ends.

The process for the Recipients of services starts.
Where applicable, essential and important entities shall communicate to the recipients of their services that are potentially affected by a significant cyber threat any measures or remedies that those recipients are able to take in response to that threat, without undue delay.
Where appropriate, essential and important entities shall also inform those recipients of the significant cyber threat itself.
The process for the Recipients of services ends.

The process for the Single points of contact starts.
In the case of a cross-border or cross-sectoral significant incident, Member States shall ensure that their single points of contact are provided in due time with relevant information notified in accordance with paragraph 4.
Where appropriate, and in particular where the significant incident concerns two or more Member States, the single point of contact shall inform the other affected Member States and ENISA of the significant incident, without undue delay.
The single point of contact shall include the type of information received in accordance with paragraph 4 when informing the other affected Member States and ENISA.
The single point of contact shall, in accordance with Union or national law, preserve the entity’s security and commercial interests as well as the confidentiality of the information provided.
At the request of the CSIRT or the competent authority, the single point of contact shall forward notifications received pursuant to paragraph 1 to the single points of contact of other affected Member States.
The single point of contact shall submit to ENISA every three months a summary report, including anonymised and aggregated data on significant incidents, incidents, cyber threats and near misses notified in accordance with paragraph 1 of this Article and with Article 30.
The process for the Single points of contact ends.

The process for the Other affected Member States starts.
Where appropriate, and in particular where the significant incident concerns two or more Member States, the other affected Member States shall be informed of the significant incident, without undue delay.
The other affected Member States shall be informed of the type of information received in accordance with paragraph 4.
The other affected Member States shall, in accordance with Union or national law, preserve the entity’s security and commercial interests as well as the confidentiality of the information provided.
The process for the Other affected Member States ends.

The process for the ENISA starts.
Where appropriate, and in particular where the significant incident concerns two or more Member States, ENISA shall be informed of the significant incident, without undue delay.
ENISA shall be informed of the type of information received in accordance with paragraph 4.
ENISA shall, in accordance with Union or national law, preserve the entity’s security and commercial interests as well as the confidentiality of the information provided.
ENISA may adopt technical guidance on the parameters of the information to be included in the summary report in order to contribute to the provision of comparable information.
ENISA shall inform the Cooperation Group and the CSIRTs network about its findings on notifications received every six months.
The process for the ENISA ends.

The process for the Cooperation Group starts.
ENISA shall inform the Cooperation Group and the CSIRTs network about its findings on notifications received every six months.
The Commission shall exchange advice and cooperate with the Cooperation Group on the draft implementing acts in accordance with Article 14(4), point (e).
The process for the Cooperation Group ends.

The process for the Commission starts.
The Commission may adopt implementing acts further specifying the type of information, the format and the procedure of a notification submitted pursuant to paragraph 1 of this Article and to Article 30 and of a communication submitted pursuant to paragraph 2 of this Article.
By 17 October 2024, the Commission shall, with regard to DNS service providers, TLD name registries, cloud computing service providers, data centre service providers, content delivery network providers, managed service providers, managed security service providers, as well as providers of online marketplaces, of online search engines and of social networking services platforms, adopt implementing acts further specifying the cases in which an incident shall be considered to be significant as referred to in paragraph 3.
The Commission may adopt such implementing acts with regard to other essential and important entities.
The Commission shall exchange advice and cooperate with the Cooperation Group on the draft implementing acts in accordance with Article 14(4), point (e).
The process for the Commission ends.

The process for the Trust service provider starts.
By way of derogation from the first subparagraph, point (b), a trust service provider shall notify the CSIRT or, where applicable, the competent authority, with regard to significant incidents that have an impact on the provision of its trust services, without undue delay and in any event within 24 hours of becoming aware of the significant incident.
The process for the Trust service provider ends.

The process for the Law enforcement authorities starts.
Where the significant incident is suspected to be of criminal nature, the CSIRT or the competent authority shall also provide guidance on reporting the significant incident to law enforcement authorities.
The process for the Law enforcement authorities ends.
