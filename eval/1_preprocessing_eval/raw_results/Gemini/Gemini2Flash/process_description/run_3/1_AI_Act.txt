The process contains the following actors: Member States, AI Office, SMEs.

The process starts with the need to support SMEs and promote AI regulation awareness.

The process for the Member States starts.
Member States shall provide SMEs, including start-ups, having a registered office or a branch in the Union, with priority access to the AI regulatory sandboxes, to the extent that they fulfil the eligibility conditions and selection criteria, except where other SMEs, including start-ups, other than those referred to in this paragraph from access to the AI regulatory sandbox, provided that they also fulfil the eligibility conditions and selection criteria.
Member States shall organise specific awareness raising and training activities on the application of this Regulation tailored to the needs of SMEs including start-ups, deployers and, as appropriate, local public authorities.
Member States shall utilise existing dedicated channels and where appropriate, establish new ones for communication with SMEs including start-ups, deployers, other innovators and, as appropriate, local public authorities to provide advice and respond to queries about the implementation of this Regulation, including as regards participation in AI regulatory sandboxes.
Member States shall facilitate the participation of SMEs and other relevant stakeholders in the standardisation development process.
The process for the Member States ends.

The process for the AI Office starts.
The AI Office shall provide standardised templates for areas covered by this Regulation, as specified by the Board in its request.
The AI Office shall develop and maintain a single information platform providing easy to use information in relation to this Regulation for all operators across the Union.
The AI Office shall organise appropriate communication campaigns to raise awareness about the obligations arising from this Regulation.
The AI Office shall evaluate and promote the convergence of best practices in public procurement procedures in relation to AI systems.
The process for the AI Office ends.

The process for the SMEs starts.
The specific interests and needs of the SME providers, including start-ups, shall be taken into account when setting the fees for conformity assessment under Article 43.
SMEs shall have those fees reduced proportionately to their size, market size and other relevant indicators.
The process for the SMEs ends.
