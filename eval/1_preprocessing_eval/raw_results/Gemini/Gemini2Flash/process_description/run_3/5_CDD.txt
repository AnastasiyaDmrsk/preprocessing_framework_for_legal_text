The process contains the following actors: Member States, Obliged entities, Credit institution, Financial institution, FIU, Legal professionals, Customers.

The process starts with the establishment of a business relationship or the carrying out of a transaction.

The process for the Member States starts.
Member States shall require that the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner be verified before establishing a business relationship or carrying out a transaction.
If it is necessary so as not to interrupt the normal conduct of business and where there is little risk of money laundering or terrorist financing, Member States may allow verification of the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner to be completed during the establishment of a business relationship.
In such situations where Member States allow verification of the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner to be completed during the establishment of a business relationship, those procedures shall be completed as soon as practicable after initial contact.
Member States may allow the opening of an account with a credit institution or financial institution, including accounts that permit transactions in transferable securities, provided that there are adequate safeguards in place to ensure that transactions are not carried out by the customer or on its behalf until full compliance with the customer due diligence requirements laid down in points (a) and (b) of the first subparagraph of Article 13(1) is obtained.
If an obliged entity is unable to comply with the customer due diligence requirements, Member States shall require that the obliged entity not carry out a transaction through a bank account, establish a business relationship, or carry out the transaction.
If an obliged entity is unable to comply with the customer due diligence requirements, Member States shall require that the obliged entity terminate the business relationship and consider making a suspicious transaction report to the FIU.
Member States shall not apply the above requirement to Legal professionals to the strict extent that those persons ascertain the legal position of their client, or perform the task of defending or representing that client in, or concerning, judicial proceedings.
Member States shall require that obliged entities apply the customer due diligence measures not only to all new customers but also at appropriate times to existing customers on a risk-sensitive basis, including when the relevant circumstances of a customer change.
The process for the Member States ends.

The process for the Obliged entities starts.
Obliged entities shall verify the identity of the customer and the beneficial owner before establishing a business relationship or carrying out a transaction.
If it is necessary so as not to interrupt the normal conduct of business and where there is little risk of money laundering or terrorist financing, obliged entities may complete verification of identity during the establishment of a business relationship.
In such situations, obliged entities shall complete those procedures as soon as practicable after initial contact.
Obliged entities shall apply the customer due diligence measures to all new customers and at appropriate times to existing customers on a risk-sensitive basis, including when the relevant circumstances of a customer change.
If an obliged entity is unable to comply with the customer due diligence requirements, it shall not carry out a transaction through a bank account, establish a business relationship, or carry out the transaction.
If an obliged entity is unable to comply with the customer due diligence requirements, it shall terminate the business relationship and consider making a suspicious transaction report to the FIU in relation to the customer in accordance with Article 33.
The process for the Obliged entities ends.

The process for the Credit institution starts.
A credit institution may open an account, including accounts that permit transactions in transferable securities, provided that there are adequate safeguards in place to ensure that transactions are not carried out by the customer or on its behalf until full compliance with the customer due diligence requirements laid down in points (a) and (b) of the first subparagraph of Article 13(1) is obtained.
The process for the Credit institution ends.

The process for the Financial institution starts.
A financial institution may open an account, including accounts that permit transactions in transferable securities, provided that there are adequate safeguards in place to ensure that transactions are not carried out by the customer or on its behalf until full compliance with the customer due diligence requirements laid down in points (a) and (b) of the first subparagraph of Article 13(1) is obtained.
The process for the Financial institution ends.

The process for the FIU starts.
Obliged entities may consider making a suspicious transaction report to the FIU in relation to the customer in accordance with Article 33 if the obliged entity is unable to comply with the customer due diligence requirements.
The process for the FIU ends.

The process for the Legal professionals starts.
Legal professionals ascertain the legal position of their client, or perform the task of defending or representing that client in, or concerning, judicial proceedings, including providing advice on instituting or avoiding such proceedings.
The process for the Legal professionals ends.

The process for the Customers starts.
Customers shall have their identity and that of their beneficial owner verified.
The process for the Customers ends.
