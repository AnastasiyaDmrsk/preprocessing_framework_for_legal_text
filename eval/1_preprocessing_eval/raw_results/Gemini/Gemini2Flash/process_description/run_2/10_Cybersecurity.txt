The process contains the following actors: Commission, ENISA (with lane: Management Board), ECCG, Stakeholders.

The process starts with a potential need for a new or revised cybersecurity certification scheme.

The process for the Commission starts.
The Commission may request ENISA to prepare a candidate scheme or to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme on the basis of the Union rolling work programme.
In duly justified cases, the Commission may request ENISA to prepare a candidate scheme or to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme which is not included in the Union rolling work programme.
The Commission may adopt implementing acts providing for a European cybersecurity certification scheme for ICT products, ICT services and ICT processes which meets the requirements set out in Articles 51, 52 and 54, based on the candidate scheme prepared by ENISA.
The Commission may request ENISA to start the process of developing a revised candidate scheme in accordance with Article 48 and this Article, if necessary.
The process for the Commission ends.

The process for ENISA starts.
Following a request from the Commission pursuant to Article 48, ENISA shall prepare a candidate scheme which meets the requirements set out in Articles 51, 52 and 54.
Following a request from the ECCG pursuant to Article 48(2), ENISA may prepare a candidate scheme which meets the requirements set out in Articles 51, 52 and 54.
ENISA shall consult all relevant stakeholders by means of a formal, open, transparent and inclusive consultation process when preparing a candidate scheme.
For each candidate scheme, ENISA shall establish an ad hoc working group in accordance with Article 20(4) for the purpose of providing ENISA with specific advice and expertise.
ENISA shall closely cooperate with the ECCG.
ENISA shall take utmost account of the opinion of the ECCG before transmitting the candidate scheme prepared in accordance with paragraphs 3, 4 and 5 to the Commission.
ENISA shall transmit the candidate scheme to the Commission.
At least every five years, ENISA shall evaluate each adopted European cybersecurity certification scheme, taking into account the feedback received from interested parties.
The process for ENISA ends.

The process for the Management Board (lane within ENISA) starts.
The Management Board shall take any decision to refuse a request from the ECCG.
The process for the Management Board ends.

The process for the ECCG starts.
In duly justified cases, the ECCG may request ENISA to prepare a candidate scheme or to review an existing European cybersecurity certification scheme which is not included in the Union rolling work programme.
The ECCG shall provide ENISA with assistance and expert advice in relation to the preparation of the candidate scheme and shall adopt an opinion on the candidate scheme.
The ECCG may request ENISA to start the process of developing a revised candidate scheme in accordance with Article 48 and this Article, if necessary.
The process for the ECCG ends.

The process for the Stakeholders starts.
Stakeholders shall be consulted by ENISA by means of a formal, open, transparent and inclusive consultation process when ENISA prepares a candidate scheme.
Stakeholders shall provide feedback to ENISA for the evaluation of each adopted European cybersecurity certification scheme.
The process for the Stakeholders ends.
