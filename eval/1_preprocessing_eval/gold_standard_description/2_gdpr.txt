The process includes the following actors: Controller, Processor, Supervisory Authority, and Data Subject.

The process starts with Processor becoming aware of a personal data breach. The Processor shall notify Controller without undue delay upon becoming aware of a personal data breach. The process for the Processor ends with Controller Notified end event.

Upon becoming aware of the breach, the process starts and Controller shall determine the risks for natural persons. If a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons is not likely, the process ends with the Process Ends end event. If a risk is likely, the Controller shall proceed in parallel with four activities:
-	The Controller shall assess whether notification to the Supervisory Authority is possible within 72 hours. If notification is possible within 72 hours, the Controller shall notify the Supervisory Authority. If notification within 72 hours is not possible, the Controller shall prepare reasons for the delay and shall subsequently notify the Supervisory Authority. If all required information was provided initially, the notification is complete. If not all information was provided initially, the Controller shall gather the remaining information and then the Controller may provide subsequent information to the Supervisory Authority.
-	The Controller shall assess whether appropriate technical and organisational protection measures have been implemented. If such measures have been implemented, the Controller shall further assess whether measures have been taken so that the high risk is no longer likely to materialise. If the high risk is no longer likely to materialise, the Controller shall assess whether communication to the Data Subject would involve a disproportionate effort. If communication would involve disproportionate effort, the communication obligation is discharged without direct contact, except where a request is subsequently received from the Supervisory Authority. If the high risk is still likely to materialise, OR if no appropriate protection measures have been implemented, OR if communication would not involve disproportionate effort, the Controller shall communicate the breach to the Data Subject.
-	The Controller shall document the breach. The Supervisory Authority shall review the breach notification and documentation upon receipt. If the breach is not likely to result in a high risk to the Data Subject, the process for the Supervisory Authority ends with SA Review Complete end event. If the breach is likely to result in a high risk, the Supervisory Authority shall require the Controller to communicate the breach to the Data Subject. The Supervisory Authority transmits this instruction to the Controller via a message flow.
-	The Controller shall await a potential Data Subject Communication Request from the Supervisory Authority. The Data Subject shall be informed of the breach upon receiving the breach communication from the Controller. The process for the Data Subject ends with Data Subject Informed.

All four parallel branches shall be completed before the process continues to the final end event.

Finally, the process ends for the Controller with the End event once all parallel branches have been completed.
