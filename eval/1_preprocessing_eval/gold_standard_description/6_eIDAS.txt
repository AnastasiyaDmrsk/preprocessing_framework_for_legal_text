The process contains the following actors: Member State, Supervisory Body, Conformity Assessment Body, Another Supervisory Body, Trust Service Provider, and Commission.

The process starts with the Member State and Trust Service Provider subprocesses.

The Member State shall designate a supervisory body and then give it the necessary powers and adequate resources. After that, Member State shall notify the names and addresses of the designated supervisory body to the Commission. Following, Member State may require the supervisory body to establish, maintain, and update a trust infrastructure if a trust infrastructure is required. If a joint investigation is required, the following two activities shall occur in parallel:
-	The Member State may authorise the supervisory bodies to carry out joint investigations if needed.
-	The Member State shall agree upon and establish the necessary arrangements and procedures.
Both parallel activities shall be completed before the Member State receives the final reports and the process ends.

Upon receiving the designation and the necessary powers and resources from the Member State, the Supervisory Body shall carry out the following nine activities in parallel:
-	The Supervisory Body shall supervise qualified trust service providers and shall assess whether requirements are met. If requirements are not met, the Supervisory Body shall require the trust service providers to remedy any failure, except where compliance is confirmed.
-	The Supervisory Body shall assess whether non-qualified trust service providers meet requirements. If requirements are not met, the Supervisory Body shall take actions and require the provider to remedy any failure, except where the provider is already compliant.
-	The Supervisory Body shall either request a conformity assessment from the Conformity Assessment Body, and receive the report OR the Supervisory Body shall carry out audits, then cooperate with the data protection authorities, and then inform the data protection authorities about the results of audits. Lastly, after either of the activities is over, the Supervisory Body shall analyse the conformity assessment reports.
-	The Supervisory Body shall receive breach notifications from the Trust Service Provider and shall inform other supervisory bodies and the public about breaches. If the breach concerns two or more Member States, the Supervisory Body shall inform the supervisory bodies in the other Member States concerned and ENISA. If the breach is in the public interest, the Supervisory Body shall either inform the public directly or shall require the Trust Service Provider to inform the public, except where no public interest is established.
-	The Supervisory Body shall verify the existence and application of termination plans.
-	The Supervisory Body, after receiving a request to establish a trust infrastructure, and shall establish, maintain, and update a trust infrastructure accordingly.
-	The Supervisory Body shall cooperate with other supervisory bodies and shall handle assistance requests from Another Supervisory Body. If the conditions for assistance are fulfilled, the Supervisory Body shall provide assistance, except where conditions are not met, in which case the Supervisory Body shall refuse assistance. If authorised by the Member State, the Supervisory Body shall carry out joint investigations.
-	The Supervisory Body shall either grant OR withdraw qualified status to trust service providers and shall then inform the body responsible for the national trusted list accordingly.
-	By 31 March each year, the Supervisory Body shall submit a report with a summary of breach notifications and shall then provide ENISA with that summary.
All nine parallel activities shall be completed before the Supervisory Body's process ends.

The Conformity Assessment Body shall perform a conformity assessment upon receiving the request from the Supervisory Body. The process for the Conformity Assessment Body ends with Assessment Complete end event.

The Another Supervisory Body shall request assistance from the Supervisory Body when required. Upon receiving a response and authorisation, the Another Supervisory Body shall carry out joint investigations, except where assistance is refused. The process ends with Assistance Complete end event.

Right after starting event, the Trust Service Provider shall take technical and organisational measures to manage risks posed to the trust services it provides. The Trust Service Provider shall take measures to prevent and minimise the impact of security incidents and shall inform stakeholders of the effects of any security incidents. If the incident is significant, the Trust Service Provider shall notify the supervisory body and other relevant bodies. If the incident is likely to adversely affect a natural or legal person, the Trust Service Provider shall notify that natural or legal person accordingly, except where no adverse effect is identified. If requested by the Supervisory Body, the Trust Service Provider shall inform the public about the breach of security or loss of integrity.

The Commission shall receive the names and addresses of the designated supervisory bodies from the Member State and shall receive the annual report and summary from the Supervisory Body. The Commission shall make the annual report available to Member States. If the formats and procedures for reporting need to be defined, the Commission may define the report's format and procedures, except where such formats are already established. If specific measures need to be further specified, the Commission may further specify those measures, except where no specification is required. The Commission shall adopt implementing acts in accordance with the examination procedure.

Finally, the process ends for all actors upon completion of their respective obligations: the Member State upon receiving all required reports, the Supervisory Body upon fulfilling all nine parallel supervisory duties, the Trust Service Provider upon completing all incident management activities, and the Commission upon adopting the necessary implementing acts.
