The process contains the following actors: Manufacturer (with two lanes: Manufacturer and Quality Management System), Natural / Legal Person, and Member State (lane: Competent Authority).

The process starts with a new device being placed on the market, simultaneously triggering the Manufacturer's compliance obligations and the Member State's market surveillance check subprocesses.

Right from the process start, the Manufacturer shall carry out the following nine activities in parallel:
-	If the device is a custom-made device, the Manufacturer shall draw up documentation in accordance with Section 2 of Annex XIII and shall then keep the documentation up to date and then, the Manufacturer shall keep documentation available to competent authorities. If the device is not custom-made, the Manufacturer shall draw up technical documentation and shall keep it up to date. If the device is not an investigation device, the Manufacturer shall draw up an EU declaration of conformity and shall then affix the CE marking of conformity. Afterwards, if the device is an implantable device, the Manufacturer shall keep all documentation available for 15 years, except where the device is not implantable, in which case the Manufacturer shall keep all documentation available for 10 years. If the manufacturer has a registered place of business outside the Union, the Manufacturer shall ensure that the authorised representative holds the required documentation. If the device is designed or manufactured by another legal or natural person, the Manufacturer shall ensure that information on the identity of that person is part of the device information. Upon receiving request of technical documentation from the Member State, the Manufacturer shall provide technical documentation. Then, if all information and documentation is requested by the Member State, the Manufacturer shall provide all information and documentation. If the Member State requests samples, the Manufacturer shall provide samples or access to the device. If the Member State requests collaboration, the Manufacturer shall cooperate with the Member State. If the Manufacturer receives any claims from the Natural / Legal Person, the Manufacturer shall have measures to provide sufficient financial coverage.
-	The Manufacturer shall establish a system for risk management. Then, the Manufacturer shall document a system for risk management. Then. The Manufacturer shall implement a system for risk management. Then, the Manufacturer shall maintain a system for risk management.
-	The Manufacturer shall ensure that devices are compliant with the requirements of the Regulation. If the device is in conformity, the process continues. If the device is not in conformity, the Manufacturer shall either bring the device into conformity, OR shall withdraw OR shall recall the device as appropriate. Manufacturer shall inform distributors, the authorised representative, and importers of any non-conformity, and then if device presents a serious risk, the Manufacturer shall inform the competent authorities and the notified body, except where no serious risk exists. Lastly, the Manufacturer shall have a system for recording and reporting of incidents independent of conformity.
-	If the device is not an investigation device, the Manufacturer shall establish a quality management system, then document a quality management system, then implement a quality management system, then maintain a quality management system, then update a quality management system, and then improve a quality management system. The Quality Management System shall cover all parts of dealing with the quality of processes, shall then govern the structure, responsibilities, procedures, and processes, and shall then address at least the aspects set out in paragraph 9.
-	The Manufacturer shall implement the post-market surveillance system and then keep up to date the post-market surveillance system.
-	The Manufacturer shall ensure that procedures are in place and shall then take into account any changes in device design or characteristics.
-	The Manufacturer shall ensure that the device is accompanied by the required information.
All nine parallel obligations shall be completed before the Manufacturer's process ends.

The Member State process starts. If documentation is requested, the Member State shall request it from the Manufacturer and shall receive it. If all information and documentation are needed, the Member State shall request all information and documentation from the Manufacturer and shall receive it. If samples or access to the device are needed, the Member State shall require the Manufacturer to provide samples or grant access, except where samples are not needed. If collaboration is required, the Member State shall request collaboration with the Manufacturer, except where no collaboration is needed. If cooperation has failed or documentation remains incomplete, the Member State shall take all appropriate measures to prohibit or restrict the device, OR the Member State shall withdraw OR the Member State shall recall the device. If the device has caused damage, the Member State shall facilitate the provision of information and documentation to the Natural / Legal Person. The process for the Member State ends.

Upon receiving information about the device from the Member State, the Natural / Legal Person shall assess whether to claim compensation for damage. If compensation is claimed, the Natural / Legal Person shall submit a claim to the Manufacturer and shall receive compensation upon resolution, except where no claim is made. The process for the Natural / Legal Person ends with Compensation Received end event.

Finally, the process ends for the Manufacturer upon completion of all nine parallel compliance obligations, for the Member State upon completion of market surveillance and damage facilitation duties, and for the Natural / Legal Person upon receiving compensation or choosing not to claim.
